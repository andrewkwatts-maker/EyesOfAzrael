# Quick Reference: Enhanced Mesoamerican Deities

## AZTEC DEITIES

### 1. Huitzilopochtli - "Hummingbird of the South"
**Role**: War god, solar deity, patron of Aztec state
**Key Symbol**: Eagle/Sun
**Calendar**: 1 Flint (Ce Tecpatl)
**Festival**: Toxcatl (May)
**Sacrifice Type**: Arrow sacrifice (Cuauhtequitl)
**Key Site**: Templo Mayor (South shrine)
**Primary Source**: Florentine Codex Book 1-2
**Historical Note**: Guided Mexica migration; justified imperial expansion

### 2. Tezcatlipoca - "Smoking Mirror"
**Role**: Supreme deity, night, sorcery, fate
**Key Symbol**: Obsidian mirror
**Calendar**: 1 Ocelotl (jaguar)
**Festival**: Ochpaniztli (purification)
**Sacrifice Type**: Heart extraction
**Key Site**: Templo Mayor (House of Darkness)
**Primary Source**: Codex Borgia, Florentine Codex Book 6
**Historical Note**: Represented abstract cosmic principles

### 3. Quetzalcoatl - "Feathered Serpent"
**Role**: Wind god, learning, civilization
**Key Symbol**: Feathered serpent
**Calendar**: 9 Wind (Chiconahui Ehecatl)
**Festival**: Agricultural calendar
**Historical Range**: 500 BCE - 1521 CE (Olmec → Aztec)
**Key Site**: Tula (Tollan, Toltec)
**Primary Source**: Florentine Codex Book 1, Anales de Cuauhtitlán
**Historical Note**: Oldest Mesoamerican deity; predates Aztecs by 1000+ years

### 4. Tlaloc - "He Who Makes Things Sprout"
**Role**: Rain god, fertility, agriculture
**Key Symbol**: Goggle eyes
**Calendar**: 3 Water (Exin Atl)
**Festival**: Atemoztli (November)
**Sacrifice Type**: Child sacrifice, drowning
**Key Site**: Templo Mayor (North shrine)
**Primary Source**: Florentine Codex Book 2, 11
**Historical Note**: Continuous 3000+ year tradition (Olmec → Aztec)

### 5. Coatlicue - "Serpent Skirt"
**Role**: Earth mother, creation/destruction duality
**Key Symbol**: Serpent skirt, necklace of hearts
**Calendar**: 1 Serpent (Ce Coatl)
**Key Symbol**: Mother of Huitzilopochtli
**Key Site**: Templo Mayor
**Famous Artifact**: Coatlicue Statue (8.5 ft monolith, discovered 1790)
**Significance**: Buried when Spaniards arrived - indicates religious priority

## MAYA DEITIES

### 1. Itzamna - "Iguana House"
**Role**: Supreme creator, lord of heavens, patron of writing/calendar
**Key Symbol**: Caiman/old wise man
**Calendar**: Ahau (Lord day)
**Key Achievement**: Invented 260-day and 365-day calendars
**Festival**: Scribal devotion, calendar rituals
**Key Site**: Izamal (pilgrimage)
**Primary Source**: Dresden Codex, Popol Vuh
**Consort**: Ixchel
**Historical Note**: Central to Maya writing tradition

### 2. Chaac - "Thunder"
**Role**: Rain god, agriculture, water
**Key Symbol**: Goggle eyes, long nose
**Calendar**: Muluc (Water day)
**Key Domain**: Cenotes (sacred wells)
**Festival**: Drought petition rituals
**Sacrifice Type**: Drowning in cenotes (ch'ac mol offerings)
**Key Site**: Sacred Cenote, Chichén Itza
**Primary Source**: Paris Codex, Madrid Codex
**Archaeological Evidence**: Human remains, jade, pottery from cenote excavations

### 3. Ixchel - "Lady Rainbow"
**Role**: Moon goddess, fertility, childbirth, weaving
**Key Symbol**: Moon, weaving loom
**Calendar**: Ix (feminine month), lunar cycles
**Sacred Practice**: Women's pilgrimage
**Key Domain**: Textiles and weaving arts
**Key Site**: Cozumel Island shrine
**Primary Source**: Dresden Codex, Popol Vuh
**Modern Connection**: Maya women still use backstrap loom unchanged for millennia
**Consort**: Itzamna

### 4. Ah Puch - "The Destroyer"
**Role**: Death god, underworld, decay
**Key Symbol**: Skeletal figure
**Calendar**: Cimi (Death day)
**Domain**: Xibalba (13-level underworld)
**Mythological Significance**: Defeated by Hero Twins (death-resurrection cycle)
**Key Text**: Popol Vuh (Xibalba section)
**Theological Role**: Death as transformation, not final ending
**Cultural Significance**: Part of cyclical cosmology

### 5. Kukulkan - "Feathered Serpent"
**Role**: Civilization bringer, wind, knowledge
**Key Symbol**: Feathered serpent
**Calendar**: Wind (Ik') day
**Astronomical Phenomenon**: Equinox shadow descent at El Castillo
**Historical Range**: 1500 BCE - 1521 CE (Olmec → Toltec → Maya)
**Key Site**: El Castillo, Chichén Itza (pyramid)
**Languages**: K'uk'ulkan (Yucatec), Q'uq'umatz (K'iche')
**Cultural Transmission**: Shows Mesoamerican religious continuity

## CALENDAR SYSTEMS

### Aztec Tonalpohualli (Count of Days)
- 260-day sacred calendar
- 20 day signs × 13 numbers
- Determined auspicious dates for rituals
- 52-year cycle (full round)
- New Fire Ceremony at cycle completion

### Maya Tzolk'in (Count of Days)
- Identical 260-day cycle (shared Mesoamerican system)
- Day names differ but pattern same
- Connected to 9 lunar months (human gestation)
- Also uses 52-year calendar round

## SACRIFICE SYSTEMS

### Aztec Sacrificial Calendar

| Festival | Month | Deity | Victim | Method |
|----------|-------|-------|--------|--------|
| Toxcatl | May | Huitzilopochtli | Young warrior | Arrow sacrifice |
| Atemoztli | November | Tlaloc | Children | Drowning/heart extraction |
| Ochpaniztli | August-Sept | Tezcatlipoca | Female captive | Heart extraction |
| Panquetzaliztli | November | Huitzilopochtli | Warriors | Heart extraction |

### Maya Sacrificial Practices
- **Cenote Sacrifice** (Chaac): Drowning in sacred wells, drought petitions
- **Bloodletting** (Royal rituals): Self-sacrifice by nobility, calendar observances
- **Occasional practices**: Tied to crisis events (drought) rather than regular schedule

## HISTORICAL TIMELINES

### Quetzalcoatl/Kukulkan Continuity (Most Ancient Deity)
```
Olmec (1500-400 BCE): Serpent symbolism
    ↓
Teotihuacan (200-750 CE): Feathered serpent temple
    ↓
Toltec (900-1200 CE): Quetzalcoatl at Tula
    ↓
Aztec (1325-1521 CE): Quetzalcoatl in pantheon
    ↓
Maya (Parallel): Kukulkan at Chichén Itza
```

### Aztec State Formation
```
Aztlan (legendary): Huitzilopochtli guides migration (12th-13th c)
    ↓
Tenochtitlan (1325): Eagle on cactus sign indicates city location
    ↓
Aztec Triple Alliance (1428): State expansion begins
    ↓
Imperial Period (1428-1521): Continuous military expansion, human sacrifice escalates
```

### Maya Civilization Periods
```
Preclassic (1500-250 BCE): Deity concepts emerge
    ↓
Classic Maya (250-900 CE): Height of civilization, most advanced knowledge
    ↓
Post-Classic (900-1521 CE): City-state period, Chichén Itza prominence
    ↓
Spanish Conquest (1519-1600s): Cultural transformation and syncretism
```

## COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS POINTS

### What Both Cultures Share
1. 260-day sacred calendar (identical cycle)
2. Feathered serpent deity (Quetzalcoatl/Kukulkan)
3. Rain god concepts (Tlaloc/Chaac)
4. Cyclical time philosophy
5. Divine mandate for rulership
6. Blood as sacred life force

### What Differs
1. **Sacrifice scale**: Aztec systematic, Maya occasional
2. **Divine authority**: Aztec imperial, Maya royal
3. **Underworld mythology**: Maya elaborate (Popol Vuh), Aztec less detailed
4. **Written records**: Aztec colonial, Maya surviving codices
5. **Historical timeline**: Classic Maya ends 900 CE; Aztec rises 1325 CE

### Historical Continuity
- Shared Olmec and Toltec heritage
- Maya civilization predates and parallels Aztec
- Quetzalcoatl/Kukulkan shows continuous tradition over 2000 years
- Calendar systems identical, suggesting ancient common source
