Welcome to the Eyes of Azrael community! We're excited that you want to contribute your knowledge, research, and theories to our collaborative mythology database.

## Quick Start

1. **Visit the submission form:** [/theories/user-submissions/submit.html](../theories/user-submissions/submit.html)

## Writing Your Submission

### **Short Description (Required)**
**Goal:** Give readers a 1-sentence "elevator pitch"
**Length:** Max 200 characters (about 30-40 words)
**Format:** Plain text, no Markdown

**Good Example:**
> "Greek god of the sky, thunder, and king of the Olympian gods. Father of many heroes and wielder of the lightning bolt."

**Bad Example:**
> "Zeus is really interesting and important and there's a lot to say about him..."

---

### **Full Description (Required)**
**Goal:** Comprehensive, well-researched content
**Length:** As long as needed (typically 300-2000 words)
**Format:** Markdown supported

**Structure Suggestion:**
1. **Introduction** - Who/what is this entity?
2. **Mythology** - Key myths, stories, role in tradition
3. **Symbolism** - What does it represent? Deeper meanings?
4. **Cultural Context** - Historical period, geographical region, social role
5. **Modern Interpretation** - How is it understood today?
6. **Cross-Cultural Parallels** - Similar concepts in other traditions

**Markdown Tips:**
```markdown
**Bold text** for emphasis
*Italic text* for foreign words
- Bullet points for lists
> Quotes for primary sources
[Link text](URL) for references
```

## Submitting Theories

### **What Makes a Good Theory Submission?**

1. **Clear Hypothesis**
   - State your main claim explicitly
   - Explain what you're proposing

2. **Evidence & Analysis**
   - Provide specific examples
   - Show patterns or correlations
   - Cite sources

3. **Intellectual Honesty**
   - Acknowledge weaknesses
   - Mention alternative explanations
   - Admit when you're speculating

4. **Structured Data**
   - Use the theory-specific fields
   - Provide confidence ratings
   - List testable predictions if possible

---

### **Confidence Score (0-100)**

Rate your own theory honestly:

- **90-100:** Proven fact, experimentally verified
  - Example: "The I Ching has exactly 64 hexagrams" (factual observation)

- **70-89:** Strong correlation, statistically significant
  - Example: "72 appears cross-culturally in sacred numbers" (well-documented pattern)

- **50-69:** Moderate correlation, plausible hypothesis
  - Example: "The 10 Sefirot may parallel 10-dimensional M-theory" (structural similarity)

- **30-49:** Weak correlation, interesting pattern
  - Example: "12 gates in Revelation may encode 12 spatial dimensions" (numerology)

- **10-29:** Highly speculative, creative interpretation
  - Example: "Egyptian deity names phonetically match element symbols" (linguistic coincidence likely)

- **0-9:** Pure speculation, thought experiment
  - Example: "Ancient aliens taught physics to mystics" (unfalsifiable claim)

---

### **Intellectual Honesty Warnings**

**Always include:**
1. **Most likely alternative explanation**
   - What's the simplest interpretation?
   - Could this be coincidence?

2. **Cognitive biases to watch for**
   - Confirmation bias (highlighting matches, ignoring mismatches)
   - Apophenia (seeing patterns in random data)
   - Retrofitting (forcing modern concepts onto ancient texts)

3. **Methodological limitations**
   - What can't you test?
   - What assumptions are you making?
   - What's the quality of your evidence?

**Example:**
> "Most likely explanation: The number 12 is symbolically ubiquitous in ancient cultures (12 months, 12 zodiac signs, 12 tribes). Retrofitting modern physics (12 dimensions in F-theory) onto this is confirmation bias. Treat as creative thought experiment, not literal encoding of physics knowledge."

## Sources & Citations

### **How to Cite Sources:**

**Primary Sources** (ancient texts):
```
Title: The Iliad
Author: Homer
Date: c. 8th century BCE
Type: Primary
Relevance: Comprehensive
```

**Secondary Sources** (modern scholarship):
```
Title: The Greek Myths
Author: Robert Graves
Date: 1955
Type: Secondary
Relevance: Significant
URL: [if available]
```

**Archaeological Evidence:**
```
Title: Temple of Zeus excavation, Olympia
Date: Discovered 1829
Type: Archaeological
Relevance: Minor
```

### **Citation Quality:**
- **Primary sources** (ancient texts, inscriptions) are best
- **Academic secondary sources** (peer-reviewed papers, scholarly books) are valuable
- **Popular sources** (Wikipedia, blogs) can be used but note their limitations
- **Personal gnosis/UPG** (unverified personal gnosis) should be clearly labeled as such
