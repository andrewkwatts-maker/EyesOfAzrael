ü™ê The Planetary Hypothesis (Zecharia Sitchin)

Proponent: Zecharia Sitchin (1920-2010)

Key Works: The 12th Planet (1976), The Cosmic Code

Core Claims:

  • Tiamat = Planet: The goddess Tiamat represents a water planet that once existed between Mars and Jupiter
  • Marduk = Nibiru: The god Marduk represents a rogue planet (Nibiru) that entered our solar system
  • The Collision: Nibiru's moons struck Tiamat, creating Earth from one half and the asteroid belt from the other
  • Mars Connection: Mars (Lahmu) may have orbited Tiamat as a moon before the catastrophe
  • Our Moon: Earth's Moon was Kingu, Tiamat's satellite, captured in the collision
  • Origin of Life: Life may have originated on the water world Tiamat and transferred to Earth through the debris

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Academic Perspective

These alternative theories are not accepted by mainstream scholarship. Professional Assyriologists, astronomers, and historians have raised significant objections:

  • Translation Issues: Sitchin's translations of Sumerian/Akkadian have been disputed by professional linguists
  • Anachronistic Reading: Critics argue these theories project modern astronomical concepts onto texts intended as theology/literature
  • Accretion Model: Mainstream astronomy holds the asteroid belt never formed into a planet due to Jupiter's gravitational influence
  • Mythological Function: The Enuma Elish can be understood as political propaganda legitimizing Babylonian supremacy
  • No Physical Evidence: No archaeological or geological evidence definitively supports these interpretations

These theories are presented for intellectual exploration, not as established fact. They represent alternative frameworks for examining mythology alongside traditional scholarly, psychological, and comparative approaches.
