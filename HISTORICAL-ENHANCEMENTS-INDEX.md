## By Deity

### Hindu Deities

#### Brahma (Creator)
- **Summary**: HISTORICAL-ENHANCEMENT-SUMMARY.md - "Brahma: Declining Worship Pattern"
- **Analysis**: HISTORICAL-ANALYSIS-HINDU-BUDDHIST.md - Part 3.1
- **Texts**: Rigveda 10.121, Brahmanas, Brahma Purana
- **Sectarian**: Non-sectarian, Smarta tradition
- **Status**: Minimal worship despite conceptual importance

#### Shiva (Destroyer-Regenerator)
- **Summary**: HISTORICAL-ENHANCEMENT-SUMMARY.md - "Shiva: Ascendant Shaiva Tradition"
- **Analysis**: HISTORICAL-ANALYSIS-HINDU-BUDDHIST.md - Part 3.2
- **Texts**: Rigveda 2.33, Svetasvatara Upanishad, Shiva Purana, Tantric texts
- **Sectarian**: Shaivism (multiple schools), Tantric traditions
- **Regional**: South India, Kashmir, Nepal (Pashupatinath)
- **Peak**: Puranic period onwards, especially medieval period

#### Vishnu (Sustainer)
- **Summary**: HISTORICAL-ENHANCEMENT-SUMMARY.md - "Vishnu: Dominant Vaishnavism"
- **Analysis**: HISTORICAL-ANALYSIS-HINDU-BUDDHIST.md - Part 3.3
- **Texts**: Bhagavad Gita, Vishnu Purana, Bhagavata Purana, Ramayana
- **Sectarian**: Vaishnavism (Sri Vaishnava, Dvaita, Gaudiya, Vallabha)
- **Regional**: Pan-Indian with regional schools
- **Peak**: Puranic period, major modern tradition

#### Krishna (Divine Teacher & Cowherd)
- **Summary**: HISTORICAL-ENHANCEMENT-SUMMARY.md - "Krishna: Hero to Cosmic Deity"
- **Analysis**: HISTORICAL-ANALYSIS-HINDU-BUDDHIST.md - Part 3.4
- **Texts**: Bhagavad Gita, Bhagavata Purana, Harivamsa, Gita Govinda
- **Sectarian**: Gaudiya Vaishnavism, Vallabha tradition
- **Regional**: North India (Mathura/Vrindavan), East India (Bengal)
- **Peak**: Bhakti movements (12th century onwards), modern ISKCON

#### Ganesha (Obstacle Remover)
- **Enhancement**: Added to script but limited detailed documentation
- **Status**: Beloved subsidiary deity
- **Worship**: Pan-Hindu, all traditions honor Ganesha
- **Significance**: Worshipped first in ceremonies

### Buddhist Deities

#### Buddha (Historical & Cosmic)
- **Summary**: HISTORICAL-ENHANCEMENT-SUMMARY.md - "Buddha: Teacher to Cosmic Being"
- **Analysis**: HISTORICAL-ANALYSIS-HINDU-BUDDHIST.md - Part 3.5
- **Historical**: 6th-5th century BCE, Lumbini (Nepal)
- **Texts**: Pali Canon (Sutta Pitaka), Mahayana sutras, Buddhist sutras
- **Transformation**: From exemplary human to cosmic principle
- **Status**: Universal in all Buddhist traditions

#### Avalokiteshvara (Compassion Bodhisattva)
- **Summary**: HISTORICAL-ENHANCEMENT-SUMMARY.md - "Avalokiteshvara: Bodhisattva Transformation"
- **Analysis**: HISTORICAL-ANALYSIS-HINDU-BUDDHIST.md - Part 3.6, Section 8.3 (transformations)
- **Texts**: Lotus Sutra, Surangama Sutra, Pure Land sutras
- **Forms**: Male (Sanskrit), Female (Guanyin, Kannon, Quan Am)
- **Regional**: Tibet (Chenrezig), China (Guanyin), Japan (Kannon), Vietnam (Quan Am)
- **Peak**: Major bodhisattva across all Mahayana traditions

#### Manjushri (Wisdom Bodhisattva)
- **Status**: Important in Tibetan Buddhism and East Asian traditions
- **Focus**: Wisdom and understanding
- **Texts**: Mahayana sutras, Tibetan practice texts

#### Guanyin (East Asian Feminine Form)
- **Transformation**: From Avalokiteshvara
- **Region**: China, Southeast Asia
- **Syncretism**: Merged with Chinese mother-goddess traditions
- **Analysis**: HISTORICAL-ANALYSIS-HINDU-BUDDHIST.md - Section 8.3 (East Asian Transformation)

#### Chenrezig (Tibetan Tantric Form)
- **Original**: Avalokiteshvara
- **Tibetan**: Chenrezig ("The One Who Looks with Clear Eyes")
- **Role**: National patron of Tibet, Dalai Lama identified as incarnation
- **Practice**: Central to Tibetan Buddhist tantric meditation
- **Key Text**: "Om Mani Padme Hum" mantra
- **Analysis**: HISTORICAL-ANALYSIS-HINDU-BUDDHIST.md - Section 2.4 (Tibetan Buddhism)

## By Time Period

### Vedic Period (1500-500 BCE)
- **File**: Analysis Part 1, Section 1.1
- **Deities**: Prajapati (Brahma), Rudra (Shiva), Indra, Agni, Varuna
- **Characteristics**: Ritualistic, nature deities, functional roles
- **Texts**: Rigveda, Yajur Veda, Atharva Veda

### Upanishadic Period (800-500 BCE)
- **File**: Analysis Part 1, Section 1.2
- **Development**: Philosophical revolution, Brahman concept
- **Texts**: Upanishads, especially Svetasvatara Upanishad
- **Impact**: Elevation of Rudra-Shiva, yoga and meditation introduced

### Epic Period (500 BCE - 500 CE)
- **File**: Analysis Part 1, Section 1.3
- **Texts**: Mahabharata (including Bhagavad Gita), Ramayana
- **Developments**: Avatar doctrine, Trimurti, dharma concept
- **Peak Theology**: Bhagavad Gita (Krishna as supreme)

### Puranic Period (500-1500 CE)
- **File**: Analysis Part 1, Section 1.4
- **Texts**: 18 Mahapuranas, Tantric texts
- **Developments**: Sectarian schools solidify, temple worship, bhakti movements
- **Regional**: South India becomes peak of Shaivism

### Modern Period (1500 CE - Present)
- **File**: Analysis Part 1, Section 1.5
- **Characteristics**: Colonial impact, reformation movements, global spread
- **Movements**: Arya Samaj, Ramakrishna, Vivekananda, ISKCON

### Early Buddhism (500-200 BCE)
- **File**: Analysis Part 2, Section 2.2
- **School**: Theravada (most conservative)
- **Focus**: Buddha as exemplary teacher
- **Texts**: Pali Canon

### Mahayana Emergence (100 BCE - 500 CE)
- **File**: Analysis Part 2, Section 2.3
- **Developments**: Buddha elevated to cosmic, bodhisattva ideal
- **Texts**: Lotus Sutra, Pure Land sutras
- **Philosophy**: Buddha-nature doctrine

### Tibetan Buddhism (600 CE - Present)
- **File**: Analysis Part 2, Section 2.4
- **Characteristic**: Tantric integration, meditation focus
- **Schools**: Nyingma, Kagyu, Sakya, Gelug
- **Central**: Avalokiteshvara (Chenrezig) as patron

### East Asian Development (400 CE - Present)
- **File**: Analysis Part 2, Section 2.5
- **Regions**: China, Japan, Korea, Vietnam
- **Development**: Gender transformations (Guanyin, Kannon)
- **Syncretism**: Merger with local traditions
