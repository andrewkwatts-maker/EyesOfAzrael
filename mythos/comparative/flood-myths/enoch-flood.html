1 Enoch 6-11, 83-84, 89, 106-107

Overview: Expanding Genesis 6:1-4

The Book of Enoch (1 Enoch) provides extensive narrative detail on one of the Bible's most cryptic passages: Genesis 6:1-4, which briefly mentions "sons of God" taking "daughters of men" as wives and producing the Nephilim. Where Genesis offers four verses, Enoch devotes entire chapters to explaining what led to the flood: a cosmic rebellion of angelic beings ("Watchers"), their illicit union with human women, the birth of giant hybrid offspring, and the teaching of forbidden knowledge that corrupted humanity beyond redemption.

In the Enochic tradition, the flood becomes not merely judgment on human wickedness, but necessary purification of earth contaminated by angelic transgression, hybrid creatures, and forbidden arts. The Watchers are bound until final judgment, the Nephilim destroyed, and Noah preserved to maintain pure human lineage. This expanded narrative profoundly influenced Second Temple Judaism, early Christianity (cited in Jude), and apocalyptic literature.

Then the LORD said, 'My Spirit shall not abide in man forever, for he is flesh: his days shall be 120 years.' The Questions Genesis Leaves Unanswered:

  • Who are "sons of God"? Divine beings? Descendants of Seth? Royal figures?
  • What are Nephilim? Giants? Fallen ones? Hybrid creatures?
  • Why did this provoke flood? What was the nature of the corruption?
  • What happened to them? How were they destroyed? What became of the "sons of God"?
  • "And also afterward"? How did Nephilim survive the flood (Numbers 13:33)?

The Book of Enoch's Answer:

1 Enoch provides detailed narrative answering all these questions: 200 angelic Watchers descend, take oaths on Mt. Hermon, marry human women, produce giant offspring called Nephilim, teach forbidden knowledge (weapons, magic, astrology), corrupt all flesh, leading to divine intervention through both angelic judgment (Watchers bound, Nephilim destroyed by mutual slaughter) and the flood to cleanse earth.

üîó Theological Significance: Why the Enochic Flood Matters

1. Cosmic Scope of Flood

The flood judges not merely human sin but angelic rebellion. It's cosmic cleansing, not just human punishment. Watchers bound, Nephilim destroyed, earth purified of supernatural contamination.

2. Genetic Corruption

Nephilim represent genetic/spiritual corruption of human race. Noah "perfect in his generations" (Gen 6:9) may mean genetically pure, not Nephilim-corrupted. Flood preserves pure human line.

3. Forbidden Knowledge

Technology and occult knowledge inappropriately revealed. Weapons, sorcery, astrology - premature revelation leading to corruption. Flood resets technological development, purges occult practices.

4. Bound Until Judgment

Watchers not destroyed but imprisoned "for seventy generations" until final judgment. Referenced in 2 Peter 2:4 ("angels who sinned... cast into hell") and Jude 6 ("angels... kept in darkness, bound with everlasting chains").

5. Post-Flood Nephilim Problem

Genesis 6:4 says Nephilim were "also afterward" - Numbers 13:33 confirms giants in Canaan. How did they survive? Theories: Ham's wife carried genetics, another incursion, or "Nephilim" became general term for giants.

6. New Testament Citations

Jude 14-15 directly quotes 1 Enoch 1:9. Jude 6 and 2 Peter 2:4 reference Watchers. Early Christianity treated Enochic traditions as authentic history, not mere mythology.
