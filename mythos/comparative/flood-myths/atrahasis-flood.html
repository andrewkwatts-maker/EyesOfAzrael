Oldest Complete Flood Narrative (c. 1700 BCE): Creation, Noise Problem, Divine Solutions, and Population Control

Overview: The Atrahasis Epic

Dating: Old Babylonian period, c. 1700 BCE (older than Gilgamesh Tablet 11)

Language: Akkadian (Babylonian)

Significance: Earliest complete flood account preserved; likely source for Gilgamesh Tablet 11

Hero: Atrahasis ("Extremely Wise")

Unique Elements: Explains why flood was necessary; includes pre-flood failed attempts at population control; post-flood resolution includes ongoing population limits

The Full Narrative Arc

Part I: Creation and Human Purpose

Unlike Genesis (humanity created in God's image for relationship), Atrahasis begins with gods creating humans as slave labor:

  • Problem: Lesser gods (Igigi) forced to dig irrigation canals, maintain rivers
  • Rebellion: Igigi revolt after 40 years of hard labor, burn their tools
  • Solution: Ea (Enki) proposes creating humans to do the work instead
  • Method: Goddess Nintu mixes clay with blood of slain god (We-ilu), creates 7 male + 7 female humans
  • Result: Humans spread across earth, dig canals, work fields, worship gods

Part II: The Noise Problem

"The land extended and the people multiplied. The land was bellowing like a bull. The god got disturbed with their uproar. Enlil heard their noise and addressed the great gods, 'The noise of mankind has become too intense for me. With their uproar I am deprived of sleep.'"

The Core Issue:

  • Humans multiply rapidly (successful at their purpose)
  • Population creates noise that disturbs gods (especially Enlil)
  • Enlil cannot sleep due to "uproar" or "din" of humanity
  • Unlike Genesis: Not moral corruption but sheer numbers and noise

Part III: Failed Solutions - Enlil's Attempts

Before resorting to flood, Enlil tries three other population control methods, each thwarted by Enki:

Attempt 1: Plague

  • Enlil's Plan: Send disease to reduce human population
  • Enki's Counter: Warns Atrahasis, instructs humans to worship Namtar (plague god) exclusively
  • Result: Namtar flattered by exclusive worship, withdraws plague
  • Outcome: Population rebounds

Attempt 2: Drought

  • Enlil's Plan: Cut off rains, create famine by drought
  • Enki's Counter: Warns Atrahasis, instructs worship of Adad (rain god)
  • Result: Adad sends rain despite Enlil's decree
  • Outcome: Crops grow, population continues

Attempt 3: Severe Famine

  • Enlil's Plan: More extreme famine - locks all resources
  • Duration: Six years of devastating famine
  • Result: Humans resort to cannibalism, mothers eating children
  • Enki's Counter: Eventually provides fish from sea to sustain remnant
  • Outcome: Humans survive even this

Part IV: The Flood - Final Solution

"Enlil made his voice heard and addressed the assembly, 'Come, all of us, and take an oath about the flood!' Anu swore first, Enlil swore, his sons swore with him. At first they were deterred from telling Atrahasis about the flood."

Divine Council:

  • Gods swear oath to proceed with flood
  • Bind each other not to warn humanity
  • Universal destruction decided

Enki's Warning

Like in Gilgamesh, Enki technically obeys oath while violating spirit:

  • Method: Speaks to reed wall of Atrahasis's house (not directly to him)
  • Instructions: "Tear down your house, build a boat. Abandon possessions, seek life. Spurn property, save life. Take aboard seed of all living creatures."

The Deluge

"The Anunnaki raised torches, lighting the land with their glow. The stillness brought by the Storm-god passed over the sky and all light was turned to darkness... For seven days and seven nights the flood swept over the land, and the huge boat was tossed about on the great waters."
  • Duration: Seven days and seven nights
  • Divine Terror: Gods frightened by flood's power, crouch in fear
  • Ishtar's Lament: Wails like woman in childbirth, regrets decision

Aftermath and Sacrifice

  • Atrahasis exits boat
  • Offers sacrifice
  • "The gods smelled the savor, the gods gathered like flies over the offering"
  • Gods realize they need humans (for food/worship)

Part V: The Resolution - Population Control Measures

Unlike Genesis (rainbow covenant, no more floods), Atrahasis ends with ONGOING population controls:

Permanent Solutions Enacted:

  1. Infant Mortality: Some children will die in childbirth or infancy
  2. Barren Women: Certain women will be infertile, unable to bear children
  3. Demons Who Snatch Babies: Specific demons (Pashittu-demon) will take newborns
  4. Celibate Priestesses: Categories of women forbidden to marry/bear children:
    • Ugbabtu-priestess
    • Entu-priestess
    • Sacred prostitutes

Purpose: Keep human population at manageable level so gods aren't disturbed again. Ongoing regulation instead of periodic annihilation.

Significance and Comparison

Why Atrahasis Matters

  • Earliest complete account: Predates Gilgamesh Tablet 11 by ~500 years
  • Provides context: Explains why flood necessary (noise problem, failed alternatives)
  • Source text: Gilgamesh Tablet 11 likely adapted from Atrahasis tradition
  • Theological framework: Shows Mesopotamian worldview: humans as divine slaves, gods as limited/flawed

What Genesis Transforms

The Genesis account takes the Atrahasis framework but radically reinterprets:

  • From utility to dignity: Humans not slaves but image-bearers
  • From annoyance to grief: God "grieved to his heart" (Gen 6:6), not just annoyed
  • From pragmatism to covenant: Not population control but relationship promise
  • From dependence to sovereignty: God needs nothing from humans, acts from love/justice
