The Eternal Struggle Between Light and Darkness

Explore one of humanity's oldest monotheistic faiths, founded by the prophet Zarathustra (Zoroaster). Zoroastrianism centers on the eternal cosmic struggle between Ahura Mazda (Wise Lord) and Angra Mainyu (Destructive Spirit), emphasizing the sacred fire, ethical dualism, and the path of good thoughts, good words, and good deeds.

Sacred Fire

Fire (Atar) is the supreme symbol of Ahura Mazda's divine light and wisdom. Fire temples house the eternal flame, which must never be extinguished. Fire represents purity, truth, and the presence of the divine in the physical world.

Ethical Path

The threefold ethical foundation of Zoroastrianism: Good Thoughts, Good Words, Good Deeds (Humata, Hukhta, Huvarshta). Every individual has free will to choose between good and evil, and their choices directly impact the cosmic struggle.

Chinvat Bridge

The bridge of judgment that souls must cross after death. The righteous pass safely to paradise, while the wicked fall into the abyss. The soul's deeds determine the bridge's width.

  • Avesta: The sacred scriptures of Zoroastrianism
  • Gathas: Hymns attributed to Zarathustra himself, the oldest part of the Avesta
  • Yasna: Liturgical texts including the Gathas, used in worship ceremonies
