Chimera

Overview

The Chimera was one of the most fearsome monsters in Greek mythologyâ€”a fire-breathing hybrid creature with the head of a lion, the body of a goat, and a serpent for a tail. This terrifying beast ravaged the countryside of Lycia in Asia Minor, devouring livestock and terrorizing the local population until it was slain by the hero Bellerophon.

Physical Description

The Chimera's monstrous form combined three distinct animals:

  • Lion's Head: The front part featured a ferocious lion's head with powerful jaws
  • Goat's Body: A second headâ€”that of a goatâ€”emerged from its back
  • Serpent's Tail: Its tail was a living serpent that could strike independently
  • Fire Breath: The creature breathed devastating flames from its lion mouth

Mythology and Origin

According to Hesiod's Theogony, the Chimera was offspring of the primordial monsters Typhon and Echidna, making it sibling to other legendary beasts including the Hydra, Cerberus, and the Nemean Lion. The creature was raised by King Amisodorus of Caria before it escaped to terrorize Lycia.

The Defeat by Bellerophon

The hero Bellerophon was sent by King Iobates of Lycia to slay the Chimeraâ€”a task the king hoped would result in Bellerophon's death. However, with the aid of the winged horse Pegasus, Bellerophon was able to attack the monster from the air, staying beyond reach of its flames and claws.

According to one version of the myth, Bellerophon attached a lump of lead to the tip of his spear. When thrust into the Chimera's flaming mouth, the lead melted and poured down the creature's throat, killing it from within. Other versions simply describe Bellerophon striking the beast down with arrows and spear from his aerial vantage point.

Symbolism and Legacy

The Chimera has become the archetypal impossible hybrid, and its name has entered common usage:

  • Impossible Ideas: A "chimera" now means any unrealistic or fanciful notion
  • Hybrid Creatures: The term describes any composite organism in biology
  • Destructive Force: Represents unnatural combination leading to chaos
  • Heroic Challenge: Symbolizes seemingly insurmountable obstacles overcome through cleverness

Modern Influence

The Chimera appears throughout fantasy literature, games, and art as the quintessential hybrid monster. In modern biology, "chimera" refers to organisms with cells from multiple genetic sources, continuing the ancient concept of impossible combination.
