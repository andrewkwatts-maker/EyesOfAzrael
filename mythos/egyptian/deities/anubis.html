Anubis ( Inpu , Anpu ) – jnpw

Guardian of the Dead, Lord of Mummification, Guide of Souls

Anubis is the ancient jackal-headed god of death, mummification, and the afterlife. As the original lord of the necropolis and guide of souls through the Duat , Anubis stands as protector of the dead and guardian of the scales of judgment. His presence ensures proper burial rites and safe passage to the realm of Osiris .

Mythology & Stories

Anubis 's mythology centers on death, preservation, and judgment. As one of the oldest gods in the Egyptian pantheon, his role evolved from lord of the dead to psychopomp and guardian of mummification secrets.

Relationships

Family

  • Parents: Osiris and Nephthys (in most traditions), though some texts claim Ra or Set as father
  • Consort: Anput (female form of Anubis ), Input
  • Children: Kebechet (goddess of purification and cooling water for the dead)
  • Siblings: Various, depending on parentage tradition

Allies & Enemies

  • Allies: Osiris (lord of the dead he serves), Isis (partner in mummification), Thoth (fellow guardian of judgment), Ma'at ( justice he upholds), Horus (fellow opponent of Set )
  • Enemies: Set (murderer of Osiris ), tomb robbers, those who desecrate the dead, demons of the Duat who threaten souls

Worship & Rituals

Sacred Sites

Anubis 's primary cult center was Cynopolis (Hardai, "City of the Dog") in Upper Egypt. He was also worshipped at necropolis sites throughout Egypt, particularly at Abydos , Memphis, and Thebes. Every tomb and embalming workshop was considered sacred to Anubis , and his priests (called "Imy-ut" or "He who is in the place of embalming") performed funerary rites.

Festivals

  • The Festival of Wag: Celebrated during the first month of the flood season, honoring the dead with offerings and processionals to tombs. Anubis received special offerings as guardian of the necropolis.
  • Opening of the Mouth Ceremony: Performed on mummies and tomb statues, this ritual reanimated the deceased's senses. Anubis priests presided, using ritual instruments to symbolically open the mouth, eyes, and ears of the dead.

Offerings

Offerings to Anubis included: myrrh and frankincense (embalming materials), natron (purification salt), linen bandages, black dogs or jackals (symbolic), bread and beer, ox meat, incense. Offerings were placed at tomb entrances and in funerary temples, asking for Anubis 's protection of the deceased and safe passage through the underworld .

Prayers & Invocations

Prayers to Anubis emphasized his protective role: "Homage to you, Anubis , who dwells in the embalming chamber. Grant that I may enter the Hall of Two Truths with a pure heart. Guard my body in the sacred land. Lead me safely through the passages of the Duat . Let your hand be upon me in the hour of judgment. May I stand justified before Osiris , my heart lighter than the feather of Ma'at ."

Embalmers invoked: " Anubis , Lord of Secrets, guide my hands in this sacred work. Preserve this flesh that the ba may return. Purify this body that it may be worthy of eternal life . As you preserved Osiris , so preserve this servant of the gods."

Iconography

Anubis appears in several iconic forms:

  • Jackal-Headed Man: Most common depiction, showing a human body with black jackal head, holding was scepter and ankh
  • Full Jackal: Lying on a shrine or tomb, representing his role as guardian of burial grounds
  • At the Scales: Depicted adjusting or watching the scales in judgment scenes from the Book of the Dead
  • Embalming Table: Shown performing mummification, often with Osiris on the table

His black color represents both the color of decomposition and the fertile black soil of the Nile, symbolizing regeneration and rebirth after death.
