Overview

The sphinx is a legendary creature with the body of a lion and the head of a human, symbolizing the union of physical strength and human intelligence. In Egyptian tradition, sphinxes served as guardians of sacred temples and tombs, protecting them from chaos and evil forces.

The Great Sphinx of Giza

The most famous sphinx is the Great Sphinx of Giza, built during the reign of Pharaoh Khafre (c. 2558-2532 BCE). This colossal limestone statue measures 73 meters long and 20 meters high, with the face believed to represent Khafre himself.

The Great Sphinx faces east, greeting the rising sun. Ancient Egyptians called it "Hor-em-akhet" (Horus of the Horizon), associating it with Ra-Horakhty, the sun god.

Types of Sphinxes

  • Androsphinx: Human-headed sphinx (most common in Egypt)
  • Criosphinx: Ram-headed sphinx, associated with Amun-Ra
  • Hieracosphinx: Falcon-headed sphinx, associated with Horus

Symbolism and Function

Sphinxes represented:

  • Royal Power: The pharaoh's strength (lion) and wisdom (human head)
  • Divine Protection: Guardians warding off chaos and evil
  • Solar Connection: Associates with the sun god's power and eternal vigilance
  • Sacred Boundaries: Marked transitions between sacred and profane space
