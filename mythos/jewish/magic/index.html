Evolution to Kabbalah

Merkavah mysticism flourished from the 1st-10th centuries CE, eventually evolving into medieval Kabbalah.

Key Texts:

  • Hekhalot Rabbati: Greater Palaces text
  • Hekhalot Zutarti: Lesser Palaces text
  • Ma'aseh Merkavah: Work of the Chariot
  • Shi'ur Komah: Measurement of the Divine Body (highly controversial)

Influence: Merkavah techniques influenced later Kabbalah, Hasidism, and continue to inspire Jewish mystical practice today, though usually in modified, safer forms.

Modern View: Most contemporary teachers view Merkavah texts as metaphorical descriptions of consciousness states rather than literal instructions for supernatural ascent.
