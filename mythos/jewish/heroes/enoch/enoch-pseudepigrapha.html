Enochic Literature: Complete Overview

From Genesis's four verses, an entire literature emerged. The Enochic corpus includes 1 Enoch, 2 Enoch, 3 Enoch, Jubilees, Genesis Apocryphon, Book of Giants, and numerous fragments. Spanning from the 4th century BCE to the 6th century CE (and beyond), these texts transform Enoch from a brief biblical character into the central figure of Jewish apocalypticism and mysticism.

1 Enoch (Ethiopic Enoch)

Date: 4th century BCE - 1st century CE (composite)
Language: Originally Aramaic → Greek → Ge'ez (Ethiopic)
Preservation: Complete in Ethiopic; Aramaic fragments at Qumran; Greek fragments
Canonical Status: Ethiopian/Eritrean Orthodox Church scripture; apocryphal elsewhere

Five Books:

  1. Book of Watchers (1-36): Fall of angels, Enoch's ascents, cosmic tour
  2. Parables/Similitudes (37-71): Son of Man figure, final judgment, Enoch's final ascent
  3. Astronomical Book (72-82): 364-day calendar, sun/moon paths, Uriel's teaching
  4. Dream Visions (83-90): Flood vision, Animal Apocalypse (allegorical history)
  5. Epistle (91-108): Apocalypse of Weeks, exhortations, woes against sinners

Importance: Foundational apocalyptic text. Quoted in Jude 14-15 (NT validates!). Developed angelology, demonology, Watchers myth, eschatology. Influenced early Christianity profoundly.

2 Enoch (Slavonic Enoch / Secrets of Enoch)

Date: 1st century CE (disputed - possibly later)
Language: Originally Greek (?) → Old Church Slavonic
Preservation: Slavonic manuscripts only
Canonical Status: Not canonical anywhere

Content:

  • Enoch's ascent through 10 heavens: Each heaven described in detail
  • Creation secrets revealed: How God created world from nothing
  • 365 years in heaven = 365 days on earth: Time dilation
  • Returns briefly to teach sons: Especially Methuselah
  • Taken back permanently: Second, final ascent
  • Moral exhortations: Ethical teachings for family

The Ten Heavens:

  1. Angels ruling stars and weather
  2. Fallen angels imprisoned in darkness
  3. Paradise (Garden of Eden) with Tree of Life
  4. Sun and moon courses
  5. Giant angels (Watchers/Grigori)
  6. Archangels and angelic hosts
  7. Thrones, dominions, powers
  8. Controlling seasons and calendar
  9. Twelve zodiac signs, angelic dwelling
  10. God's throne, seraphim, cherubim - Enoch sees God's face!

Unique elements: Most detailed creation account in Enochic lit; elaborate heaven structure; Enoch sees God directly (rare!).

3 Enoch (Sefer Hekhalot / Hebrew Enoch)

Date: 5th-6th century CE (possibly earlier material)
Language: Hebrew
Tradition: Merkavah (chariot/throne) mysticism
Canonical Status: Not canonical

Content:

  • Rabbi Ishmael's ascent through seven heavenly halls
  • Meets Metatron: "I am Enoch, son of Jared"
  • Enoch's transformation: Cosmic body, fiery substance, 72 wings, 365,000 eyes
  • Metatron = "Lesser YHWH": Bears divine Name
  • "Two Powers" controversy: Aher sees Metatron seated, thinks "two gods"
  • Metatron punished with 60 fiery lashes to clarify subordination

Significance: Human becomes greatest angel. Most radical theosis in Jewish mysticism. Influenced Kabbalah. "Lesser YHWH" title provocative.

Book of Jubilees

Date: c. 160-150 BCE
Language: Hebrew → Greek → Ge'ez (Ethiopic)
Genre: Rewritten Bible (Genesis-Exodus retold)
Canonical Status: Ethiopian/Eritrean Orthodox scripture; apocryphal elsewhere
Qumran: 15 manuscripts found (highly valued by Essenes)

Enoch in Jubilees:

  • First human to learn writing (Jub 4:17-18)
  • Wrote signs of heaven according to months (calendar!)
  • Testified against Watchers (Jub 4:22)
  • Taken to Garden of Eden (Jub 4:23) - specific location!
  • Writes condemnation and judgment of world there (ongoing role)
  • Set as sign and witness until day of condemnation
  • Taught Methuselah writing, wisdom, calendar

Calendar emphasis: Jubilees passionately advocates 364-day solar calendar. Condemns lunar calendar as "forgetting feasts."

Genesis Apocryphon (1QapGen)

Date: 1st century BCE - 1st century CE
Language: Aramaic
Source: Qumran Cave 1
Condition: Damaged, partially legible

Enoch's Role (Columns 0-17):

  • Noah's birth: Lamech sees his unusual appearance (glowing, white, beautiful)
  • Suspects Watcher paternity: Fears wife Bat-Enosh had affair with fallen angel
  • Methuselah consults Enoch (who is already in heaven!)
  • Enoch confirms: Noah is Lamech's son, not Watcher offspring
  • Prophecy of Flood: Enoch reveals coming judgment

Remarkable detail: Enoch is consulted after his assumption - he's accessible from heaven! Continues to guide family.

Book of Giants (Qumran + Manichean)

Date: 2nd-1st century BCE
Language: Aramaic (Qumran), Middle Persian/Parthian/Sogdian (Manichean)
Sources: 4Q530-533 (Dead Sea Scrolls); Central Asian Manichean texts
Relation: Part of Enochic Book of Watchers tradition

Content:

  • Nephilim giants' perspective: Sons of Watchers
  • Dreams of doom: Giants have ominous visions
  • Consult Enoch: Ask him to interpret dreams
  • Enoch pronounces judgment: Giants and Watchers will be destroyed
  • Giants' fear and violence: Before Flood wipes them out

Named giants: Ohyah, Hahyah, Mahaway (sent to consult Enoch)

The Enochic Movement

These texts weren't random compositions but represent a coherent Enochic Judaism - an alternative to what became rabbinic Judaism. The movement emphasized:

  • Heavenly revelation over Torah tradition: Direct access to God's secrets
  • Apocalyptic worldview: Cosmic battle, coming judgment, angels/demons active
  • Priestly calendar: 364-day year vs. rabbinic lunar
  • Enoch as founder: First scribe, first priest (?), first prophet
  • Separation from Jerusalem Temple: Compromised, using wrong calendar

The Qumran community embodied this Enochic vision. Early Christianity inherited much of it (angels, demons, eschatology, Jude's quote). Ethiopian Christianity preserved it fully. But rabbinic Judaism rejected it, and 1 Enoch was lost to the West for centuries - until rediscovered in Ethiopia in the 18th century.
