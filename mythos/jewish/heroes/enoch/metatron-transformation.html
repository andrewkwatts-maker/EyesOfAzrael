3 Enoch: Enoch Becomes Metatron

3 Enoch (also called Sefer Hekhalot or Hebrew Enoch) is a Jewish mystical text from the 5th-6th century CE that describes Rabbi Ishmael's ascent to heaven, where he meets the angel Metatron - who reveals that he was once the patriarch Enoch. The text describes Enoch's transformation into the greatest of angels, granted cosmic proportions, fiery substance, and the divine Name itself. Metatron becomes "the Lesser YHWH," second only to God in heaven.

Rabbi Ishmael's Vision (3 Enoch 1-2)

"Rabbi Ishmael said: When I ascended on high to behold the vision of the Merkabah and had entered the six halls, one within the other, and when I came to the door of the seventh hall, I stood still in prayer before the Holy One, blessed be He. I lifted up my eyes to the height and said..."

Rabbi Ishmael ascends through seven heavenly halls (Hekhalot) and meets Metatron at the entrance to the seventh. Metatron introduces himself:

"I am Enoch, son of Jared." (3 Enoch 4:2)

This shocking revelation - that the greatest of angels was once human - forms the basis of 3 Enoch's theology.

The Transformation of Enoch (3 Enoch 3-16)

FROM HUMAN TO ANGEL: THE METAMORPHOSIS

Stage 1: Taken from Earth

"The Holy One, blessed be He, took me from the generation of the Flood. And He lifted me on the wings of the Shekhinah to the highest heaven and brought me into the great palaces which are in the height of the Araboth heaven, where are the Throne of the Shekhinah, the Merkabah, the hosts of angels, and the seraphim."

⚠️ CONTROVERSIAL: "Lesser YHWH"

The title "Lesser YHWH" (YHWH ha-Qatan) is one of the most theologically daring claims in all of Jewish literature. Metatron bears the divine Name itself - the Tetragrammaton. This raised concerns about "two powers in heaven" heresy.

Biblical basis: Exodus 23:21 - "My Name is in him" (said of the angel who leads Israel)

Stage 6: Name Change

"The Holy One, blessed be He, called me 'Metatron' in the presence of all His heavenly household."

Etymology of "Metatron" (uncertain):

  • Meta-thronos (Greek): "One who serves behind the throne" or "next to the throne"
  • Metator (Latin): "Measurer" or "guide" (angelic guide)
  • Hebrew derivation: Possibly from מט"ט root (uncertain)
  • Gematria: Numerical value = Shaddai (Almighty)

The "Two Powers" Controversy (3 Enoch 16)

Heresy and Punishment: Aher's Error

"When Aher [Elisha ben Abuyah] came to behold the vision of the Merkabah, he gazed at Metatron and saw that permission was given to him to sit and write down the merits of Israel. He said: 'It is taught that on high there is no sitting, no emulation, no back, and no weariness. Perhaps - God forbid! - there are two powers in heaven!' Immediately a voice came forth from heaven and said, 'Return, backsliding children (Jeremiah 3:14) - except for Aher!'" (3 Enoch 16:1-5)

The Incident:

  • Aher (Elisha ben Abuyah): Famous rabbi who became heretic/apostate
  • Sees Metatron seated: Shocking - angels stand, only God sits
  • Concludes: "Two powers in heaven!" (dualism/bitheism heresy)
  • Result: Excluded from repentance, becomes permanent apostate

Metatron's Punishment:

"Immediately the Holy One, blessed be He, said to Metatron: 'Why did you not stand up before him when you saw him?' At once permission was given to Anafiel the Prince to strike Metatron with sixty lashes of fire so as to make him stand up before him."
  • 60 fiery lashes: Severe punishment to demonstrate subordination
  • Made to stand: Clarify he is not equal to God
  • Purpose: Prevent "two powers" heresy from spreading

Talmudic Parallels:

  • Hagigah 15a: "Aher cut the plantings" - became heretic after seeing Metatron
  • Sanhedrin 38b: Discussion of "two powers in heaven" as forbidden belief
  • Debate: Was Metatron tradition itself borderline heretical?

Theological Implications:

The Metatron tradition walks a razor's edge between monotheism and dualism:

  • Radical claim: Human becomes divine-like, bears God's Name, sits on throne
  • Safeguard: Still subordinate, can be punished, serves God
  • Question: How close can an angel get to God without violating monotheism?
  • Christian parallel: Christ as divine yet distinct from Father - similar theological tension

Metatron's Cube

In sacred geometry, Metatron's Cube is a geometric figure containing all five Platonic solids, representing the underlying structure of creation. Metatron, as cosmic architect/scribe, oversees the geometric patterns of the universe.

The Meaning of Transformation

Enoch's transformation into Metatron represents the ultimate human potential - complete theosis (deification) within Jewish mystical thought. A mortal man becomes cosmic, fiery, enthroned, bearing the very Name of God. Yet the tradition carefully maintains his subordination: he can be punished, he serves rather than rules, he is "Lesser YHWH" not YHWH himself. This tension between exaltation and subordination reflects Judaism's struggle to express mystical experience within strict monotheism. Enoch-Metatron becomes the bridge between human and divine, the proof that "walking with God" can lead to transformation beyond imagination - yet without collapsing the Creator-creature distinction. He is the highest thing creation can become while still being creation.
