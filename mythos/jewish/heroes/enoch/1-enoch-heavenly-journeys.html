1 Enoch: Heavenly Journeys and Cosmic Secrets

1 Enoch (also called Ethiopic Enoch or the Book of Enoch) is the most important and extensive of the Enochic writings. Composed in multiple stages between the 3rd century BCE and 1st century CE, it presents Enoch as a heavenly scribe who is shown the secrets of creation, the fate of the Watchers, the structure of the cosmos, and the coming judgment. The book had enormous influence on Second Temple Judaism and early Christianity.

Structure of 1 Enoch

1 Enoch is a composite work consisting of five major sections plus an appendix, written over several centuries:

Book 1

Book of Watchers

Chapters 1-36

c. 3rd century BCE

  • Fall of the Watchers (angels)
  • Enoch's intercession
  • First ascent to throne of God
  • Cosmic tour - ends of earth
  • Paradise, Sheol, judgment places
Book 2

Book of Parables/Similitudes

Chapters 37-71

c. 1st century BCE - 1st century CE

  • Three parables/visions
  • Son of Man figure
  • Final judgment scenes
  • Enoch's final ascent
  • Possibly becomes "Son of Man"
Book 3

Astronomical Book

Chapters 72-82

c. 4th-3rd century BCE (OLDEST)

  • Paths of sun and moon
  • 12 gates of heaven
  • 364-day solar calendar
  • Stars and seasons
  • Uriel as guide
Book 4

Book of Dream Visions

Chapters 83-90

c. 2nd century BCE (Maccabean period)

  • Vision of coming Flood
  • Animal Apocalypse (allegorical history)
  • Israel's history as animals
  • Seventy shepherds (angels over nations)
  • Final judgment and new Jerusalem
Book 5

Epistle of Enoch

Chapters 91-108

c. 2nd century BCE

  • Apocalypse of Weeks (history in 10 weeks)
  • Exhortations to righteousness
  • Woes against sinners
  • Blessing of righteous
  • Enoch's final words to Methuselah
Appendix

Birth of Noah

Chapters 106-107

Late addition

  • Noah born with unusual appearance
  • Lamech fears Watcher paternity
  • Methuselah asks Enoch (in heaven!)
  • Enoch confirms Noah is Lamech's son
  • Prophecy of coming Flood

Preservation and Manuscripts:

  • Complete: Only in Ge'ez (Ethiopic) - preserved by Ethiopian Orthodox Church
  • Aramaic fragments: Dead Sea Scrolls (4Q201-212, 4Q530-533) - proves pre-Christian Jewish origin
  • Greek fragments: Codex Panopolitanus, other papyri
  • Lost sections: Parables (chs. 37-71) NOT found at Qumran - may be later Christian addition or missing from DSS collection

Enoch's First Ascent: The Throne of God (1 Enoch 12-16)

Context: The Watchers' Fall

Before Enoch's ascent, chapters 6-11 describe the Watchers' sin:

  • 200 angels descended on Mt. Hermon (1 En 6:6)
  • Took human wives, fathered Nephilim giants (1 En 7:1-2)
  • Taught forbidden knowledge: weapons, cosmetics, sorcery, astrology (1 En 8:1-3)
  • Giants devoured humanity, violence filled earth (1 En 7:3-6)
  • Humanity cried out to heaven (1 En 8:4)
1 Enoch 12

Enoch Summoned as Scribe and Messenger

"And they took me to a place where there were those who were like flames of fire, and when they desired they appeared like men. And they brought me to a place of storm and to a mountain, the peak of which reached to heaven. And I saw the places of the luminaries..."
  • Enoch called before his translation
  • Appointed "scribe of righteousness" (1 En 12:4)
  • Given message for Watchers: no intercession, no forgiveness
1 Enoch 13-14

Message to the Watchers

"Go say to the Watchers of heaven, who sent you to petition on their behalf: 'You should petition on behalf of men, not men on behalf of you. Why have you left the high, holy, and eternal heaven, and lain with women...'"
  • Enoch delivers condemnation (1 En 13:1-7)
  • Watchers tremble with fear (1 En 13:3)
  • Ask Enoch to write their petition (1 En 13:4-7)
  • Enoch reads petition, falls asleep, has vision (1 En 13:8)
1 Enoch 14:8-25

The Vision of God's Throne - Enoch's First Ascent

THE THRONE VISION

First House - Outer Court
"I saw a house built of crystals, and among those crystals tongues of living fire. And I saw a house large as this house which was built of tongues of fire, and in every respect it excelled in splendor and magnificence and extent..." (1 En 14:10-11)
  • Walls of crystal
  • Floor of crystal tiles
  • Roof like path of stars and lightning
  • Cherubim patrol
Second House - Inner Sanctuary
"And I looked and saw in it a lofty throne; its appearance was as crystal, and the wheels thereof as the shining sun, and there was the vision of cherubim. And from underneath the throne came streams of flaming fire so that I could not look at it." (1 En 14:18-19)
  • Greater house, more magnificent
  • Floor: tongues of fire
  • Ceiling: path of stars
  • Throne of crystal
  • Wheels like shining sun (cf. Ezekiel 1!)
  • Streams of flaming fire from beneath
The Great Glory on the Throne
"And the Great Glory sat on it, and His raiment shone more brightly than the sun and was whiter than any snow. None of the angels could enter and behold His face by reason of the magnificence and glory, and no flesh could behold Him." (1 En 14:20-21)
  • "The Great Glory" - circumlocution for God
  • Raiment brighter than sun, whiter than snow
  • No angel can see his face
  • No flesh can behold him
  • Flaming fire around throne
Enoch's Reaction
"And I fell on my face, and trembled. And the Lord called me with His own mouth, and said to me: 'Come here, Enoch, and hear my word.'" (1 En 14:24)
  • Falls on face in terror
  • Trembles before divine presence
  • God calls him personally
  • Summoned to hear judgment message

Parallels with Biblical Throne Visions:

  • Isaiah 6:1-4: "I saw the Lord sitting on a throne, high and lifted up... seraphim... Holy, holy, holy..."
  • Ezekiel 1:4-28: Cherubim, wheels, throne like sapphire, figure like a man in fire and brightness
  • Daniel 7:9-10: "Ancient of Days... throne with wheels of burning fire... stream of fire..."
  • Revelation 4:1-11: Throne in heaven, crystal sea, twenty-four elders, four living creatures

1 Enoch 14 is part of Jewish Merkavah (chariot/throne) mysticism tradition!

1 Enoch 15-16

The Message of Condemnation

"Go, say to the Watchers of heaven... 'You were in heaven, but all the mysteries had not yet been revealed to you... and you knew worthless mysteries... You have defiled yourselves with the blood of women... therefore you shall have no peace.'" (1 En 16:2-4)
  • Watchers condemned for leaving heavenly station
  • Defiling themselves with women
  • Teaching forbidden mysteries
  • No intercession possible - "You should petition for men, not men for you!"
  • Spirits of giants become demons (1 En 15:8-12)
  • Watchers bound until final judgment

Enoch's Cosmic Tour (1 Enoch 17-36)

After delivering God's message to the Watchers, angels take Enoch on a tour of the ends of the earth and the hidden places of creation:

Seven Mountains

Mountain in midst reaches heaven; six others around it (1 En 18:6-9)

Seven Stars Bound

Fallen Watchers bound like burning mountains (1 En 18:13-16, 21:1-10)

Fiery Abyss

Place of judgment, prison for angels and men (1 En 21:7-10)

Sheol/Hades

Four hollow places for dead, separated by type (1 En 22:1-14)

Tree of Life

Fragrant tree, no flesh may touch until judgment (1 En 24:4-25:6)

Jerusalem Center

Middle of earth, where Tree of Life will be planted (1 En 26:1-2)

Valley of Hinnom

Accursed valley for final judgment (1 En 27:1-3)

Paradise in East

Garden of righteousness, every tree planted (1 En 32:3-6)

Gates of Heaven

12 gates for winds and stars (1 En 33-36)

Four Streams

Rivers flowing from Paradise (1 En 32:2)

Pillars of Heaven

Cosmic supports at ends of earth (1 En 18:1-5)

Storehouses

Winds, lightning, thunder, hail, snow (1 En 34-36, 60, 69)

The Four Hollow Places of Sheol (1 Enoch 22)

Compartment Occupants Condition Destiny
1st Hollow Righteous martyrs Bright spring of water, light Await resurrection to glory
2nd Hollow Ordinary sinners (unpunished in life) Suffering, torment Punished until judgment day
3rd Hollow Sinners already punished in life Suffering, but less severe Not resurrected for judgment (already paid?)
4th Hollow Ordinary righteous Peace, rest Await resurrection to life

Significance of Sheol Compartments:

This is one of the earliest Jewish texts to describe differentiated afterlife - not just "Sheol" generally, but separate places for righteous and wicked, martyrs and ordinary believers. This influenced later Jewish and Christian concepts of Hades, Paradise/Abraham's Bosom, and eventual resurrection.

Compare: Luke 16:19-31 (Rich Man and Lazarus) - Abraham's bosom vs. place of torment, "great chasm fixed between"

Enoch's Final Ascent and Transformation (1 Enoch 70-71)

The Ultimate Exaltation

In the Book of Parables (chs. 37-71), Enoch experiences a final, climactic ascent that may involve his transformation into the "Son of Man" figure he has been seeing in visions:

1 Enoch 70

Enoch's Name Taken Up

"And it came to pass after this that his name during his lifetime was raised aloft to that Son of Man and to the Lord of Spirits from amongst those who dwell on the earth." (1 En 70:1)
  • Enoch's "name" (identity/person) taken up
  • Raised to "Son of Man" and "Lord of Spirits"
  • Chariot of spirit carries him off (1 En 70:2)
  • From that day forward not numbered among mortals (1 En 70:3-4)
1 Enoch 71

Greeted as "Son of Man"?

"And the angel came to me and greeted me with his voice and said to me: 'You are the son of man who was born to righteousness, and righteousness dwells in you, and the righteousness of the Head of Days will not forsake you.'" (1 En 71:14)

Interpretive Debate:

  • View 1: Enoch becomes/is identified with "Son of Man" figure
  • View 2: "son of man" = human born for righteousness (not the eschatological Son of Man)
  • View 3: Corruption in text; originally separate figures
"And he said to me: 'You and your seed will dwell with the righteous forever, and righteousness will be your dwelling place forever; and it will be with you eternally, and you will have no sorrow or sickness or suffering or affliction or want.'" (1 En 71:16-17)
  • Enoch and his seed dwell with righteous forever
  • Eternal righteousness
  • No more suffering
  • Michael, Raphael, Gabriel, Phanuel show him all hidden things

The "Son of Man" in 1 Enoch:

The Parables section repeatedly describes a heavenly "Son of Man" figure:

  • Pre-existent: "Before sun and signs were created, before stars were made, his name was named before Lord of Spirits" (1 En 48:3)
  • Chosen One: "The Chosen One stands before the Lord of Spirits" (1 En 49:2)
  • Judge: "He will judge the secret things" (1 En 49:4)
  • Eschatological king: "All who dwell on earth will fall down and worship before him" (1 En 48:5)

Whether Enoch becomes this figure or is simply addressed as "a son of man" (human) in ch. 71 remains debated!

Enoch's Roles in 1 Enoch

1. Scribe of Righteousness

"And they said to me: 'Enoch, you scribe of righteousness...'" (1 En 12:4, 15:1)

  • Writes down heavenly visions
  • Records judgment against Watchers
  • Documents secrets shown to him
  • Passes knowledge to Methuselah and descendants

2. Witness Against the Watchers

"Go and make known to the Watchers of heaven..." (1 En 12:4)

  • Summoned to deliver condemnation
  • Refuses to intercede for them
  • Declares their judgment irreversible
  • Writes their petition but it is rejected

3. Revealer of Cosmic Secrets

"And they showed me all the hidden things..." (1 En 18:1)

  • Shown ends of earth
  • Secrets of Sheol and afterlife
  • Paradise and Tree of Life
  • Astronomical secrets (calendar)
  • Gates of heaven and storehouses

4. Prophet of Judgment

"Enoch, seventh from Adam, prophesied..." (Jude 14, quoting 1 En 1:9)

  • Prophesies coming Flood (through Methuselah → Noah)
  • Proclaims final judgment
  • "Behold, he comes with ten thousands of holy ones"
  • Warns against ungodliness

5. Mediator Between Heaven and Earth

Unique position: human taken to heaven while still alive

  • Stands before divine throne
  • Returns to deliver messages
  • Teaches Methuselah what he learned
  • Bridges mortal and immortal realms

6. Calendar Reformer

Astronomical Book (chs. 72-82)

  • Shown true solar calendar (364 days)
  • Condemns lunar calendar as error
  • Teaches proper feast days
  • Uriel reveals paths of heavenly bodies

The Significance of 1 Enoch

1 Enoch transforms the brief Genesis account into an elaborate mythology of heavenly ascent, cosmic revelation, and eschatological judgment. Enoch becomes the prototype of the apocalyptic visionary - one who is granted access to heaven's secrets and returns to warn humanity of coming judgment. The book influenced Second Temple Judaism profoundly, shaping ideas about angels, demons, the afterlife, final judgment, and the Son of Man figure. Early Christians embraced it (Jude quotes it as prophecy!), and it remained scripture for Ethiopian Orthodox Christians. Though not canonical in Judaism or most of Christianity, 1 Enoch's impact on biblical interpretation and theology cannot be overstated. It fills the silences of Genesis 5:24 with a universe of revelation.
