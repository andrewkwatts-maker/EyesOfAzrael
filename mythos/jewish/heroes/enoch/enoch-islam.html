Enoch as Idris in Islamic Tradition

In Islamic tradition, Idris (إدريس) is identified with the biblical Enoch. The Qur'an mentions him twice as a prophet and man of truth whom Allah "raised to a high station." Islamic tradition elaborates: Idris was the first to write with a pen, taught astronomy and mathematics, and was taken to the fourth heaven where he remains. Muhammad meets him during the Night Journey (Isra and Mi'raj).

Qur'anic References

Surah 19:56-57 (Maryam)

"And mention in the Book, Idris. Indeed, he was a man of truth and a prophet. And We raised him to a high station."

Key phrase: "Raised him to a high station" (رَفَعْنَاهُ مَكَانًا عَلِيًّا rafa'nahu makanan 'aliyyan) - clear assumption/ascension language

Surah 21:85-86

"And [mention] Ishmael and Idris and Dhul-Kifl. All were of the patient ones. And We admitted them into Our mercy. Indeed, they were of the righteous."

Idris listed among patient prophets, admitted to Allah's mercy (paradise/heaven)

Name Etymology

Idris (إدريس) possibly derived from:

  • Arabic درس (darasa): "to study, learn" - scholar, teacher
  • Hebrew חנוך (Chanokh): Enoch (phonetic adaptation)
  • Syriac/Aramaic influence: Early Muslim contact with Christian traditions

Traditional identification: Islamic scholars unanimously identify Idris with biblical Enoch (though some minority views suggest Ezra or Elijah)

Islamic Traditions About Idris

First to Write with Pen

Idris was the first human to write with a pen. Taught humanity writing and record-keeping.

Parallel: Jubilees 4:17 - "Enoch... first among men born on earth who learned writing and knowledge and wisdom"

First to Sew Garments

Before Idris, humans wore animal skins. He taught tailoring and sewing with needle and thread.

Culture-bringer role (like Prometheus in Greek myth)

Astronomer and Mathematician

Taught astronomy, calculation of seasons, mathematics. Revealed knowledge of stars and planets.

Parallel: 1 Enoch 72-82 - Astronomical Book with calendar/star knowledge

30 Divine Scrolls Revealed

Some traditions say Allah revealed 30 scrolls (suhuf) to Idris, containing divine wisdom.

Parallel: Abraham (10 scrolls), Seth (50 scrolls) in Islamic tradition

Lived 365 Years

Islamic tradition preserves the biblical detail: Idris lived 365 years before being raised.

Direct from Genesis 5:23

Prophet Before Noah

Idris preached monotheism to his generation. Warned against idol worship and sin before the Flood.

Antediluvian prophet chronology preserved

Nearly Perfect Alignment

The Islamic Idris tradition preserves nearly all elements of the biblical/Enochic tradition: assumption to heaven, 365 years, first writer, astronomical knowledge, antediluvian era, prophet role. This suggests either:

  1. Common source: Both traditions preserve genuine historical memory
  2. Transmission: Islamic tradition inherited from Jewish/Christian sources (likely, given pre-Islamic Arabia's contact)
  3. Independent revelation: Islamic theological claim - Allah revealed same truths

Three Abrahamic Traditions, One Figure

Enoch/Idris is one of the few figures honored across Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. All three traditions agree on the essential points: a righteous prophet who walked with God, was assumed to heaven without dying, and now dwells in God's presence. The variations in detail (which heaven, what he taught, etc.) show cultural elaboration, but the core narrative remains stable. In an age of interfaith tension, Enoch/Idris stands as a unifying figure - proof that the three traditions share a common heritage and reverence for those who "walk with God."
