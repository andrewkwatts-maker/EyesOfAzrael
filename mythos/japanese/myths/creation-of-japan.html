The Birth of the Islands from the Primordial Waters

The Creation of Japan is the foundational myth of Japanese cosmology, recorded in the Kojiki (712 CE) and Nihon Shoki (720 CE). After the spontaneous generation of the first kami in Takamagahara (the High Plain of Heaven), the divine couple Izanagi and Izanami descended to the primordial ocean and stirred the cosmic waters with a jeweled spear, giving birth to the islands of Japan and the myriad kami that inhabit them.

📖 The Story

🌞

The Primordial Beginning

At the dawn of time, when heaven and earth were not yet separated, the universe was a chaotic, unformed mass like an egg. Within this void, the first three primordial deities came into being spontaneously in Takamagahara - Ame-no-Minakanushi (Center of Heaven), Takamimusubi (High Creating Deity), and Kamimusubi (Divine Creating Deity). These invisible kami appeared alone and then hid themselves.

Realm: Takamagahara (High Plain of Heaven)

State: Chaos, unformed potential

⚡

The Seven Generations of Gods

After the first three came additional generations of kami. Two more deities appeared alone - Umashiashikabihikoji and Amenotokotachi. Then came five couples of brother-sister gods. The seventh and final generation of these primordial kami were Izanagi-no-Mikoto ("He Who Invites") and Izanami-no-Mikoto ("She Who Invites"), destined to become the creators of the world.

Generations: Seven divine generations (Kamiyonanayo)

Final Pair: Izanagi and Izanami

🌇

The Heavenly Mandate

The celestial kami gathered the younger deities Izanagi and Izanami on the Floating Bridge of Heaven (Ame-no-Ukihashi) and gave them the Heavenly Jeweled Spear (Ame-no-Nuboko). Their task: "Complete and solidify this drifting land!" Below them stretched an endless ocean of brine, formless and dark, awaiting the touch of divine will to transform chaos into order.

Location: Ame-no-Ukihashi (Floating Bridge of Heaven)

Tool: Ame-no-Nuboko (Heavenly Jeweled Spear)

Mission: Create solid land from the primordial waters

🌊

Stirring the Ocean

Standing on the Floating Bridge, Izanagi and Izanami together grasped the jeweled spear and thrust it down into the churning brine below. They stirred the ocean with the spear, drawing great circles through the waters. When they lifted the spear back up, brine dripped from its tip. As the drops fell back into the ocean, they coagulated and hardened, forming the first solid land.

Action: Stirring the primordial ocean (onogoro)

Result: Brine solidifies into land

🏝

Birth of Onogoro Island

The solidified drops of brine formed Onogoro-jima (the "Self-Curdling Island" or "Spontaneously Congealed Island"), the first land in existence. This sacred island became the foundation of all creation. Izanagi and Izanami descended from the heavens to this newborn island, which would serve as the marriage bed and birthplace of the Japanese archipelago and the myriad kami.

First Land: Onogoro-jima (Self-Curdling Island)

Significance: Foundation stone of creation

Sacred Status: The axis mundi, center of creation

⛩️

The Heavenly Pillar

Upon Onogoro Island, Izanagi and Izanami erected the Heavenly Pillar (Ame-no-Mihashira), a sacred column connecting heaven and earth. Around this pillar they built the Eight-Fathom Palace (Yahiro-dono), which would be their dwelling. The pillar stood at the center of creation, the cosmic axis around which all things would revolve, the connection between the divine realm above and the earthly realm being born.

Structure: Ame-no-Mihashira (Heavenly Pillar)

Function: Axis mundi, cosmic center

Palace: Yahiro-dono (Eight-Fathom Palace)

💏

The Marriage Ritual

Izanagi asked Izanami: "How is your body formed?" She replied: "My body is completely formed except for one part that is not yet complete." Izanagi said: "My body is completely formed, and there is one part that is formed to excess. I propose that we unite my excess part with your incomplete part, and thus create lands." They agreed to perform a marriage ceremony by circling the Heavenly Pillar in opposite directions and meeting on the far side.

Divine Conversation: Recognition of complementarity

Ritual: Circumambulation of the sacred pillar

Purpose: Sacred marriage (hieros gamos)

❌

The First Failed Birth

When they met after circling the pillar, Izanami spoke first: "What a beautiful and amiable youth!" Then Izanagi spoke: "What a beautiful and amiable maiden!" They united and Izanami gave birth - but the first child was Hiruko (Leech Child), a deformed being without bones. They placed Hiruko in a reed boat and set him adrift on the ocean. The second child was Awashima ("Foam Island"), also defective. These were not counted among their children.

First Child: Hiruko (Leech Child) - deformed, set adrift

Second Child: Awashima (Foam Island) - incomplete

Problem: Woman spoke first (ritual error)

🔴

Consulting Heaven

Troubled by these failures, Izanagi and Izanami ascended to heaven and consulted the elder kami through divination. The divine answer came: "It was not good that the woman spoke first. You must descend again and repeat the ritual correctly." The order of speech in the sacred marriage ritual was crucial - male initiative must precede female response, reflecting the cosmic order they were meant to establish.

Method: Futomani divination (bone oracle)

Verdict: Ritual protocol was violated

Solution: Repeat ceremony with correct order

✅

The Second Ritual

Izanagi and Izanami descended again to Onogoro Island and circled the Heavenly Pillar once more. This time, when they met, Izanagi spoke first: "What a beautiful and amiable maiden!" Then Izanami responded: "What a beautiful and amiable youth!" With the proper protocol observed, they united in sacred marriage. This time, their union would be fruitful and proper, giving birth to the world itself.

Correction: Male speaks first, female responds

Result: Successful hieros gamos

Cosmic Order: Established through ritual propriety

🏝🏞🏟

Birth of the Eight Islands

From their proper union, Izanami gave birth to the eight great islands of Japan. First came Awaji (the Island of Awaji), then Shikoku (the Island with Four Countries), then Oki, followed by Kyushu (the Island with Nine Countries). Next came Iki, then Tsushima, then Sado, and finally the largest - Honshu (called Oyamato Toyoakitsushima, "Great-Yamato-Fertile-Autumnal-Harvest-Island"). These eight islands together formed Oyashima-guni, the Land of Eight Great Islands - Japan itself.

The Eight Islands (Oyashima):

  • 1. Awaji-no-hō-no-sawake-no-shima (Awaji)
  • 2. Iyo-no-futana-no-shima (Shikoku)
  • 3. Oki-no-mitsugo-no-shima (Oki Islands)
  • 4. Tsukushi-no-shima (Kyushu)
  • 5. Iki-no-shima (Iki Island)
  • 6. Tsushima (Tsushima Island)
  • 7. Sado-no-shima (Sado Island)
  • 8. Ōyamato-toyoakitsu-shima (Honshu)
🏛

Birth of the Smaller Islands

After creating the eight great islands, Izanagi and Izanami continued their creative work. They gave birth to six more islands: Kibitsushima, Azukijima, Ōshima, Himeshima, Chikabushima, and the Mitsugo Islands. These smaller islands surrounded and completed the Japanese archipelago. The physical geography of Japan was now complete - a sacred land formed by divine union, ready to be populated with kami and eventually with humanity.

Additional Islands: Six smaller islands

Completion: The full archipelago

🌌

Birth of the Nature Kami

With the land formed, Izanami next gave birth to the kami who would inhabit and govern these islands. She bore the gods of wind, trees, mountains, and plains. From her womb came Ōyamatsu-mi (the god of mountains), Kaya-no-Hime (goddess of grasses), Kuku-no-Chi (god of trees), and countless others. Each geographical feature, each natural phenomenon received its divine patron. The land was not merely physical but sacred, every mountain and river the body or domain of a kami.

Nature Deities: Kami of natural phenomena

Examples: Mountain gods, tree gods, wind gods

Principle: All nature is inhabited by kami

🌱

Birth of Civilization Kami

Izanami continued giving birth to kami representing aspects of human civilization and cosmic order. She bore deities of the seas, harbors, winds, and food. Among these were Ōwatatsumi (the great sea god), Hayaakitsuhiko (god of wind), and Uke-Mochi (goddess of food). Each kami brought order to a particular sphere of existence, transforming raw nature into a cosmos suitable for divine and human habitation.

Culture Deities: Kami of civilization

Domains: Sea, food, dwelling, tools

Purpose: Making the world habitable

🔥

The Final Birth - Fire

At last, Izanami gave birth to Kagutsuchi, the god of fire. But this final birth proved catastrophic. The flames of the fire god burned Izanami's body terribly. Despite Izanagi's desperate attempts to save her, Izanami died from her burns - the first death in the cosmos. Even in her death throes, more kami were born from her vomit, urine, and feces, showing that creation continues even through dissolution. Her death marked the end of the age of pure creation and the beginning of death's presence in the world.

Final Child: Kagutsuchi (Fire God)

Consequence: Izanami's death - first death in the cosmos

Transition: From creation to mortality

👤 Key Characters

✨

Izanagi - He Who Invites

Izanagi-no-Mikoto is the primordial male creator deity, the seventh generation of celestial gods. Together with his sister-wife Izanami, he stirred the cosmic ocean to create Japan and fathered the myriad kami. After Izanami's death, his grief and subsequent purification gave birth to the three noble children - Amaterasu, Tsukuyomi, and Susanoo.

Role: Male creator principle

Symbols: The jeweled spear, the pillar

Domain: Life, order, creation

🌈

Izanami - She Who Invites

Izanami-no-Mikoto is the primordial female creator deity who gave birth to the islands of Japan and the first kami. Her body served as the vessel of creation, bringing forth land and gods through divine birth. Her death giving birth to fire marked the first mortality and transformed her from creator goddess to queen of Yomi, the underworld.

Role: Female creator principle

Function: Divine mother, birthgiver

Transformation: From life-giver to death-ruler

🌞

The Kotoamatsukami

The Kotoamatsukami are the first primordial deities who came into being in Takamagahara at the beginning of creation. These include Ame-no-Minakanushi (Center of Heaven), Takamimusubi (High Creating Deity), and Kamimusubi (Divine Creating Deity). They appeared spontaneously, alone, and then hid themselves, but their existence enabled all subsequent creation.

Number: First three to five deities

Nature: Invisible, primordial principles

Function: Foundation of existence

🏝

Onogoro Island

Though not a deity, Onogoro-jima (Self-Curdling Island) is the sacred first land, the foundation of all creation. Born from drops of brine falling from the Heavenly Jeweled Spear, it became the marriage bed of the creator couple and the birthplace of Japan. Its exact location is mythical, though several locations in Japan claim the honor.

Significance: First solid matter in existence

Function: Cosmic center, axis mundi

Status: Sacred foundation of Japan

🔥

Kagutsuchi - The Fire God

Kagutsuchi (or Hinokagutsuchi) is the fire deity whose birth killed Izanami, ending the age of pure creation. Though slain in rage by Izanagi, his blood and body parts gave birth to many additional kami, especially those associated with mountains, metal, and volcanic phenomena. His birth and death represent the dangerous power of transformative forces.

Role: God of fire

Significance: Cause of first death

Legacy: Parent to mountain and forge deities

💡 Symbolic Interpretations

🌊

Order from Chaos

The stirring of the primordial ocean represents the imposition of divine order upon primal chaos. The formless waters (chaos) are transformed into solid land (order) through divine will and action. This mirrors the universal creation pattern of organizing the unorganized, the movement from potential to actual, from void to form.

Theme: Cosmos emerging from chaos

Symbolism: Spear = divine will, Ocean = potential

♂♀

Sacred Marriage (Hieros Gamos)

The union of Izanagi and Izanami represents the hieros gamos - the sacred marriage of opposites that generates creation. Male and female, heaven and earth, form and essence unite to produce the manifest world. The ritual circumambulation of the pillar enacts the cosmic dance of complementary principles joining to create life.

Archetype: Divine marriage creates the world

Principle: Union of opposites generates reality

⛩️

The World Pillar

The Heavenly Pillar (Ame-no-Mihashira) represents the axis mundi - the cosmic center connecting heaven, earth, and underworld. Found in mythologies worldwide, this world pillar anchors creation, provides cosmic orientation, and allows communication between realms. It is the still point around which all creation revolves.

Universal Motif: Axis mundi, world tree

Function: Cosmic center and connector

💬

Ritual Speech and Cosmic Order

The failed first marriage because Izanami spoke first reveals the importance of ritual propriety and cosmic hierarchy. In ancient Japan, proper order in speech and action was believed to affect the outcome of ritual. The myth validates social structures while teaching that cosmic order must be reflected in human conduct.

Theme: Ritual precision affects reality

Social Dimension: Hierarchy and proper roles

🏛

Japan as Sacred Land

Unlike universal creation myths that explain the entire world, this myth specifically creates Japan, making it a uniquely sacred land - not merely discovered or settled, but born from divine union. Every island is a child of the gods, every mountain a kami's body. This establishes Japan's special status in Shinto cosmology and national identity.

Uniqueness: Creation myth for a specific nation

Implication: Japan as uniquely sacred

🌌

Divine Birth and Creation

The cosmogonic process is explicitly procreative - the world comes into being through birth rather than crafting or speaking. This gynomorphic creation model presents the earth as a divine body and creation as an organic, generative process. Nature is not artifact but offspring, not made but born.

Model: Biological rather than technological creation

Worldview: Nature as living body, not mechanism

🔥

Creation Contains Destruction

The birth of fire that kills Izanami reveals that creation necessarily includes the seeds of destruction. The creative force is also dangerous - fire enables civilization but can destroy life. The very act of bringing forth life creates the conditions for death. This tragic dimension prevents the creation myth from being purely optimistic.

Paradox: Creation leads to death

Wisdom: Light and shadow are inseparable

✨

Spontaneous Divine Generation

The first kami appear spontaneously in Takamagahara, without cause or creator. This suggests that divinity itself is self-originating and eternal. Unlike monotheistic creation from nothing or from a singular creator, Shinto cosmology begins with plural deities emerging from the void, each contributing to subsequent creation.

Concept: Divinity as self-originating

Contrast: Multiple spontaneous origins vs. single creator

📚 Literary and Historical Sources

📖

Kojiki (Records of Ancient Matters)

The Kojiki (712 CE), compiled by Ō no Yasumaro, contains the most complete and poetic version of the creation myth. Its account emphasizes the ritual elements and provides detailed dialogue between the deities. The Kojiki's version has become the standard telling of Japan's creation.

Date: 712 CE

Style: Poetic, detailed, ritual-focused

Status: Oldest Japanese chronicle

📜

Nihon Shoki (Chronicles of Japan)

The Nihon Shoki (720 CE), also called Nihongi, provides alternate versions of the creation myth with variations in detail and emphasis. Compiled as an official imperial history, it sometimes rationalizes or elaborates elements of the Kojiki's account. The multiple variant traditions preserved in the Nihon Shoki show the myth's fluidity.

Date: 720 CE

Style: Historical, multiple variants

Purpose: Official imperial chronicle

⛩️

Shinto Tradition

The creation myth forms the foundation of Shinto belief and practice. It establishes Japan as sacred land, validates the imperial lineage (descended from Amaterasu, who was born from Izanagi), and explains the origins of the kami worshipped at shrines throughout Japan. Many shrines claim connection to events or deities in this myth.

Religious Role: Foundation of Shinto cosmology

Practice: Ritual reenactments, shrine connections

🗺

Geographic Claims

Various locations in Japan claim to be Onogoro Island or other sites from the creation myth. Notable claimants include Awaji Island, Eshima in Lake Biwa, and Onogoroshima Island near Osaka. While the myth's geography is clearly mythological, these associations make the sacred narrative tangible and locally relevant.

Claimants: Awaji, Eshima, various small islands

Function: Connecting myth to physical landscape

🌈

National Identity

The creation myth has profoundly shaped Japanese national identity, especially during periods of nationalist emphasis. The idea of Japan as uniquely divine land (shinkoku), born from the gods themselves, distinguished it from other nations. This belief influenced politics, culture, and Japan's self-understanding, especially during the Meiji period and World War II era.

Political Use: Divine nation concept (kokutai)

Historical Impact: Nationalism, imperial ideology

🎭

Artistic Depictions

The creation of Japan has been depicted in Japanese art for centuries - from ancient shrine illustrations to ukiyo-e woodblock prints to modern manga and anime. The image of Izanagi and Izanami standing on the Floating Bridge of Heaven, stirring the ocean with the jeweled spear, has become iconic in Japanese visual culture.

Art Forms: Paintings, woodblock prints, sculpture

Modern: Manga, anime, popular culture
