The Holy Trinity & Heavenly Host

Christianity centers on the mystery of the Trinity - One God in three persons - alongside the celestial hierarchy of angels and the communion of saints who have achieved union with the divine.

üî∫ The Holy Trinity

The central mystery of Christian faith: God revealed in three distinct yet inseparable persons. Each person of the Trinity represents a different mode of divine presence and action in the world.

God the Father

First Person

Creator of heaven and earth, transcendent source of all being, loving Father who providentially governs creation. Revealed as YHWH in the Old Testament.

Domains: Creation, Providence, Authority, Sovereignty

Symbols: Throne, Crown, Ancient of Days, All-Seeing Eye

Jesus Christ

Second Person - The Son

The Word (Logos) made flesh, God incarnate in human form. Born of the Virgin Mary, died for humanity's sins, rose from the dead, ascended to heaven.

Domains: Redemption, Salvation, Love, Sacrifice

Symbols: Cross, Lamb, Good Shepherd, Sacred Heart, Alpha & Omega

The Holy Spirit

Third Person

The divine presence actively working in the world and in believers. The Comforter, Advocate, and source of spiritual gifts, wisdom, and sanctification.

Domains: Inspiration, Transformation, Gifts, Guidance

Symbols: Dove, Flame, Wind, Water, Oil

üëº Angelic Hierarchy

The celestial beings who serve God as messengers, warriors, and worshippers. Organized into nine orders or choirs according to medieval theology (based on Pseudo-Dionysius the Areopagite).

‚ú® Saints & Holy Ones

The communion of saints - men and women recognized for their extraordinary holiness, many martyred for their faith. Saints serve as intercessors, examples, and patrons of various causes.

Categories of Saints

The Apostles

The Twelve chosen by Jesus, plus Paul. Peter (rock of the church), John (beloved disciple), Paul (apostle to Gentiles), and others who spread the Gospel.

Martyrs

Those who died for their faith. Stephen (first martyr), early Roman martyrs, and countless witnesses who chose death over renouncing Christ.

Church Fathers

Great theologians and bishops: Augustine, Jerome, Athanasius, Gregory, Chrysostom, Ambrose. Shapers of Christian doctrine and practice.

Mystics

Those who experienced direct union with God: Teresa of Avila, John of the Cross, Catherine of Siena, Hildegard of Bingen, Francis of Assisi.

Patron Saints

Saints associated with specific causes: Christopher (travelers), Anthony (lost items), Jude (hopeless causes), Lucy (eyesight), Sebastian (plague protection).

Modern Saints

Recent canonizations: Mother Teresa, John Paul II, Oscar Romero, and contemporary witnesses to faith in challenging times.

üåë Fallen Angels & Demons

The shadow side of the spiritual realm - angels who rebelled against God and now seek to lead humanity astray. Led by Satan (the Adversary), also called Lucifer (Light-Bearer) before his fall.

üêç Satan / Lucifer

The chief adversary, tempter, accuser. Appears as serpent in Eden, adversary in Job, tempter of Christ. Prince of this world, defeated by Christ's resurrection.

üíÄ The Seven Deadly Sins

Pride, Greed, Lust, Envy, Gluttony, Wrath, Sloth. Sometimes personified as demon princes (e.g., Lucifer=Pride, Mammon=Greed).

üî± Legion & Named Demons

Demons cast out by Jesus. Medieval demonology identified hierarchies: Beelzebub (lord of flies), Asmodeus (lust), Belial (worthlessness), Azazel (scapegoat).

üêâ The Antichrist

Eschatological figure opposing Christ. The Beast of Revelation, false prophet, embodiment of deception who will appear before the Second Coming.
