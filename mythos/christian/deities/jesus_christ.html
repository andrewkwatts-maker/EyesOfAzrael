Jesus Christ (Yeshua Ha-Mashiach)

The Messiah, Son of God, Second Person of the Trinity

  • Titles: The Christ, Son of Man, Son of God, Emmanuel, The Word (Logos), Lamb of God, King of Kings, Lord of Lords
  • Role: Savior of humanity, incarnate God, High Priest, Judge
  • Domain: Salvation, Redemption, Resurrection
  • Symbols: Cross, Ichthys (Fish), Lamb, Chi-Rho
  • Earthly Life: c. 4 BCE - c. 30-33 CE

Nature and Role

Jesus Christ is the central figure of Christianity, believed to be the divine Son of God and the promised Messiah of the Old Testament. According to Christian doctrine, Jesus is fully divine and fully human, the second person of the Holy Trinity. Through his life, death, and resurrection, he offers salvation and reconciliation between humanity and God.

Biography

Birth and Early Life

Jesus was born in Bethlehem to the Virgin Mary, conceived by the power of the Holy Spirit. His earthly father was Joseph, a carpenter from Nazareth. The Archangel Gabriel announced his coming to Mary, proclaiming him as Emmanuelâ€”"God with us."

Ministry

At approximately thirty years of age, Jesus began his public ministry after being baptized by John the Baptist in the Jordan River. He gathered twelve disciples, including Peter, John, and Judas Iscariot. His ministry was marked by miraculous healings, exorcisms, the raising of the dead, and teachings about the Kingdom of God through parables and the Sermon on the Mount.

Passion, Death, and Resurrection

Jesus was betrayed by Judas, arrested by the Jewish authorities, and tried before Pontius Pilate. He was condemned to death by crucifixion, the Roman method of execution. According to the Gospels, Jesus died on the cross, was buried, and rose from the dead on the third dayâ€”Easter Sunday. After appearing to his disciples for forty days, he ascended into Heaven, where he sits at the right hand of God the Father.

Theological Significance

Jesus' death is understood as the ultimate sacrifice for humanity's sins, fulfilling the Old Testament system of blood sacrifice. His resurrection demonstrates victory over death and sin, offering believers eternal life. The concept of graceâ€” unmerited divine favorâ€”is central to understanding Christ's redemptive work.
