What is Apokatastasis?

Apokatastasis (·ºÄœÄŒøŒ∫Œ±œÑŒ¨œÉœÑŒ±œÉŒπœÇ) is a Greek word meaning "restoration," "reestablishment," or "return to the original state." In Christian theology, it refers to the doctrine that all of creation - including all souls - will ultimately be reconciled to God and restored to their original perfection. This teaching affirms that God's love and mercy are so infinite that they will eventually overcome all evil, suffering, and separation from the divine.

The doctrine was prominently taught by several early Church Fathers, most notably Origen of Alexandria and Gregory of Nyssa, who believed that God's redemptive work would continue even beyond death until all souls were purified and restored.

üïäÔ∏è Origen of Alexandria (c. 184-253)

The most famous proponent of Apokatastasis, Origen taught that God's love would ultimately transform all souls, even the devil himself. He believed that hell was not eternal but served a purgative, educational purpose to restore souls to God. His work De Principiis (On First Principles) extensively develops this theology.

"The destruction of the last enemy must be understood in this way, not that its substance which was made by God shall perish, but that the hostile purpose and will which proceeded not from God but from itself will come to an end." ‚Äî Origen

üåü Gregory of Nyssa (c. 335-395)

One of the Cappadocian Fathers and a Doctor of the Church, Gregory affirmed that all will eventually be purified and restored to God. He taught that evil has no eternal existence and that God's goodness would ultimately triumph over all evil.

"Being good, God entertains pity for fallen humanity; and being wise, He is not ignorant of the method of recovery." ‚Äî Gregory of Nyssa

üìö Clement of Alexandria (c. 150-215)

Clement emphasized God's pedagogical discipline leading all to salvation. He taught that punishment serves a corrective purpose, not retribution, and that God's love would eventually win over all souls.

üí´ Isaac the Syrian (7th century)

Isaac proclaimed God's boundless mercy that extends to all creation, including demons. He taught that hell is the suffering of those who resist God's love, but that eventually all will be unable to resist the transformative power of divine love.

"I say that those who are suffering in hell are suffering in being scourged by love." ‚Äî Isaac the Syrian

üî• The Nature of Divine Judgment

Purification, Not Punishment

In the Apokatastasis framework, divine judgment is not about eternal retribution but about purification and restoration. The "fire" of judgment is the fire of God's love, which burns away all that is not of God, not to destroy but to purify and heal.

This view sees God as a loving physician rather than a wrathful judge, working to heal all souls no matter how long it takes. The goal is always restoration, never abandonment.

‚ôæÔ∏è The Scope of Restoration

All Means All

The Apokatastasis doctrine takes seriously the biblical language of "all":

  • All creation - Not just humans, but all of creation will be restored
  • All people - Every soul will eventually be reconciled to God
  • All things - Heaven and earth, visible and invisible
  • All in all - God will be all in all, with nothing outside His love
