Revelation 12: War in Heaven

A woman clothed with the sun, crowned with 12 stars, gives birth to a male child (Christ). A great red dragon (Satan) with 7 heads and 10 horns tries to devour the child, but the child is caught up to God's throne. War breaks out in heaven; Michael and his angels defeat the dragon, who is cast down to earth.

Key Elements

  • Woman: Israel/God's people giving birth to Messiah (12 stars = 12 tribes)
  • Dragon: "that ancient serpent called the devil, or Satan" (12:9)
  • Male Child: Christ, who will rule nations with iron scepter (Psalm 2)
  • War in Heaven: Michael casts Satan down; "accuser of our brothers thrown down" (12:10)
  • Dragon Pursues Woman: Persecution of God's people; woman given wings, protected in wilderness
  • War on Offspring: Dragon makes war on "those who keep God's commands and hold fast testimony about Jesus" (12:17)

Theological Significance

This chapter unveils the spiritual warfare behind earthly persecution. Satan's defeat in heaven (accomplished through Christ's death and resurrection) leads to intensified attacks on earth. But God protects His people through tribulation.
