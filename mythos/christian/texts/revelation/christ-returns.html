Revelation 19: King of Kings and Lord of Lords

After Babylon's fall, heaven erupts in worship. The marriage supper of the Lamb is announced. Then heaven opens and Christ appears on a white horse as conquering King.

Hallelujah Chorus (19:1-10)

  • "Hallelujah! Salvation and glory and power belong to our God" (19:1)
  • "Let us rejoice and be glad and give him glory! For the wedding of the Lamb has come, and his bride has made herself ready" (19:7)
  • The bride wears fine linen (righteous acts of God's holy people)
  • "Blessed are those who are invited to the wedding supper of the Lamb!" (19:9)

The White Horse Rider (19:11-16)

  • Rider: Faithful and True, with eyes like blazing fire
  • Many crowns on his head; a name written that no one knows but himself
  • Robe dipped in blood; name: Word of God
  • Armies of heaven follow him on white horses, dressed in fine linen
  • Sharp sword from his mouth to strike down the nations
  • Treads the winepress of the fury of the wrath of God Almighty
  • On robe and thigh: KING OF KINGS AND LORD OF LORDS

Defeat of Beast and False Prophet (19:17-21)

  • Angel summons birds: "Come, gather for the great supper of God"
  • Beast and kings of earth gather to wage war against the Rider
  • Beast and false prophet captured, thrown alive into lake of fire
  • Rest killed with sword from the Rider's mouth

Theological Significance

Christ returns not as suffering servant but as conquering King. He defeats all enemies with the word of his mouth. The marriage supper celebrates the union of Christ and His bride (the Church). Justice is finally established.
