Revelation 4:6-8

"In the center, around the throne, were four living creatures, and they were covered with eyes, in front and in back. The first living creature was like a lion, the second was like an ox, the third had a face like a man, the fourth was like a flying eagle. Each of the four living creatures had six wings and was covered with eyes all around, even under its wings. Day and night they never stop saying: 'Holy, holy, holy is the Lord God Almighty, who was, and is, and is to come.'"

The Four Faces

  • Lion: King of wild beasts, strength, royalty
  • Ox: Strongest domestic animal, service, sacrifice
  • Man: Humanity, intelligence, dominion
  • Eagle: King of birds, swiftness, transcendence

Together they represent all creation worshiping God: wild animals, domestic animals, humanity, and birds of the air.

OT Background: Ezekiel 1:5-14

Ezekiel saw four living creatures, each with four faces (man, lion, ox, eagle), four wings, covered with eyes. They moved with wheels full of eyes, representing God's omniscience and omnipresence.

Revelation simplifies: Instead of each creature having four faces, each of the four creatures has one face. The symbolism remains: all creation worships the Creator.

Isaiah 6:2-3

Isaiah saw seraphim with six wings crying "Holy, holy, holy is the LORD Almighty." Revelation's creatures combine the imagery of Ezekiel's cherubim and Isaiah's seraphim.

Symbolism

  • Six Wings: For worship and service
  • Covered with Eyes: All-seeing, complete awareness of God's glory
  • "Holy, holy, holy": Trisagion‚Äîemphasizing God's perfect holiness (Father, Son, Spirit)
  • Never Stop Worshiping: Heaven's primary activity is unceasing worship

Traditional Christian Interpretation

The four creatures have been associated with the four Gospels:

  • Lion: Matthew (Christ as King)
  • Ox: Mark (Christ as Servant)
  • Man: Luke (Christ as Son of Man)
  • Eagle: John (Christ's divinity soaring to heights)
