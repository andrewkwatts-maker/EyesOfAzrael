Introduction

The Book of Daniel is the single most influential Old Testament source for the Book of Revelation. Both are apocalyptic visions revealing God's sovereignty over history, the rise and fall of empires, and the ultimate triumph of God's kingdom. John explicitly draws on Daniel's imagery of beasts, thrones, the Son of Man, and prophetic time periods to show how Jesus fulfills Daniel's visions.

1. The Four Beasts Become One Composite Beast

Daniel 7 Revelation 13

Daniel 7:3-7

"Four great beasts came up out of the sea, each different from the others. The first was like a lion... a second beast, which looked like a bear... another beast, one that looked like a leopard... a fourth beast‚Äîterrifying and frightening and very powerful. It had large iron teeth... It had ten horns."

Revelation 13:1-2

"And I saw a beast coming out of the sea. It had ten horns and seven heads... The beast I saw resembled a leopard, but had feet like those of a bear and a mouth like that of a lion. The dragon gave the beast his power and his throne and great authority."

Theological Connection

Daniel's four beasts represent successive empires: Babylon (lion), Medo-Persia (bear), Greece (leopard), and Rome (fourth beast). Revelation's single beast combines all four, showing that the final evil empire embodies the accumulated wickedness of all previous empires. The beast from the sea is the ultimate anti-God kingdom, empowered by the Dragon (Satan).

2. The Ancient of Days and the Heavenly Throne

Daniel 7 Revelation 4

Daniel 7:9-10

"As I looked, thrones were set in place, and the Ancient of Days took his seat. His clothing was as white as snow; the hair of his head was white like wool. His throne was flaming with fire, and its wheels were all ablaze. A river of fire was flowing, coming out from before him. Thousands upon thousands attended him; ten thousand times ten thousand stood before him."

Revelation 4:2-5

"And there before me was a throne in heaven with someone sitting on it. And the one who sat there had the appearance of jasper and ruby. A rainbow that shone like an emerald encircled the throne. Surrounding the throne were twenty-four other thrones... From the throne came flashes of lightning, rumblings and peals of thunder. In front of the throne, seven lamps were blazing."

Theological Connection

Both visions depict the heavenly throne room where God reigns supreme over all earthly powers. The Ancient of Days in Daniel corresponds to the One seated on the throne in Revelation. The fire, light, and countless worshipers emphasize God's holiness, power, and worthiness to judge.

3. The Son of Man Receives the Kingdom

Daniel 7 Revelation 1, 5, 14

Daniel 7:13-14

"In my vision at night I looked, and there before me was one like a son of man, coming with the clouds of heaven. He approached the Ancient of Days and was led into his presence. He was given authority, glory and sovereign power; all nations and peoples of every language worshiped him. His dominion is an everlasting dominion that will not pass away, and his kingdom is one that will never be destroyed."

Revelation 1:13, 5:6-7, 14:14

"And among the lampstands was someone like a son of man..." (1:13)

"Then I saw a Lamb, looking as if it had been slain, standing at the center of the throne... He went and took the scroll from the right hand of him who sat on the throne." (5:6-7)

"There before me was a white cloud, and seated on the cloud was one like a son of man with a crown of gold on his head." (14:14)

Theological Connection

Jesus is the Son of Man from Daniel 7. He is the divine-human figure who receives eternal dominion from the Ancient of Days. In Revelation, this is depicted as the Lamb taking the sealed scroll (Rev 5) ‚Äî the act of receiving authority over history and judgment. Christ conquers through sacrificial death and is exalted to universal kingship.

4. The Little Horn and the Beast's Blasphemy

Daniel 7 Revelation 13

Daniel 7:8, 20-21, 25

"While I was thinking about the horns, there before me was another horn, a little one... This horn had eyes like the eyes of a human being and a mouth that spoke boastfully... He will speak against the Most High and oppress his holy people and try to change the set times and the laws. The holy people will be delivered into his hands for a time, times and half a time."

Revelation 13:5-7

"The beast was given a mouth to utter proud words and blasphemies and to exercise its authority for forty-two months. It opened its mouth to blaspheme God, and to slander his name and his dwelling place and those who live in heaven. It was given power to wage war against God's holy people and to conquer them. And it was given authority over every tribe, people, language and nation."

Theological Connection

The little horn in Daniel (often interpreted as Antiochus IV Epiphanes) prefigures the beast in Revelation. Both blaspheme God, persecute the saints, and rule for a limited time. Daniel's "time, times, and half a time" (3.5 years) = Revelation's "42 months" (3.5 years). This shows that evil's reign is limited, and God sets the boundaries.

5. The Saints Persevere and Receive the Kingdom

Daniel 7 Revelation 20

Daniel 7:18, 22, 27

"But the holy people of the Most High will receive the kingdom and will possess it forever‚Äîyes, for ever and ever... until the Ancient of Days came and pronounced judgment in favor of the holy people of the Most High, and the time came when they possessed the kingdom... Then the sovereignty, power and greatness of all the kingdoms under heaven will be handed over to the holy people of the Most High."

Revelation 20:4, 6

"I saw thrones on which were seated those who had been given authority to judge. And I saw the souls of those who had been beheaded because of their testimony about Jesus and because of the word of God... They came to life and reigned with Christ a thousand years... They will be priests of God and of Christ and will reign with him for a thousand years."

Theological Connection

In both visions, the faithful saints who endure persecution ultimately share in Christ's kingdom. Daniel promises that the holy people will receive the kingdom; Revelation shows the martyrs reigning with Christ. Suffering is not the final word ‚Äî vindication and glory await God's people.

6. Books Opened in Judgment

Daniel 7 Revelation 20

Daniel 7:10

"The court was seated, and the books were opened."

Revelation 20:12

"And I saw the dead, great and small, standing before the throne, and books were opened. Another book was opened, which is the book of life. The dead were judged according to what they had done as recorded in the books."

Theological Connection

Both visions depict a final judgment scene where books are opened. This imagery emphasizes God's perfect knowledge and righteous judgment. Every deed is recorded; nothing is hidden. The Book of Life determines eternal destiny.

7. Michael the Archangel and the Great Tribulation

Daniel 12 Revelation 12

Daniel 12:1

"At that time Michael, the great prince who protects your people, will arise. There will be a time of distress such as has not happened from the beginning of nations until then. But at that time your people‚Äîeveryone whose name is found written in the book‚Äîwill be delivered."

Revelation 12:7-9

"Then war broke out in heaven. Michael and his angels fought against the dragon, and the dragon and his angels fought back. But he was not strong enough, and they lost their place in heaven. The great dragon was hurled down‚Äîthat ancient serpent called the devil, or Satan, who leads the whole world astray."

Theological Connection

Michael the archangel appears in both texts as the defender of God's people during a time of unprecedented distress. In Daniel, he arises to protect Israel; in Revelation, he casts Satan down from heaven. This cosmic battle results in the persecution of God's people on earth, but ultimate victory belongs to Michael and the saints.

8. Sealed Until the Time of the End

Daniel 12 Revelation 10, 22

Daniel 12:4, 9

"But you, Daniel, roll up and seal the words of the scroll until the time of the end... Go your way, Daniel, because the words are rolled up and sealed until the time of the end."

Revelation 22:10

"Then he told me, 'Do not seal up the words of the prophecy of this scroll, because the time is near.'"

Theological Connection

Daniel was told to seal his vision because the fulfillment was far off. In contrast, John is told NOT to seal Revelation because "the time is near." This indicates that Daniel's sealed visions are now unveiled in Christ. The end times have begun with Jesus' first coming and will culminate in His second coming.

Summary of Daniel-Revelation Connections

  • Beast Kingdoms: Daniel's four beasts merge into Revelation's composite beast
  • Ancient of Days: God on the throne in both visions
  • Son of Man: Jesus receives eternal kingdom from the Father
  • Little Horn/Beast: Blasphemous ruler who persecutes saints for 3.5 years
  • Saints Inherit Kingdom: Martyrs reign with Christ
  • Books Opened: Final judgment scene
  • Michael the Archangel: Defeats the Dragon in cosmic warfare
  • Unsealed Prophecy: What was sealed in Daniel is revealed in Christ
