1. The Four Horsemen

Zechariah 1, 6Revelation 6

Zechariah 1:8; 6:1-7

"During the night I had a vision, and there before me was a man mounted on a red horse. He was standing among the myrtle trees... Behind him were red, brown and white horses... I looked up again, and there before me were four chariots coming out from between two mountains... The first chariot had red horses, the second black, the third white, and the fourth dappled... These are the four spirits of heaven, going out from standing in the presence of the Lord of the whole world."

Revelation 6:1-8

"I watched as the Lamb opened the first of the seven seals... I looked, and there before me was a white horse! Its rider held a bow... Then another horse came out, a fiery red one... Then I looked, and there before me was a black horse! Its rider was holding a pair of scales... I looked, and there before me was a pale horse! Its rider was named Death."

Theological Connection

Zechariah's colored horses patrolling the earth as divine agents become Revelation's four horsemen bringing conquest, war, famine, and death. Both visions show God's sovereignty over world events.

2. Seven Lampstands and Seven Eyes

Zechariah 4Revelation 1-2

Zechariah 4:2, 10

"I see a solid gold lampstand with a bowl at the top and seven lamps on it, with seven channels to the lamps... These seven are the eyes of the LORD, which range throughout the earth."

Revelation 1:12-13, 20

"I turned around to see the voice that was speaking to me. And when I turned I saw seven golden lampstands, and among the lampstands was someone like a son of man... The seven lampstands are the seven churches."

Theological Connection

Zechariah's seven lamps = eyes of the LORD symbolize God's omniscience. In Revelation, the seven lampstands = seven churches, with Christ walking among them. The Church is God's light in the world, empowered by His Spirit.

3. The Two Witnesses / Two Olive Trees

Zechariah 4Revelation 11

Zechariah 4:3, 11-14

"Also there are two olive trees by it, one on the right of the bowl and the other on its left... What are these two olive branches beside the two gold pipes?... These are the two who are anointed to serve the Lord of all the earth."

Revelation 11:3-4

"And I will appoint my two witnesses, and they will prophesy for 1,260 days, clothed in sackcloth. They are the two olive trees and the two lampstands, and they stand before the Lord of the earth."

Theological Connection

Zechariah's two olive trees (likely Joshua the high priest and Zerubbabel the governor) prefigure Revelation's two witnesses who prophesy with power (often identified as Moses and Elijah returned).

4. Measuring the City

Zechariah 2Revelation 11, 21

Zechariah 2:1-2

"Then I looked up, and there before me was a man with a measuring line in his hand. I asked, 'Where are you going?' He answered me, 'To measure Jerusalem, to find out how wide and how long it is.'"

Revelation 11:1; 21:15

"I was given a reed like a measuring rod and was told, 'Go and measure the temple of God and the altar, with its worshipers.'... The angel who talked with me had a measuring rod of gold to measure the city, its gates and its walls."

Theological Connection

Measuring signifies God's protection and ownership. What is measured belongs to God and will be preserved despite persecution.

5. The Pierced One

Zechariah 12Revelation 1

Zechariah 12:10

"And I will pour out on the house of David and the inhabitants of Jerusalem a spirit of grace and supplication. They will look on me, the one they have pierced, and they will mourn for him as one mourns for an only child."

Revelation 1:7

"'Look, he is coming with the clouds,' and 'every eye will see him, even those who pierced him'; and all peoples on earth 'will mourn because of him.' So shall it be! Amen."

Theological Connection

Zechariah prophesied Israel would look on the one they pierced and mourn. Revelation applies this to Christ's return‚Äîall will see the crucified and risen Lord.
