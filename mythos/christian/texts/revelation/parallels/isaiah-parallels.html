Introduction

Isaiah's prophecies of new creation, God's throne room, and the Day of the Lord profoundly shape Revelation's vision of ultimate restoration and judgment.

1. New Heavens and New Earth

Isaiah 65-66Revelation 21

Isaiah 65:17-19

"See, I will create new heavens and a new earth. The former things will not be remembered, nor will they come to mind. But be glad and rejoice forever in what I will create, for I will create Jerusalem to be a delight and its people a joy. I will rejoice over Jerusalem and take delight in my people; the sound of weeping and of crying will be heard in it no more."

Revelation 21:1, 4

"Then I saw a new heaven and a new earth, for the first heaven and the first earth had passed away... He will wipe every tear from their eyes. There will be no more death or mourning or crying or pain, for the old order of things has passed away."

Theological Connection

Isaiah's prophecy of new creation reaches its ultimate fulfillment in Revelation 21. God doesn't merely renovate the old creation but makes all things new. The end of tears, death, and sorrow is central to both visions.

2. The Throne Room Vision

Isaiah 6Revelation 4

Isaiah 6:1-3

"I saw the Lord, high and exalted, seated on a throne; the train of his robe filled the temple. Above him were seraphim, each with six wings... And they were calling to one another: 'Holy, holy, holy is the LORD Almighty; the whole earth is full of his glory.'"

Revelation 4:2-3, 8

"At once I was in the Spirit, and there before me was a throne in heaven with someone sitting on it... Day and night they never stop saying: 'Holy, holy, holy is the Lord God Almighty, who was, and is, and is to come.'"

Theological Connection

Isaiah's vision of God's holiness and glory on the throne is echoed in Revelation 4. The trisagion ("Holy, holy, holy") emphasizes God's perfect holiness and is sung by both seraphim (Isaiah) and the four living creatures (Revelation).

3. No More Tears - God Swallows Up Death

Isaiah 25Revelation 21

Isaiah 25:8

"He will swallow up death forever. The Sovereign LORD will wipe away the tears from all faces; he will remove his people's disgrace from all the earth. The LORD has spoken."

Revelation 21:4

"He will wipe every tear from their eyes. There will be no more death or mourning or crying or pain, for the old order of things has passed away."

Theological Connection

Isaiah's promise that God will destroy death and wipe away tears finds its culmination in the New Jerusalem. Death itself is defeated, cast into the lake of fire (Rev 20:14).

5. Nations Stream to Zion

Isaiah 60, 66Revelation 21-22

Isaiah 60:1-3, 11

"Arise, shine, for your light has come, and the glory of the LORD rises upon you... Nations will come to your light, and kings to the brightness of your dawn... Your gates will always stand open, they will never be shut, day or night, so that people may bring you the wealth of the nations."

Revelation 21:24-26

"The nations will walk by its light, and the kings of the earth will bring their splendor into it. On no day will its gates ever be shut, for there will be no night there. The glory and honor of the nations will be brought into it."

Theological Connection

Isaiah's vision of nations streaming to Zion with their wealth and glory is fulfilled in the New Jerusalem, where the redeemed from all nations bring their treasures. The gates stand open perpetually, welcoming all who enter.

6. No More Sun or Moon

Isaiah 60Revelation 21-22

Isaiah 60:19-20

"The sun will no more be your light by day, nor will the brightness of the moon shine on you, for the LORD will be your everlasting light, and your God will be your glory. Your sun will never set again, and your moon will wane no more; the LORD will be your everlasting light."

Revelation 21:23; 22:5

"The city does not need the sun or the moon to shine on it, for the glory of God gives it light, and the Lamb is its lamp." (21:23)
