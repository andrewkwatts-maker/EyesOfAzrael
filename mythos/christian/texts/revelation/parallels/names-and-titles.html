Introduction: Jesus Bears the Divine Name

One of the most profound testimonies to Christ's deity in Revelation is the direct application of Yahweh's Old Testament titles to Jesus. Names and titles that the prophets reserved exclusively for the LORD God of Israel‚Äî"First and Last," "Alpha and Omega," "King of Kings," "Faithful and True"‚Äîare now ascribed to Christ.

This is not mere poetic language. In the ancient world, a name revealed the nature and authority of a person. When Jesus bears the names of Yahweh, Revelation declares His full divine identity. This page catalogs every major divine title from the Old Testament and shows its fulfillment in Revelation.

1. "I AM" - The Divine Name

Exodus Gospel of John Revelation

Exodus 3:14

"God said to Moses, 'I AM WHO I AM. This is what you are to say to the Israelites: I AM has sent me to you.'"

John 8:58

"'Very truly I tell you,' Jesus answered, 'before Abraham was born, I AM!'"

Revelation 1:8

"'I am the Alpha and the Omega,' says the Lord God, 'who is, and who was, and who is to come, the Almighty.'"

The Eternal, Self-Existent One

Exodus: God reveals His covenant name as "I AM" (YHWH)‚Äîthe eternally self-existent one who needs no other explanation for His being.
John: Jesus claims "I AM" directly, causing the Jews to attempt stoning Him for blasphemy (John 8:59).
Revelation: "Alpha and Omega" (first and last letters of Greek alphabet) = "I AM" expanded‚Äîthe one who encompasses all of existence, beginning and end.

2. "The First and the Last"

Isaiah (Yahweh) Revelation (Jesus)

Isaiah 44:6

"This is what the LORD says‚ÄîIsrael's King and Redeemer, the LORD Almighty: 'I am the first and I am the last; apart from me there is no God.'"

Isaiah 48:12

"Listen to me, Jacob, Israel, whom I have called: I am he; I am the first and I am the last."

Revelation 1:17

"Do not be afraid. I am the First and the Last. I am the Living One; I was dead, and now look, I am alive for ever and ever!"

Revelation 2:8

"These are the words of him who is the First and the Last, who died and came to life again."

Revelation 22:13

"I am the Alpha and the Omega, the First and the Last, the Beginning and the End."

The Title of Unique Deity

Critical observation: In Isaiah, "First and Last" is Yahweh's title, stated alongside "apart from me there is no God." This is an exclusivity claim‚Äîonly the one true God can be first and last.

Revelation's application to Jesus: By calling Jesus "the First and the Last," John declares Him equal with Yahweh. There are not two "first and last" beings‚ÄîJesus IS Yahweh incarnate. The title now includes the resurrection: "I was dead, and now look, I am alive for ever and ever" (Rev 1:18)‚Äîthe eternal one entered death and conquered it.

Theological Significance: Jesus IS Yahweh

The Christological Claim of Revelation

By applying Yahweh's exclusive divine titles to Jesus, Revelation makes the most exalted Christological claim in Scripture. This is not subordinationism, adoptionism, or mere exaltation of a man. This is full identification of Jesus with the God of Israel.

Why This Matters

  • Jewish Monotheism: "Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God, the LORD is one" (Deut 6:4). There is only ONE God.
  • Exclusivity of Divine Titles: Isaiah repeatedly says "I am the LORD, and there is no other" (45:5, 6, 18, 22)
  • Revelation's Solution: Jesus is not a second God‚ÄîHe IS the one God. The titles prove His deity.

The "First and Last" Argument

This is perhaps the strongest case for Christ's deity in Revelation:

  1. Premise 1: Isaiah 44:6 says "I am the first and I am the last; apart from me there is no God."
  2. Premise 2: "First and Last" is an exclusive title‚Äîonly the one true God can be first and last.
  3. Premise 3: Revelation 1:17-18 applies "First and Last" to Jesus: "I am the First and the Last. I am the Living One; I was dead, and now look, I am alive for ever and ever!"
  4. Conclusion: Therefore, Jesus IS the one true God of Isaiah. There are not two "first and last" beings.

The Paradoxes Resolved

Lion and Lamb: Conquering king (Lion of Judah) AND suffering servant (Lamb slain)‚Äîboth Isaiah's prophecies fulfilled in one person.

Root and Offspring: David's creator (Root) AND David's descendant (Offspring)‚Äîfully divine and fully human.

Shepherd and Sheep: The Good Shepherd (John 10:11) who lays down His life as the Lamb, then rises to shepherd forever.

First and Last: Eternal God (First and Last) who enters time, dies, and rises (adding "I was dead" to the eternal title).

Revelation's Answer to "Who Is Jesus?"

He is the Alpha and the Omega, the First and the Last, the Beginning and the End‚Äîthe one who was, and is, and is to come‚Äî the Almighty. He is Yahweh incarnate, bearing every divine name, worthy of the worship reserved for God alone.
