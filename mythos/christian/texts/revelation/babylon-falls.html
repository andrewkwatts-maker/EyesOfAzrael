Revelation 17-18: The Great Harlot

A woman sitting on a scarlet beast, dressed in purple and scarlet, drunk with the blood of the saints. She is "Babylon the Great," the mother of prostitutes and abominations. She represents worldly empire, economic oppression, idolatry, and persecution of God's people.

Chapter 17: Mystery Babylon

  • The great prostitute sits on many waters (peoples, nations, languages)
  • Kings of earth committed adultery with her
  • She sits on the beast with 7 heads (7 hills, 7 kings) and 10 horns
  • Written on her forehead: "BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF PROSTITUTES"
  • The beast will turn on her and destroy her

Chapter 18: Babylon's Fall Lamented

  • "Fallen! Fallen is Babylon the Great!" (18:2)
  • "Come out of her, my people" (18:4)
  • Kings, merchants, and seafarers lament her destruction
  • A mighty angel throws a millstone into the sea: symbol of Babylon's violent end

Interpretation

Historical: Rome, the persecuting empire of John's day

Symbolic: Every oppressive system that exalts itself against God‚Äîeconomic greed, political tyranny, false religion

OT Background: Isaiah 13-14, Jeremiah 50-51 (oracles against historical Babylon), which destroyed Jerusalem and took Israel into exile
