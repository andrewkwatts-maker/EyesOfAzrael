No Longer Jew and Gentile, But One in Christ

The Radical Inclusion That Points to Universal Salvation

The early Church's shocking inclusion of Gentiles - without requiring them to become Jews first - was more than a practical decision. It was a theological earthquake that revealed God's ultimate plan: the complete dissolution of ALL barriers between God and humanity. If the ancient wall between Jew and Gentile could be demolished by Christ, then NO wall can permanently stand. This trajectory leads inevitably to universal salvation - all nations, all peoples, all souls included in God's redemptive embrace.

The Dividing Wall Demolished

"For he himself is our peace, who has made the two groups one and has destroyed the barrier, the dividing wall of hostility, by setting aside in his flesh the law with its commands and regulations. His purpose was to create in himself one new humanity out of the two, thus making peace, and in one body to reconcile both of them to God through the cross, by which he put to death their hostility."

— Ephesians 2:14-16

The Revolutionary Nature of This Teaching

For thousands of years, there was a clear division: Jews (God's chosen people) and Gentiles (everyone else, outside the covenant). This wasn't just ethnic - it was theological. Yet Paul declares this ancient wall DESTROYED:

  • Made the Two Groups ONE: Not separate but equal - completely unified
  • Destroyed the Barrier: Not lowered, not made permeable - obliterated
  • One New Humanity: A completely new category that transcends the old divisions
  • Through the Cross: Christ's death reconciles BOTH groups to God
  • Hostility Put to Death: The ancient antagonism between Jew and Gentile is killed

The Logical Extension

If the most fundamental religious division in human history (Jew vs. Gentile) could be demolished by Christ, what division can't be?

  • Believer vs. Unbeliever? If God broke the Jew/Gentile barrier, this can be broken too
  • Saved vs. Damned? If ancient hostility can be put to death, eternal separation can be too
  • Heaven vs. Hell? If Christ creates one new humanity from two groups, He can unite all groups
  • Any Human Division? Race, class, gender, nationality - all barriers fall before Christ's unity

The inclusion of Gentiles establishes the PRINCIPLE: God's plan is to eliminate ALL barriers and include ALL people. The trajectory is clear - universal inclusion leads to universal salvation.

From Israel's Jealousy to Gentile Inclusion to All Israel's Salvation

The Divine Strategy in Romans 9-11

"I will call them 'my people' who are not my people; and I will call her 'my loved one' who is not my loved one,"

— Romans 9:25 (quoting Hosea 2:23)

Those Who Were NOT God's People Become His People

Paul quotes Hosea to show that God's plan always included those outside the covenant. The "not my people" who become "my people" are:

  • Gentiles: Who had no covenant relationship with God
  • The Distant: Far from God's promises
  • The Excluded: Outside the boundary of God's chosen
  • The Lost: Wandering in spiritual darkness

Yet God declares He will CALL them "my people." This is not based on their merit, their ethnicity, or their choice - it's based entirely on God's sovereign decision to include them.

"Again I ask: Did they stumble so as to fall beyond recovery? Not at all! Rather, because of their transgression, salvation has come to the Gentiles to make Israel envious. But if their transgression means riches for the world, and their loss means riches for the Gentiles, how much greater riches will their full inclusion bring!"

— Romans 11:11-12

The Three-Stage Divine Plan

Paul reveals God's strategic timeline:

  1. Stage 1 - Israel's Partial Hardening: Israel (as a whole) initially rejects Jesus as Messiah
  2. Stage 2 - Gentile Inclusion: Because of Israel's rejection, salvation comes to the Gentiles
  3. Stage 3 - Israel's Jealousy and Full Inclusion: Seeing Gentile blessing, Israel becomes envious and turns to Messiah

This is not a failure of God's plan but the PLAN ITSELF. God uses Israel's temporary hardening to bring salvation to Gentiles, then uses Gentile salvation to provoke Israel to jealousy, resulting in ALL being saved.

"I do not want you to be ignorant of this mystery, brothers and sisters, so that you may not be conceited: Israel has experienced a hardening in part until the full number of the Gentiles has come in, and in this way all Israel will be saved."

— Romans 11:25-26

"All Israel Will Be Saved"

This is one of the clearest statements in Scripture of eventual universal inclusion:

  • "All Israel": Not "some Jews" or "a remnant" but ALL Israel
  • "Will Be Saved": Future certainty, not mere possibility
  • After Gentile Fullness: When the complete number of Gentiles comes in
  • The Mystery: What seemed like failure (Israel's rejection) was actually part of a larger plan for universal salvation

"For God has bound everyone over to disobedience so that he may have mercy on them all."

— Romans 11:32

The Ultimate Conclusion

Paul's argument in Romans 9-11 culminates in this breathtaking declaration: God's purpose in binding ALL (Jews and Gentiles) to disobedience is so that He can show mercy to ALL.

If ALL Israel will be saved, and the FULL NUMBER of Gentiles will come in, and God will have mercy on THEM ALL - then who exactly is left out? The answer: NO ONE.

The Gospel to ALL Nations - The Great Commission

"Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, and teaching them to obey everything I have commanded you. And surely I am with you always, to the very end of the age."

— Matthew 28:19-20

ALL Nations - No Exceptions

Jesus' final command before ascending is to make disciples of ALL NATIONS (Greek: πάντα τὰ ἔθνη, panta ta ethne):

  • Universal Scope: Not some nations, not friendly nations - ALL nations
  • Success Implied: Jesus sends them to accomplish this, implying it will be done
  • His Presence Guaranteed: "I am with you always" - He ensures the mission succeeds
  • Until the End: The mission continues until the age's completion

"And this gospel of the kingdom will be preached in the whole world as a testimony to all nations, and then the end will come."

— Matthew 24:14

"But you will receive power when the Holy Spirit comes on you; and you will be my witnesses in Jerusalem, and in all Judea and Samaria, and to the ends of the earth."

— Acts 1:8

To the Ends of the Earth

The geographical progression is symbolic of complete inclusion:

  • Jerusalem: The Jewish center, the holy city
  • Judea: The Jewish heartland
  • Samaria: The half-Jews, the despised neighbors
  • Ends of the Earth: The Gentile world, all nations, everyone everywhere

This progression from exclusive (Jerusalem) to completely universal (ends of the earth) models God's salvific plan: starting with a particular people but always intended to include ALL.

Peter's Vision - Nothing Unclean

"About noon the following day... Peter went up on the roof to pray. He became hungry and wanted something to eat, and while the meal was being prepared, he fell into a trance. He saw heaven opened and something like a large sheet being let down to earth by its four corners. It contained all kinds of four-footed animals, as well as reptiles and birds. Then a voice told him, 'Get up, Peter. Kill and eat.' 'Surely not, Lord!' Peter replied. 'I have never eaten anything impure or unclean.' The voice spoke to him a second time, 'Do not call anything impure that God has made clean.'"

— Acts 10:9-15

The Radical Redefinition

Peter's vision wasn't just about food - it was about PEOPLE. The lesson:

  • "All Kinds of Animals": Representing all kinds of people, including Gentiles
  • "Impure or Unclean": How Jews viewed Gentiles - ritually and morally unacceptable
  • "God Has Made Clean": God declares NO ONE inherently unclean or unworthy
  • Repeated Three Times: Emphasizing the importance and certainty of this revelation

"Then Peter began to speak: 'I now realize how true it is that God does not show favoritism but accepts from every nation the one who fears him and does what is right.'"

— Acts 10:34-35

No Favoritism - All Nations Accepted

Peter's conclusion is revolutionary for a first-century Jew:

  • God Shows No Favoritism: Not even to His ancient chosen people Israel
  • Every Nation: Not just Israel or covenant peoples - literally every nation
  • Accepts Them: Not tolerates, not reluctantly allows - actively accepts
  • Based on Heart, Not Birth: "Fears him and does what is right" - not ethnic identity

If God accepts people from every nation without favoritism, and if nothing God has made is unclean, then NO human being is beyond God's redemptive reach.

The Jerusalem Council - No Barrier to Gentile Inclusion

"Now then, why do you try to test God by putting on the necks of Gentiles a yoke that neither we nor our ancestors have been able to bear? No! We believe it is through the grace of the Lord Jesus that we are saved, just as they are."

— Acts 15:10-11 (Peter speaking)

The Crucial Decision

The Jerusalem Council (Acts 15) faced a momentous question: Do Gentiles need to become Jews (through circumcision and Law observance) to be saved? The answer was revolutionary:

  • No Law Required: Gentiles don't need to observe Torah to be saved
  • Grace Alone: Salvation is through grace for both Jews and Gentiles
  • Same Means: Jews are saved "just as they are" - both through grace, not works
  • No Barriers: Nothing (not even God's own Law) stands between God and those He calls

"It is my judgment, therefore, that we should not make it difficult for the Gentiles who are turning to God."

— Acts 15:19 (James speaking)

The Principle Established

James' conclusion establishes a crucial principle: Don't make it difficult for people to turn to God.

If the early Church decided not to make it difficult for Gentiles (by requiring circumcision and Law observance), how much more should we not make it difficult by claiming only a tiny percentage will be saved?

The trajectory is clear: Remove barriers, expand inclusion, make the way accessible. This leads inevitably to universal accessibility and ultimately universal salvation.

No Longer Jew and Gentile

"There is neither Jew nor Gentile, neither slave nor free, nor is there male and female, for you are all one in Christ Jesus."

— Galatians 3:28

Paul's declaration abolishes ALL identity categories that divide: ethnic (Jew/Gentile), social (slave/free), and gender (male/female). In Christ, there is ONE unified humanity.

If these fundamental human divisions are abolished in Christ, then ALL divisions will ultimately be abolished - including the division between saved and lost.

Gnostic Universalism - No Boundaries in the Spirit

The Gospel of Thomas - Neither Jew nor Greek

"Jesus said, 'When you make the two one, and when you make the inside like the outside and the outside like the inside, and the above like the below, and when you make the male and the female one and the same... then will you enter the kingdom.'"

The Gospel of Thomas emphasizes the transcendence of all dualities - including the duality between insider and outsider, chosen and unchosen. The kingdom is entered by overcoming division, not by maintaining it.

Valentinian Teaching - All From the Same Source

Valentinian Gnosticism taught that all divine sparks (pneuma) originated from the same source - the Pleroma. Therefore:

  • No Ethnic Preference: The divine spark is not limited to any race or people
  • Universal Distribution: Sparks are scattered throughout all humanity
  • Common Origin: All spiritual seeds come from Sophia, making all humans spiritual siblings
  • Common Destiny: All sparks will return to the Pleroma regardless of earthly identity

The Tripartite Tractate - The Totality Saved

"The entirety agreed about this redemption... The Logos was sent into the world... that he might gather together the entirety... It is not only the release from the dominion of the left ones... but also a release from the dominion of the right ones."

The Tripartite Tractate describes salvation as comprehensive ("the entirety"), transcending not just obvious divisions (left/evil) but even the supposedly righteous divisions (right/good). All are gathered into the unity of the Pleroma.

The Prophetic Vision - All Nations Streaming to God's House

"In the last days the mountain of the LORD's temple will be established as the highest of the mountains; it will be exalted above the hills, and all nations will stream to it. Many peoples will come and say, 'Come, let us go up to the mountain of the LORD, to the temple of the God of Jacob. He will teach us his ways, so that we may walk in his paths.'"

— Isaiah 2:2-3

The Prophet's Vision of Universal Pilgrimage

Isaiah envisions a time when ALL NATIONS will voluntarily stream to God's presence:

  • "All Nations": Not Israel alone, but every people group
  • "Will Stream": Actively moving toward God, not forced
  • "Many Peoples Will Come": Plural - diversity united in worship
  • "Teach Us His Ways": Seeking knowledge and relationship, not compulsion

"Turn to me and be saved, all you ends of the earth; for I am God, and there is no other. By myself I have sworn, my mouth has uttered in all integrity a word that will not be revoked: Before me every knee will bow; by me every tongue will swear."

— Isaiah 45:22-23

"Therefore God exalted him to the highest place and gave him the name that is above every name, that at the name of Jesus every knee should bow, in heaven and on earth and under the earth, and every tongue acknowledge that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father."

— Philippians 2:9-11

Every Knee Bowing - Universal Acknowledgment

Paul applies Isaiah's prophecy to Jesus. The scope is comprehensive:

  • "Every Knee": No exceptions - all will bow
  • "In Heaven, Earth, and Under the Earth": All realms - the living, the dead, the spiritual beings
  • "Every Tongue Acknowledge": Not forced submission but genuine confession
  • "To the Glory of God": The goal is God's glory through universal worship, not universal torture

This is the language of worship and salvation, not of forced submission. When all tongues confess Jesus as Lord "to the glory of God," they are being saved, not damned.

The Trajectory is Clear

From: One man (Abraham) → One family (Israel) → Twelve tribes

To: All Jews → All Gentiles → All nations → All creation → ALL IN ALL

God's plan has always been moving from particular to universal, from exclusive to inclusive, from some to ALL. The inclusion of Gentiles proves the principle: God will not stop expanding His grace until everyone is included.

Modern Application - Radical Inclusion Today

What the Gentile Inclusion Means for Us

The early Church's radical inclusion of Gentiles provides principles for today:

  • No Group Beyond Reach: If ancient pagans could be included, anyone can
  • Remove Barriers: Don't make it difficult for people to turn to God
  • Grace Not Merit: Inclusion is based on God's grace, not human worthiness
  • Expand Vision: Constantly expand our understanding of who's included
  • Trust the Trajectory: God is moving from exclusive to universal - trust that direction

Breaking Down Modern Walls

Just as the Jew/Gentile wall was broken, we should work to break down modern divisions:

  • Religious: Christian/non-Christian, believer/unbeliever - all are God's offspring
  • Racial: All races bear the divine image equally
  • National: No nation is favored; God accepts people from every nation
  • Economic: Neither slave nor free - economic status doesn't determine spiritual worth
  • Gender: Neither male nor female - full equality in Christ
  • Sexual Orientation: All are created in God's image and loved by Him

The Hope This Provides

If Gentile inclusion points to universal salvation, then:

  • Your Atheist Friend: Not beyond God's reach - the wall will come down
  • Other Religions: God is working in all cultures to draw all to truth
  • The Worst Sinner: No one is "too far gone" - grace exceeds all sin
  • Those Who Died Without Hearing: God's justice ensures they'll have opportunity
  • Your Enemies: Will one day be your brothers and sisters in the Pleroma

All Walls Coming Down

"Then I heard every creature in heaven and on earth and under the earth and on the sea, and all that is in them, saying: 'To him who sits on the throne and to the Lamb be praise and honor and glory and power, for ever and ever!'"

— Revelation 5:13

This is the final vision: EVERY CREATURE, in EVERY REALM, offering worship to God and the Lamb. Not some. Not the elect. Not just humans. Every creature.

The wall between Jew and Gentile was just the first to fall. Eventually, all walls fall, all barriers dissolve, and all creation is unified in worship and salvation.
