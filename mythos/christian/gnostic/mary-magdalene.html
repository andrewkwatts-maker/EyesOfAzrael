The Beloved Disciple - Christ's Companion and Teacher

First witness of the Resurrection. Apostle to the Apostles. The one Jesus loved most. Teacher whose authority rivaled Peter's. Leader of early Gnostic communities. For two thousand years, the Church has tried to silence, diminish, and slander her. But the Gnostic gospels preserve the truth: Mary Magdalene was not a repentant prostitute - she was Christ's beloved companion, his spiritual equal, and the bearer of secret teachings the male apostles refused to accept.

üìñ Mary Magdalene in the Canonical Gospels

Even in the heavily edited canonical texts, Mary Magdalene's importance shines through. She appears at the most crucial moments of Jesus's ministry:

Follower and Financial Supporter

Luke 8:1-3: "Jesus traveled about from one town and village to another, proclaiming the good news of the kingdom of God. The Twelve were with him, and also some women who had been cured of evil spirits and diseases: Mary (called Magdalene) from whom seven demons had gone out; Joanna the wife of Chuza, the manager of Herod's household; Susanna; and many others. These women were helping to support them out of their own means."

  • Seven demons: Not moral failing, but spiritual oppression healed by Jesus
  • Financial supporter: She was wealthy enough to fund Jesus's ministry
  • Named first: Among the women, she has primacy
  • Traveled with Jesus: Part of the inner circle, not just occasional follower

Witness of the Crucifixion

All four gospels record that Mary Magdalene was present at the crucifixion, watching Jesus die. When the male disciples fled in fear, the women remained.

  • Matthew 27:55-56: "Many women were there, watching from a distance... Among them were Mary Magdalene..."
  • Mark 15:40-41: "Some women were watching from a distance. Among them were Mary Magdalene..."
  • John 19:25: "Near the cross of Jesus stood his mother, his mother's sister, Mary the wife of Clopas, and Mary Magdalene."
  • Courage and loyalty: She stayed when the Twelve abandoned him

First Witness of the Resurrection

This is the most important fact: In ALL FOUR canonical gospels, Mary Magdalene is the first person to witness the risen Christ.

Gospel Mary Magdalene's Role
Matthew 28:1-10 First at tomb with "the other Mary." Angel announces resurrection. Jesus appears to them first: "Go tell my brothers."
Mark 16:1-9 First at tomb. Jesus "appeared first to Mary Magdalene." She goes to tell disciples.
Luke 24:1-10 Among the women first at tomb. Angels announce resurrection. She tells the apostles (who don't believe her).
John 20:1-18 Goes alone to tomb. First to see empty tomb. Jesus appears to her personally. She announces: "I have seen the Lord!"

The resurrection is the foundational event of Christianity. The person who first witnesses it is "Apostle to the Apostles" - sent by Jesus to announce the good news. That person is Mary Magdalene.

"Jesus said to her, 'Mary!' She turned toward him and cried out in Aramaic, 'Rabboni!' (which means 'Teacher'). Jesus said, 'Do not hold on to me, for I have not yet ascended to the Father. Go instead to my brothers and tell them, "I am ascending to my Father and your Father, to my God and your God."' Mary Magdalene went to the disciples with the news: 'I have seen the Lord!'" ‚Äî John 20:16-18

Why Mary? The Significance

  • Jesus trusted her first: Not Peter, not John - Mary
  • Women's testimony was legally invalid: Jesus deliberately chose to reveal himself to a woman in a culture that didn't accept women's witness
  • She recognized him by voice: Deep intimacy - he only had to say her name
  • Apostolic commission: "Go tell my brothers" - Jesus sends her as apostle
  • Foundation of the faith: Without Mary's testimony, we have no resurrection witnesses

üìú The Gospel of Mary - Her Own Story

Discovered in 1896, the Gospel of Mary (likely written early 2nd century) preserves a tradition of Mary Magdalene as authoritative teacher who received special revelation from Jesus. It also reveals the conflict between her leadership and patriarchal resistance.

The Special Revelation

The gospel opens after the resurrection. The disciples are afraid and uncertain. Mary comforts them:

"Do not weep and do not grieve nor be irresolute, for his grace will be entirely with you and will protect you. But rather, let us praise his greatness, for he has prepared us and made us into men." ‚Äî Gospel of Mary 5:4-6

Peter then asks her: "Sister, we know that the Savior loved you more than the rest of women. Tell us the words of the Savior which you remember - which you know but we do not, nor have we heard them."

The Vision Teaching

Mary recounts a private vision/teaching from Jesus about the soul's ascent through the cosmic powers. She teaches sophisticated Gnostic cosmology:

  • The soul ascends through four powers: Darkness, Desire, Ignorance, Wrath of Death
  • The soul overcomes each by recognizing its divine nature
  • Liberation comes through gnosis (knowledge), not submission
  • The feminine soul is the hero of the journey

‚öîÔ∏è The Conflict with Peter

This is where the Gospel of Mary becomes explosive. After Mary shares her teaching, Andrew and Peter reject it:

"Andrew answered and said to the brethren, 'Say what you wish to say about what she has said. I at least do not believe that the Savior said this. For certainly these teachings are strange ideas.'" ‚Äî Gospel of Mary 10:1-3
"Peter answered and spoke concerning these same things. He questioned them about the Savior: 'Did he really speak with a woman without our knowledge and not openly? Are we to turn about and all listen to her? Did he prefer her to us?'" ‚Äî Gospel of Mary 10:3-5

This is naked patriarchal rage. Peter cannot accept that:

  • Jesus would teach a woman privately
  • The disciples should listen to female teaching
  • Jesus might prefer Mary to the male apostles
  • Women have spiritual authority equal to men

Levi Defends Mary

But not all the male disciples are threatened. Levi (possibly Matthew) defends her:

"Peter, you have always been hot-tempered. Now I see you contending against the woman like the adversaries. But if the Savior made her worthy, who are you indeed to reject her? Surely the Savior knows her very well. That is why he loved her more than us. Rather let us be ashamed and put on the perfect man and acquire him for ourselves as he commanded us, and preach the gospel, not laying down any other rule or other law beyond what the Savior said." ‚Äî Gospel of Mary 10:6-10

Levi makes three devastating points:

  1. "The Savior made her worthy" - Jesus himself chose her
  2. "He loved her more than us" - Explicitly states Jesus's preference
  3. "Who are you to reject her?" - Peter's authority is not absolute

Then they went forth to preach the gospel. The text ends with them accepting Mary's teaching and going out to spread the message.

üíï The Gospel of Philip - The Beloved Companion

The Gospel of Philip contains the most explicit statements about the special relationship between Jesus and Mary Magdalene. The manuscript has tantalizing gaps, but the meaning is clear.

The Three Marys

"There were three who always walked with the Lord: Mary his mother and her sister and Magdalene, the one who was called his companion. His sister and his mother and his companion were each a Mary." ‚Äî Gospel of Philip 32

Mary Magdalene is ranked equally with Jesus's mother and aunt - the three Marys of his inner circle. But she has a special title: Œ∫ŒøŒπŒΩœâŒΩœåœÇ (koin≈çn√≥s) - "companion," "partner," "consort."

The Beloved More Than All

"And the companion of the [Savior is] Mary Magdalene. [But Christ loved] her more than [all] the disciples and used to kiss her [often] on her [mouth]. The rest of [the disciples were offended by it and expressed disapproval]. They said to him, 'Why do you love her more than all of us?'" ‚Äî Gospel of Philip 63:32-64:5

Let's address the brackets: The manuscript is damaged, but scholars have reconstructed the likely text:

  • "companion of the [Savior is]" - highly probable reconstruction
  • "[But Christ loved]" - context makes this certain
  • "kiss her [often] on her [mouth]" - could be "forehead" or "cheek," but "mouth" fits the Coptic better
  • The pattern is clear: Special love, physical affection, disciples' jealousy

What Does the Kiss Mean?

Scholars debate whether this is:

  1. Romantic/sexual partnership: Some argue Jesus and Mary were married or romantically involved
  2. Spiritual transmission: In Gnostic practice, the kiss transmits gnosis/spirit (like breath)
  3. Syzygy partnership: Sacred masculine-feminine pairing at spiritual level
  4. Teaching authority: The kiss signifies she receives his teaching directly (mouth to mouth)

The text likely intends multiple meanings: a spiritual partnership that doesn't exclude physical affection, a teaching transmission that involves intimacy, a sacred union that the patriarchal disciples cannot understand or accept.

Jesus's Response to Jealousy

When the disciples complain about Jesus loving Mary more, he responds:

"The Savior answered and said to them, 'Why do I not love you like her? When a blind man and one who sees are both together in darkness, they are no different from one another. When the light comes, then he who sees will see the light, and he who is blind will remain in darkness.'" ‚Äî Gospel of Philip 64:5-9

Jesus doesn't apologize or justify. He states that Mary sees spiritually what the male disciples remain blind to. This is why he loves her more - she understands.

üî• Gospel of Thomas Saying 114 - The Controversy

The Gospel of Thomas ends with a troubling saying that requires careful interpretation:

"Simon Peter said to them, 'Let Mary leave us, for women are not worthy of life.' Jesus said, 'I myself shall lead her in order to make her male, so that she too may become a living spirit resembling you males. For every woman who will make herself male will enter the kingdom of heaven.'" ‚Äî Gospel of Thomas, Saying 114

Patriarchal Reading vs. Gnostic Reading

‚ùå Misogynist Surface Reading

  • Women are inferior to men
  • Women must literally "become male"
  • Femaleness is incompatible with salvation
  • Reinforces patriarchal hierarchy

This reading makes the saying contradict everything else in Gnostic texts about the divine feminine and Mary's authority.

‚úì Gnostic Symbolic Reading

  • Peter represents patriarchal exclusion
  • "Male" = spiritual wholeness, not biological sex
  • Reuniting masculine/feminine within
  • Transcending gender divisions
  • Condemning Peter, defending Mary

This reading fits Gnostic theology of restoring primordial androgyny and condemning archontic binaries.

The Correct Interpretation

Saying 114 is not about biology but about spiritual wholeness:

  • Peter is condemned: His statement "women not worthy" is the patriarchal lie Jesus rejects
  • "Make her male": Restore the divine image that includes both masculine and feminine (Genesis 1:27)
  • Like the male disciples: Who themselves must become "female" (receptive, wise) to enter the kingdom
  • Saying 22: "When you make the male and female into a single one" - gender transcendence
  • The problem is division: Separating masculine/feminine is the archontic trap
  • Mary is the example: She has already achieved this integration - that's why Jesus defends her

‚õ™ The Historical Mary Magdalene - What Happened?

How did the first witness of the resurrection, beloved companion of Jesus, and authoritative teacher become reduced to "repentant prostitute"? This is a story of deliberate patriarchal erasure.

The Big Lie: "Mary Magdalene Was a Prostitute"

There is ZERO biblical evidence that Mary Magdalene was a prostitute. None. Not in canonical gospels, not in Gnostic texts, nowhere.

So where did this come from? In 591 CE, Pope Gregory I gave a sermon conflating three separate women:

  1. Mary Magdalene - follower of Jesus, first resurrection witness
  2. Mary of Bethany - sister of Martha and Lazarus, who anointed Jesus
  3. The "sinful woman" in Luke 7:36-50 (never named, probably a prostitute)

Gregory declared they were all the same person - making Mary Magdalene a reformed prostitute. This conflation stuck for 1400 years until the Catholic Church officially separated them in 1969.

Why the Lie?

This wasn't an innocent mistake. It served patriarchal interests:

  • Discredit her authority: A prostitute can't be an apostle or teacher
  • Sexual slander: Classic patriarchal tactic to silence powerful women
  • Erase competition with Peter: If she's a sinner, Peter wins the authority battle
  • Reinforce male priesthood: Women can be repentant sinners, not ordained leaders
  • Suppress Gnostic texts: If Gospel of Mary shows her authority, call it heresy

The Suppression of Gnostic Christianity

Mary Magdalene's leadership was preserved in Gnostic communities. But when proto-orthodox Christianity gained power:

  • 325 CE - Council of Nicaea: Standardization begins, diversity suppressed
  • Late 4th century: Gnostic texts declared heretical, systematically destroyed
  • Women's ordination ended: Early church had women deacons, priests, bishops - all erased
  • Gospel of Mary hidden: Buried in the Egyptian desert for 1500 years
  • Apostle to Apostles forgotten: Her title removed from liturgy and teaching

Modern Rehabilitation

The truth is being recovered:

  • 1896: Gospel of Mary discovered in Egypt
  • 1945: Nag Hammadi library found, including Gospel of Philip and Thomas
  • 1969: Catholic Church officially separates Mary Magdalene from the "sinful woman"
  • 2016: Pope Francis elevates her feast day to equal the apostles
  • Scholarly consensus: She was a leading disciple, not a prostitute
  • Feminist theology: Reclaiming her as model of women's apostolic authority
