Until He Finds It

The Shepherd Who Never Stops Searching

"What man of you, having a hundred sheep, if he has lost one of them, does not leave the ninety-nine in the open country, and go after the one that is lost, until he finds it?"
— Luke 15:4

The Parable: Three Crucial Words

Word #1: "LEAVE"

The shepherd doesn't delegate the search. He doesn't send servants. He personally leaves the ninety-nine to search for the one. This is Christ descending from the Pleroma, leaving the safety and light of heaven to enter the dark material cosmos where the lost sheep wanders.

Word #2: "GO AFTER"

This is active pursuit, not passive waiting. The shepherd goes into the wilderness, the dangerous places, the dark valleys. God doesn't sit in heaven hoping we'll find our way back. God comes looking. God pursues. God seeks.

Word #3: "UNTIL HE FINDS IT"

This is the guarantee. Not "if he finds it." Not "tries to find it." UNTIL HE FINDS IT. The search doesn't end until the sheep is found. The shepherd doesn't return empty-handed. Success is certain.

The Implication for Universal Salvation

If the shepherd searches UNTIL he finds the one lost sheep...

And humanity has not just ONE lost sheep but MANY...

Then the shepherd will not rest UNTIL ALL ARE FOUND.

Universal salvation isn't just possible—it's guaranteed by the shepherd's promise.

The Shepherd Carries the Sheep Home

"And when he has found it, he lays it on his shoulders, rejoicing."

— Luke 15:5

The Sheep Doesn't Walk Back

Notice: the shepherd doesn't just point the way home and expect the sheep to follow. The sheep is exhausted, wounded, disoriented. The shepherd carries it on his shoulders—the place of honor and safety.

This is GRACE, not works. We don't save ourselves by finding our way back. Christ carries us home. Our awakening (gnosis) makes us willing to be found, but the strength for the journey is His, not ours.

"I am the good shepherd. The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep... I have other sheep that are not of this fold. I must bring them also, and they will listen to my voice. So there will be one flock, one shepherd."

— John 10:11, 16

One Flock, One Shepherd—ALL Gathered

Jesus explicitly says He has "other sheep" beyond the current fold, and He MUST bring them. Not "might" or "hopes to." MUST. This is divine necessity. The Good Shepherd's mission is incomplete until all sheep are gathered into one flock under one shepherd.

Heaven's Joy: The Celebration

"And when he comes home, he calls together his friends and his neighbors, saying to them, 'Rejoice with me, for I have found my sheep that was lost.' Just so, I tell you, there will be more joy in heaven over one sinner who repents than over ninety-nine righteous persons who need no repentance."

— Luke 15:6-7

The Pleroma Incomplete Without Us

The shepherd doesn't celebrate alone—he calls all his friends and neighbors. Heaven itself rejoices when one lost soul is found. Why? Because the divine fullness (Pleroma) is incomplete while any spark remains lost in matter.

Every soul matters. Every spark of divine light has infinite value. The return of even one justifies cosmic celebration. How much more when ALL are finally home?

The Lost Coin: The Feminine Divine Searches Too

"Or what woman, having ten silver coins, if she loses one coin, does not light a lamp and sweep the house and seek diligently until she finds it?"

— Luke 15:8

Jesus pairs the shepherd parable with a woman searching for a lost coin. The feminine divine (Sophia, the Holy Spirit) also seeks the lost—lighting a lamp (revelation), sweeping the house (thorough search), seeking diligently UNTIL she finds it.

Both masculine and feminine aspects of God are involved in the search. All of heaven is committed to finding every lost soul. The search will not cease until all are found.
