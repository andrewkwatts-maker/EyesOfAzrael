What Kind of Mourning?

This beatitude is paradoxical: How can mourners be blessed? The mourning here is primarily spiritual mourning:

  1. Mourning over sin - Godly grief over our own sinfulness (2 Corinthians 7:10)
  2. Mourning over the brokenness of the world - Grieving the effects of sin in the world
  3. Mourning in suffering - The tears of those who suffer in this fallen world

This mourning is the natural response of the "poor in spirit" (Beatitude 1) who recognize their spiritual poverty and the world's brokenness.

The Divine Passive

The verb is passive: they will be comforted (by God). God himself is the comforter. This echoes Isaiah's prophecy:

Isaiah 61:1-3 - "The Spirit of the Sovereign LORD is on me... to comfort all who mourn, and provide for those who grieve in Zionâ€”to bestow on them a crown of beauty instead of ashes, the oil of joy instead of mourning, and a garment of praise instead of a spirit of despair."

God promises to turn mourning into joy, ashes into beauty. The comfort comes both now (the Holy Spirit's comfort) and in the future (the consummation of the kingdom when "He will wipe every tear from their eyes" - Revelation 21:4).

ğŸ› ï¸ Practical Application

Three Types of Godly Mourning

1. Mourning Over Personal Sin

  • 2 Corinthians 7:10 - "Godly sorrow brings repentance that leads to salvation and leaves no regret, but worldly sorrow brings death."
  • This is the grief that leads to repentance - being genuinely sorrowful over sin, not just its consequences.
  • Example: Peter wept bitterly after denying Jesus (Luke 22:62)

2. Mourning Over the World's Brokenness

  • Psalm 119:136 - "Streams of tears flow from my eyes, for your law is not obeyed."
  • The godly grieve over injustice, suffering, unbelief, and the effects of sin in the world.
  • Example: Nehemiah wept over Jerusalem's ruins (Nehemiah 1:4)

3. Mourning in Suffering

  • Grief over loss, pain, death - the natural sorrows of life in a fallen world.
  • God does not condemn tears; he promises to wipe them away (Revelation 21:4).
  • Example: Jesus wept at Lazarus' tomb, even though he was about to raise him (John 11:35)
