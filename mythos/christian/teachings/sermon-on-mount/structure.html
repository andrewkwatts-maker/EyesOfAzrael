üèõÔ∏è The Three-Part Architecture

The Sermon on the Mount is masterfully structured in three movements: Character (who we are) ‚Üí Conduct (how we live) ‚Üí Wisdom (how we discern)

üìã Overview of Structure

  1. PART I: THE BEATITUDES (Matthew 5:3-12) - Kingdom Character
  2. PART II: RIGHTEOUSNESS THAT EXCEEDS (Matthew 5:13-6:34) - Kingdom Ethics
    • Salt & Light (5:13-16)
    • Fulfilling the Law (5:17-20)
    • Six Antitheses (5:21-48)
    • Three Piety Practices (6:1-18)
    • Single-Minded Devotion (6:19-34)
  3. PART III: KINGDOM WISDOM (Matthew 7:1-27) - Kingdom Discernment

üíé PART I: The Beatitudes (5:3-12)

Kingdom Character - Who We Are

The Beatitudes (from Latin beatus, "blessed") describe the character of citizens of God's kingdom. They are not moral achievements but descriptions of those whom God blesses - revolutionary reversals of worldly values.

5:3 - Blessed are the poor in spirit ‚Üí theirs is the kingdom of heaven
5:4 - Blessed are those who mourn ‚Üí they will be comforted
5:5 - Blessed are the meek ‚Üí they will inherit the earth
5:6 - Blessed are those who hunger/thirst for righteousness ‚Üí they will be filled
5:7 - Blessed are the merciful ‚Üí they will receive mercy
5:8 - Blessed are the pure in heart ‚Üí they will see God
5:9 - Blessed are the peacemakers ‚Üí they will be called children of God
5:10-12 - Blessed are the persecuted ‚Üí theirs is the kingdom of heaven

Each beatitude has two parts: a description (poor in spirit) and a promise (kingdom of heaven). Together they paint a portrait of Jesus himself.

‚öñÔ∏è PART II: Righteousness That Exceeds (5:13-6:34)

Kingdom Conduct - How We Live

Matthew 5:20 - "Unless your righteousness exceeds that of the Pharisees and teachers of the law, you will certainly not enter the kingdom of heaven."

A. Identity & Mission (5:13-16)

  • 5:13 - Salt of the earth (preserving, flavoring)
  • 5:14-16 - Light of the world (illuminating, revealing)

B. Fulfillment of the Law (5:17-20)

  • 5:17 - "I have not come to abolish but to fulfill"
  • 5:18-19 - Not the smallest letter will pass from the Law
  • 5:20 - Righteousness must exceed Pharisees

C. Six Antitheses: "You Have Heard... But I Say" (5:21-48)

Jesus radicalizes the Law by addressing the heart, not just external behavior:

5:21-26 - Anger = murder in the heart
5:27-30 - Lust = adultery in the heart
5:31-32 - Divorce - God's original intent for marriage
5:33-37 - Oaths - let your yes be yes
5:38-42 - Non-retaliation - turn the other cheek
5:43-48 - Love enemies - be perfect as Father is perfect

D. Three Acts of Piety (6:1-18)

"Be careful not to practice your righteousness in front of others to be seen by them" (6:1)

  • 6:2-4 - Giving to the needy (in secret)
  • 6:5-15 - Prayer (The Lord's Prayer)
  • 6:16-18 - Fasting (in secret)

E. Single-Minded Devotion to God (6:19-34)

  • 6:19-21 - Treasures in heaven
  • 6:22-23 - The eye is the lamp of the body
  • 6:24 - Cannot serve God and money
  • 6:25-34 - Do not worry - seek first the kingdom

ü¶â PART III: Kingdom Wisdom (7:1-27)

Kingdom Discernment - How We Judge

The final section gives practical wisdom for living out the kingdom ethics:

7:1-6 - Judge not (log in your eye, pearls before swine)
7:7-12 - Ask, seek, knock ‚Üí Golden Rule
7:13-14 - Two ways: narrow gate to life, wide road to destruction
7:15-20 - False prophets - know them by their fruits
7:21-23 - True disciples - not everyone who says "Lord, Lord"
7:24-27 - Wise & foolish builders - house on rock vs. sand

Conclusion: Response (7:28-29)

"When Jesus had finished saying these things, the crowds were amazed at his teaching, because he taught as one who had authority, and not as their teachers of the law."

Jesus teaches with divine authority - the authority of God himself. This is not the opinion of a rabbi; it is the word of the Lord.

üå≥ The Progression: From Being to Doing to Discerning

The Sermon's Logic

  1. WHO WE ARE (Beatitudes) - God transforms our character through grace. We become poor in spirit, meek, merciful, pure in heart.
  2. HOW WE LIVE (Righteousness) - From transformed character flows transformed conduct. Our righteousness exceeds external law-keeping and reaches the heart.
  3. HOW WE DISCERN (Wisdom) - Wisdom teachings help us navigate the Christian life: avoid hypocrisy, trust God, choose the narrow way, build on the rock.

The structure reveals that ethics flow from identity. We don't do to become; we become and therefore do.
