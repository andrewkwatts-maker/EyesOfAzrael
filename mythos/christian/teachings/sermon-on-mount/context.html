üèîÔ∏è The Mountain Setting

Why the Mountain?

The mountain is thick with Old Testament symbolism. Mountains are where God meets humanity:

  • Mount Ararat - Where Noah's ark came to rest (Genesis 8:4)
  • Mount Moriah - Where Abraham offered Isaac (Genesis 22), later site of Solomon's Temple
  • Mount Sinai - Where Moses received the Law (Exodus 19-20)
  • Mount Carmel - Where Elijah confronted the prophets of Baal (1 Kings 18)
  • Mount Zion - God's holy mountain, Jerusalem (Psalm 48:1-2)

By ascending the mountain, Jesus evokes Moses and positions himself as the new lawgiver, delivering God's authoritative word.

üìñ Old Testament Prophecy

Deuteronomy 18:15 - Moses prophesied: "The LORD your God will raise up for you a prophet like me from among you, from your fellow Israelites. You must listen to him."

Jesus is the ultimate fulfillment of this prophecy - the prophet greater than Moses, who not only delivers God's word but IS the Word made flesh (John 1:14).

üë• The Audience

Who Was Jesus Teaching?

Matthew 5:1-2 - "When Jesus saw the crowds, he went up on a mountainside and sat down. His disciples came to him, and he began to teach them."

Primary Audience: The Disciples

Jesus specifically teaches "his disciples" - those who have chosen to follow him. The sermon is kingdom ethics for those entering God's kingdom, not a moral code for the unregenerate world.

Secondary Audience: The Crowds

Matthew 7:28 - "When Jesus had finished saying these things, the crowds were amazed at his teaching."

Though directed to disciples, the crowds overhear and are astonished. The sermon is an invitation to join God's kingdom.

Tertiary Audience: All Who Read

Through Matthew's Gospel, the sermon has been preserved for the church throughout all ages. We are the audience today.

ü™ë Jesus Sat Down

The Posture of Authority

Matthew 5:1 - "He went up on a mountainside and sat down."

In Jewish culture, rabbis would stand to read Scripture but sit to teach with authority. By sitting, Jesus assumes the posture of the authoritative teacher:

  • Luke 4:20 - After reading Isaiah in Nazareth, Jesus "sat down" to teach
  • Acts 2:30 - Peter speaks of Christ sitting on David's throne
  • Hebrews 1:3 - After purifying sins, Christ "sat down at the right hand of the Majesty in heaven"

Sitting indicates finished work and established authority. Jesus teaches as one who has the right to interpret God's law definitively.

üìç Where Was This Mountain?

Traditional Location

The traditional site is the Mount of Beatitudes (Hebrew: Har HaOsher), a hill on the northwestern shore of the Sea of Galilee, near Capernaum. A church commemorating the sermon was built there in 1938.

But Does It Matter?

The exact location is less important than the theological significance. Matthew emphasizes the mountain as the place of divine revelation, echoing Sinai. Whether the exact hill, or a symbolic reference to Jesus' authoritative teaching position, the mountain establishes Jesus as the new Moses.

‚è∞ When in Jesus' Ministry?

Early in the Galilean Ministry

The Sermon on the Mount occurs early in Jesus' public ministry in Galilee, after:

  • Matthew 4:1-11 - Jesus' temptation in the wilderness
  • Matthew 4:12-17 - Jesus begins preaching in Galilee: "Repent, for the kingdom of heaven has come near"
  • Matthew 4:18-22 - Jesus calls his first disciples (Peter, Andrew, James, John)
  • Matthew 4:23-25 - Jesus' ministry of teaching, preaching, and healing spreads; crowds follow him

The sermon functions as Jesus' inaugural address - the manifesto of the kingdom he is proclaiming.
