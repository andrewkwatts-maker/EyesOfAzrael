The First Apostolic Martyr

Son of Zebedee, brother of John, member of Christ's inner circle. The first apostle martyred, beheaded by Herod Agrippa in 44 CE.

James the Greater, Son of Zebedee

  • Name: James (Hebrew: Ya'akov/Jacob)
  • Title: "The Greater" (to distinguish from James son of Alphaeus)
  • Family: Son of Zebedee, brother of John; mother possibly Salome
  • Occupation: Fisherman on the Sea of Galilee
  • Nickname: "Boanerges" (Son of Thunder) with his brother John
  • Symbol: Sword, scallop shell, pilgrim's staff
  • Feast Day: July 25
  • Patronage: Spain, pilgrims, soldiers
  • Martyrdom: Beheaded by Herod Agrippa I in Jerusalem, 44 CE

Biography

James the Greater was one of the first disciples called by Jesus and a member of His inner circle. The son of Zebedee and brother of John, James shared the fiery temperament that earned them the nickname "Sons of Thunder." He witnessed the Transfiguration, the raising of Jairus's daughter, and Jesus' agony in Gethsemane. His zeal for Christ led him to become the first apostle to be martyred. Tradition holds that he preached in Spain before returning to Jerusalem for his martyrdom.
