Creation Myths

This section is currently under development

Creation Myths represent some of humanity's most profound attempts to answer the ultimate question: How did everything begin? These narratives explore the emergence of the cosmos, the birth of gods, and the origins of humanity itself.

Across cultures, creation myths share common themes: order emerging from chaos, divine craftsmen shaping reality, cosmic eggs hatching the universe, or primordial beings sacrificed to form the world. Whether it's the Babylonian Enuma Elish, the Genesis creation, the Norse Ginnungagap, or the Chinese Pangu myth, these stories provide foundational worldviews and cosmological frameworks.
