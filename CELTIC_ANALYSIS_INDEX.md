## Deity Evidence at a Glance

### Quick Lookup by Deity

#### Aengus Óg
- **Tier**: 3 (Moderate)
- **Primary Evidence**: Medieval Irish texts
- **Key Source**: Wooing of Etain
- **Main Manuscripts**: Book of Leinster, Book of Ballymote
- **Continental**: Minimal
- **Confidence**: Moderate (good insular documentation)

#### Brigid
- **Tier**: 2 (Strong)
- **Primary Evidence**: Medieval texts + Christian syncretism + festival
- **Key Source**: Life of Saint Brigid, Imbolc continuity
- **Syncretism**: Goddess → Saint Brigid
- **Continental**: Possible (Minerva parallels)
- **Confidence**: High (multiple evidence types)

#### Cernunnos
- **Tier**: 4 (Archaeological)
- **Primary Evidence**: Gundestrup Cauldron, coins, art
- **Key Artifact**: Gundestrup Cauldron (1st c. BCE)
- **Insular**: Weak textual evidence
- **Continental**: Strong archaeological
- **Confidence**: High (archaeology), Low (insular texts)

#### Dagda
- **Tier**: 2 (Strong)
- **Primary Evidence**: Multiple medieval manuscripts
- **Key Texts**: Lebor Gabála Érenn, Cath Maige Tuired
- **Role**: Central to Tuatha Dé Danann
- **Continental**: Minimal
- **Confidence**: High (extensive insular texts)

#### Danu
- **Tier**: 5 (Etymological)
- **Primary Evidence**: Etymology + genealogy
- **Key Source**: Lebor Gabála Érenn (mother goddess)
- **Linguistic**: Danube river connection
- **Narrative**: Very limited
- **Confidence**: Moderate (etymology + brief texts)

#### Lugh
- **Tier**: 1 (Highest)
- **Primary Evidence**: Multiple independent sources
- **Key Sources**: Place names (Lugdunum), festival (Lughnasadh), texts, inscriptions
- **Archaeological**: Coins, dedications
- **Roman**: Mercury identification
- **Confidence**: Highest (continental + insular + roman)

#### Manannan mac Lir
- **Tier**: 3 (Moderate)
- **Primary Evidence**: Irish + Welsh texts (shared tradition)
- **Key Texts**: Immram Brain, Echtra Connla, Mabinogion
- **Unique**: Documented in both Irish and Welsh traditions
- **Continuous**: Otherworld guide and sea god
- **Confidence**: Moderate-High (parallel traditions)

#### Morrigan
- **Tier**: 2 (Strong)
- **Primary Evidence**: Multiple medieval texts
- **Key Texts**: Cath Maige Tuired, Longes mac nUisneach
- **Role**: War and fate goddess, shape-shifter
- **Continuity**: Consistent characterization
- **Confidence**: High (consistent insular tradition)

#### Nuada Airgetlám
- **Tier**: 3 (Moderate)
- **Primary Evidence**: Medieval texts + kingship theology
- **Key Texts**: Cath Maige Tuired, Baile in Scáil
- **Unique**: Illustrates "blemish on kingship" concept
- **Role**: King of Tuatha Dé Danann, healing themes
- **Confidence**: Moderate (good textual basis, theological interest)

#### Ogma
- **Tier**: 1 (Highest)
- **Primary Evidence**: Lucian's explicit account + texts + inscriptions
- **Key Source**: Lucian of Samosata's Hercules (2nd c. CE)
- **Gaulish**: Direct inscriptions to Ogmios
- **Medieval**: Ogham script connections
- **Confidence**: Highest (explicit Roman + Gaulish + insular)

## Evidence Categories

### Continental Evidence (Hallstatt & La Tene, 800-50 BCE)

**Archaeological**:
- Gundestrup Cauldron (Cernunnos)
- Celtic coins with deity imagery
- Carved religious figures
- Temple structures and sanctuaries

**Epigraphic**:
- Gaulish inscriptions and dedications
- Bilingual Latin-Gaulish texts
- Place names (Lugdunum, etc.)

**Documentary**:
- Caesar (Commentarii de Bello Gallico)
- Lucian (explicit Ogma/Ogmios account)
- Strabo (geographic descriptions)
- Other Roman authors

### Insular Evidence (Medieval, 8th-14th centuries CE)

**Manuscript Witnesses**:
- Book of Leinster (12th c.)
- Yellow Book of Lecan (14th c.)
- Book of Ballymote (11th c.)
- White Book of Rhydderch (13th c., Welsh)
- Red Book of Hergest (12th c., Welsh)

**Text Types**:
- Lebor Gabála Érenn (Book of Invasions)
- Cath Maige Tuired (Battle narratives)
- Mabinogion (Welsh mythology)
- Metrical Dindshenchas (place lore)
- Various Ulster Cycle texts

### Linguistic Evidence

**Etymology**:
- Deity names and meanings
- Proto-Indo-European reconstructions
- Language family connections

**Place Names**:
- Lugdunum (Lyon) = Sacred to Lugus
- Danube possibly from Danu root
- Geographic cult distribution

## Timeline of Evidence Preservation

```
Pre-Christian Era (before 5th century)
    ↓ Oral tradition, no written record

Monastic Period (5th-7th century)
    ↓ Early monastery establishment, fragmentary records

Compilation Period (8th-10th century)
    ↓ Systematic collection of traditions

Manuscript Production (8th-14th century)
    ↓ Peak manuscript creation

Roman Period Parallels (50 BCE - 400 CE)
    ↓ Ongoing during European dark ages
    ↓ Gaulish religion documented through Rome

Modern Scholarship (1800s-present)
    ↓ Academic analysis and interpretation

Contemporary Understanding
```
