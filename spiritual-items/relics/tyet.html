Tyet - Knot of Isis

The Blood of Isis, Amulet of Protection and Resurrection

Protection in the afterlife

Description and Appearance

The Tyet, also known as the Knot of Isis or the Blood of Isis, is an ancient Egyptian amulet closely associated with the goddess Isis and her powers of protection, healing, and resurrection. Resembling the ankh but with its arms folded down, the tyet was one of the most important funerary amulets in ancient Egypt.

The amulet was traditionally made from red materials - jasper, carnelian, or red glass - representing the magical blood of Isis. It was placed on the throat or chest of mummies to invoke the protection of Isis in the journey through the underworld.

Symbolic Meanings:

  • Blood of Isis: The life force and magical power of the goddess
  • Protection: Guarding the deceased in the afterlife
  • Resurrection: Isis's power to restore life (as she did for Osiris)
  • Feminine Power: Connection to the womb and motherhood
  • Binding Magic: The knot that binds and protects

The Book of the Dead

Chapter 156 of the Book of the Dead specifically concerns the tyet amulet, providing instructions for its preparation and the spell to be recited:

"You have your blood, Isis. You have your power, Isis. You have your magic, Isis. The amulet is a protection for this Great One; it will drive away whoever would commit a crime against him."

The text specifies that the amulet should be made of red jasper and placed on the deceased's neck, ensuring that the person would have the protection of Isis's blood and magical power throughout their journey through the Duat (underworld).
