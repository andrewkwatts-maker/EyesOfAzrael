Gae Bolg

The Barbed Spear of Cu Chulainn, the Belly Spear of Death

Celtic Irish Ulster Cycle Heroic
Type
Magical Spear / Barbed Javelin
Owner
Cu Chulainn, Hound of Ulster
Origin
Made from bones of sea monster Coinchenn
Special Attack
Thrown with the foot, creates 30 barbs inside victim

Description and Nature

The Gae Bolg (also spelled Gae Bulg or Gae Bulga) is the legendary spear of Cu Chulainn, Ireland's greatest mythological hero. This terrible weapon, made from the bones of a sea monster, inflicted wounds that were invariably fatal. Its name translates variously as "belly spear," "spear of mortal pain," or "barbed spear," all indicating its gruesome lethality.

What made the Gae Bolg unique was its method of attack and its aftermath. Upon entering a victim's body, the single point would split into thirty barbs, each finding a different path through the flesh. The wound was impossible to heal without cutting the body open, and removal meant certain death if the initial strike had not already proven fatal.

Characteristics:

  • Unique Casting: Thrown with the foot using a special technique called the "throw of the brave"
  • Barbed Head: Single point that splits into 30 barbs upon entering the body
  • Guaranteed Kill: No one struck by it ever survived
  • Monster Origin: Fashioned from the bones of the sea beast Coinchenn
  • Training Required: Only Cu Chulainn knew the proper technique to wield it

Origin and Training

Gift of Scathach

Cu Chulainn received the Gae Bolg from Scathach, the legendary warrior woman who trained him in Scotland's Isle of Skye (Dun Scaith). Scathach was the greatest martial instructor in the Celtic world, and only her best pupils learned the secrets of the Gae Bolg. The weapon came with the knowledge of a special throwing technique where the spear was set in the stream, picked up by the toes, and cast with a kick of the foot.

The Death of Ferdiad

The most famous use of the Gae Bolg came during the Tain Bo Cuailnge (Cattle Raid of Cooley), when Cu Chulainn was forced to fight his foster-brother Ferdiad. The two had trained together under Scathach and were as close as brothers. But Ferdiad was compelled by Queen Medb of Connacht to fight for her army against Ulster.

For four days the two heroes fought, evenly matched in all the combat arts they had learned together. On the fourth day, Cu Chulainn finally called for the Gae Bolg. He cast it into the water with his foot, and it tore through Ferdiad's defenses, filling his body with barbs. Cu Chulainn held his dying foster-brother and mourned that he had been forced to kill the only man who was his equal.
