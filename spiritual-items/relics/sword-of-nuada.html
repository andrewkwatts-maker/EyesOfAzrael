Description and Nature

The Sword of Nuada, known as Claoimh Solais (the "Sword of Light"), is one of the Four Treasures of the Tuatha De Danann. Brought from the mythical city of Findias when the Tuatha De Danann came to Ireland, this sword was the weapon of their first king, Nuada Airgetlam ("Silver Hand").

The sword's most terrifying property was its inescapability: once the sword was drawn from its sheath, no enemy could flee from it, and no one could resist its deadly stroke. As a blade of pure light, it represented justice and righteous warfare, the legitimate violence of the king in defense of his people.

Powers of the Sword:

  • Inescapable: Once drawn, no enemy could flee from it
  • Irresistible: No blow could be defended against
  • Shining Light: The blade blazed with supernatural luminosity
  • Royal Authority: Symbolized the king's right to judge and punish

Nuada and the Four Treasures

King Nuada

Nuada was the first king of the Tuatha De Danann in Ireland. During the First Battle of Mag Tuired against the Fir Bolg, he lost his hand and had to relinquish kingship, as Celtic law required a king to be physically perfect. The physician-god Dian Cecht fashioned him a silver hand, but he was not restored to kingship until Miach (Dian Cecht's son) gave him a hand of flesh.

The Four Treasures United

Together, the Four Treasures represented complete sovereignty over Ireland:

  • Stone of Fal: Legitimacy - screamed under the true king
  • Sword of Nuada: Justice - from which none could escape
  • Spear of Lugh: Victory - which never lost a battle
  • Cauldron of Dagda: Prosperity - which satisfied all
