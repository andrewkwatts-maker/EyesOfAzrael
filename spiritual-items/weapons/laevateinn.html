Description and Nature

Laevateinn (Old Norse: "Damage Twig" or "Wounding Wand") is one of the most enigmatic weapons in Norse mythology, mentioned only in the Eddic poem Fjolsvinnsmal. This mysterious weapon was forged by Loki (called Lopt in the poem) and is kept locked in a chest guarded by Sinmara, often identified as the wife of the fire giant Surtr.

The exact nature of Laevateinn is debated by scholars. The name suggests either a wand or staff ("damage twig"), but many interpretations and later traditions present it as a sword. Some scholars believe it may be the same weapon as Surtr's flaming sword, which will be used to destroy the world at Ragnarok.

What makes Laevateinn particularly significant is its stated purpose: it is the only weapon capable of killing Vidofnir, the golden rooster that sits atop the world tree Yggdrasil (or, in some interpretations, another cosmic tree called Mimameidr). This specificity suggests the weapon was created for a particular cosmic purpose.

Name Etymology:

  • Laeva: Related to words meaning "damage," "harm," or "deceit"
  • Teinn: Meaning "twig," "wand," or "thin object"
  • Combined: "Damage-twig" or "Wounding wand"
  • Alternative Reading: Some scholars connect it to words for "deceiver" or "trickster," fitting Loki's nature

Source: Fjolsvinnsmal

Laevateinn appears in only one source: the Eddic poem Fjolsvinnsmal (The Lay of Fjolsvinnr), which takes the form of a question-and-answer dialogue. In this poem, the hero Svipdag questions the giant Fjolsvinnr about the mysteries of a fortress and its surroundings.

The relevant passage describes Laevateinn as the only weapon that can kill Vidofnir:

"Laevateinn it is called, and Lopt (Loki) made it, down below the gates of death; In Laegarn's chest by Sinmara it lies, and nine locks hold it firm."

"Tell me, Fjolsvinnr, what I wish to know and want to learn: What must be given to Sinmara to win the shining blade?"

"In your pouch you must bear the bright tail-feather of Vidofnir, and give it to Sinmara's hand."

- Fjolsvinnsmal, stanzas 26-28

This creates a paradox: the only weapon that can kill Vidofnir is locked away and can only be obtained by presenting a feather from Vidofnir. This circular puzzle suggests that either the hero must find another way or that the myth describes an impossible situation meant to illustrate cosmic truths about the interconnectedness of all things.

Scholarly Interpretations

Surtr's Flaming Sword

Some scholars identify Laevateinn with the flaming sword that Surtr will wield at Ragnarok to destroy the gods and set the world on fire. If Sinmara is indeed Surtr's wife, it makes sense that she would guard her husband's weapon. The idea that Loki made this weapon aligns with his role as the instigator of Ragnarok.

Mistletoe Theory

Another interpretation connects Laevateinn to the mistletoe that killed Baldr. Loki fashioned the mistletoe dart that Hodr used to kill the beloved god. The name "damage twig" could describe this small but deadly weapon. This theory positions Laevateinn as an instrument of Loki's betrayal.

Magical Staff

Taking the name literally as "wounding wand," some interpret Laevateinn as a magical staff or scepter rather than a sword. Such an object would fit Loki's sorcerous nature better than a conventional blade and aligns with the tradition of powerful magical implements in Norse mythology.

Symbol of Inevitable Doom

From a mythological perspective, Laevateinn may represent the inevitability of cosmic destruction. Just as Vidofnir cannot be killed without it, the destruction of the world at Ragnarok cannot occur without Loki's machinations. The circular paradox of obtaining the weapon reflects the inescapable nature of fate.

Powers and Attributes

Vidofnir-Slaying The weapon's primary stated purpose is as the only means to kill Vidofnir, the golden rooster. This specificity suggests cosmic significance beyond mere lethality.
Forged Below Death's Gates According to Fjolsvinnsmal, Loki made Laevateinn "down below the gates of death," suggesting infernal origins and possibly underworld power.
Nine Locks Protection The weapon is secured with nine locks - a sacred number in Norse mythology - indicating its tremendous importance and danger.
Loki's Craft As a weapon forged by the trickster god, Laevateinn likely possesses deceptive or unexpected qualities beyond simple destruction.

Modern Depictions

Despite its obscurity in the original sources, Laevateinn has become popular in modern fantasy and gaming:

  • Fate Series: Features Laevateinn as a powerful flaming sword, one of the more prominent modern depictions
  • Final Fantasy: Various entries include Laevateinn as a weapon
  • Shin Megami Tensei: Features the weapon in connection with Norse mythology references
  • Various Anime/Manga: Often depicted as a flaming sword or magical weapon associated with fire and destruction
  • Fantasy Literature: Appears in numerous Norse-inspired fantasy works, usually as a sword of fire

Most modern depictions interpret Laevateinn as a flaming sword, reflecting the connection to Surtr and Ragnarok rather than the "wand" interpretation suggested by the original name.
