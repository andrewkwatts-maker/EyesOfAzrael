Spear of Lugh

The Flaming Spear That Never Missed Its Mark

Celtic Irish Tuatha De Danann Four Treasures
Irish Name
Slea Bua (Spear of Victory)
Also Known As
Gae Assail, Areadbhair
Origin
City of Gorias in the Otherworld
Primary Power
Guaranteed victory, lightning speed

Description and Nature

The Spear of Lugh (Gae Assail or Areadbhair) is one of the Four Treasures of the Tuatha De Danann, the magical race who ruled Ireland in the mythological age. This fiery spear was brought from the city of Gorias by the Tuatha De Danann when they came to Ireland, and it was wielded by Lugh Lamhfada (Lugh of the Long Arm), the master of all skills.

The spear was so bloodthirsty that it had to be kept submerged in a cauldron of water (or sometimes poppy juice) when not in battle, lest it burn down the city around it with its perpetual flame. When Lugh took it to battle, it would flash through the air with the speed of lightning, and no enemy could stand against it.

Magical Properties:

  • Unerring Flight: Never missed its target, returned to thrower's hand
  • Eternal Flame: Burned with perpetual fire that could not be quenched
  • Victory Assurance: No battle could be lost when the spear was used
  • Lightning Speed: Flew faster than any other weapon
  • Blood Hunger: Required restraint when not in battle

Mythology and Legend

Lugh of the Long Arm

Lugh was the greatest warrior of the Tuatha De Danann, a god of light, skill, and victory. Called Lamhfada ("Long Arm") for his prowess with the throwing spear, he was master of every art and craft. When he came to Tara seeking admission to the court, he claimed to practice every skill, and proved it against all challengers.

The Second Battle of Mag Tuired

The spear's most famous use was in the Second Battle of Mag Tuired, where the Tuatha De Danann fought the Fomorians, a race of demonic giants. Lugh led the Tuatha De Danann forces and, wielding his flaming spear, struck down Balor of the Evil Eye, the terrible Fomorian king whose gaze could kill armies. With Balor's death, the Fomorians were broken and driven from Ireland.
