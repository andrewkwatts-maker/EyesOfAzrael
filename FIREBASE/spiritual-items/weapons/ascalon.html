Description and Nature

Ascalon is the name traditionally given to the lance or spear wielded by Saint George in his legendary battle against the dragon. While the weapon is not named in the earliest versions of the Saint George legend, later medieval traditions gave it this name, possibly derived from the city of Ashkelon in the Levant, where George was said to have originated.

The lance represents the archetypal weapon of the Christian knight - the primary arm of a mounted warrior, used to strike the first and often decisive blow in combat. In Saint George's hands, Ascalon became a symbol of faith's power to overcome evil, the physical manifestation of spiritual warfare.

Unlike many legendary weapons that possess magical properties, Ascalon's power in Christian tradition comes not from enchantment but from the righteousness of its wielder and the blessing of God. It is a mortal weapon made sacred through holy purpose.

Symbolic Elements:

  • The Lance: Represents the knight's primary weapon and the Christian soldier's readiness for spiritual battle
  • The Cross Guard: Many depictions show Ascalon with a cruciform guard, emphasizing its holy nature
  • The Piercing: The lance's function of piercing evil parallels Christ's piercing of death and sin

The Legend of Saint George

The Dragon and the Princess

According to the most popular version of the legend (found in Jacobus de Voragine's Golden Legend, c. 1260), a fearsome dragon lived near the city of Silene in Libya (or sometimes Beirut or Cappadocia, depending on the version). The dragon poisoned the countryside and demanded daily sacrifices of sheep to appease its hunger.

When sheep grew scarce, the city began sacrificing humans, selected by lot. One day the lot fell upon the king's daughter. Despite the king's pleas, the princess was led to the dragon's lake to await her fate.

Saint George's Intervention

George, a Roman soldier from Cappadocia and a Christian knight, happened upon the princess awaiting the dragon. Learning of her plight, he resolved to confront the monster. When the dragon emerged, George charged on horseback, making the sign of the cross and commending himself to God.

With Ascalon, George struck the dragon a grievous blow, wounding it severely. He then had the princess tie her girdle around the dragon's neck, and the beast followed them meekly into the city. There, George promised to slay the dragon if the people would convert to Christianity. When they agreed, he killed the beast with his sword, and the king built a church on the spot.

"Saint George the knight made the sign of the cross and commended himself to God. Then he rode against the dragon and struck it so mightily with his lance that he wounded it grievously and cast it to the ground."

- Jacobus de Voragine, The Golden Legend

Symbolism and Significance

Triumph of Faith Over Evil

Ascalon represents the power of Christian faith to overcome evil. The dragon in medieval Christian symbolism often represented Satan, paganism, or sin itself. George's victory with Ascalon thus symbolizes the triumph of the Church over its spiritual enemies.

Chivalric Ideal

The lance of Saint George became the archetypal weapon of the ideal Christian knight - the miles Christi or "soldier of Christ." Ascalon embodies the chivalric virtues of courage, faith, and protection of the innocent that medieval culture celebrated.

National Symbolism

As Saint George became the patron saint of England, Portugal, Georgia, and numerous other nations and cities, his lance became associated with national identity. The image of George with Ascalon appears on coats of arms, flags, and national symbolism throughout Europe.

The Knight's Weapon

The choice of a lance rather than a sword for George's dragon-slaying reflects the medieval understanding of martial hierarchy. The lance was the knight's primary weapon, the one used for the initial charge that decided battles. George's use of Ascalon marks him as a true knight.

Modern Depictions

  • Guild Wars 2: Features Ascalon as a significant location and Ascalonian weapons as a weapon set
  • Various RPGs: Saint George's lance appears as a holy weapon effective against dragons and evil
  • Heraldry: The image of George with his lance remains common in English, Georgian, and other national symbolism
  • Religious Art: Countless paintings, sculptures, and icons depict George with Ascalon piercing the dragon
