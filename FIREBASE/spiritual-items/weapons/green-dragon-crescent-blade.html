Description and Appearance

The Green Dragon Crescent Blade (Chinese: Qinglong Yanyue Dao) is perhaps the most famous weapon in Chinese literature and folklore, the legendary glaive wielded by Guan Yu during the tumultuous Three Kingdoms period (220-280 CE). This massive pole weapon has become an enduring symbol of Chinese martial tradition, loyalty, and righteousness.

The weapon's name derives from its distinctive crescent-shaped blade, said to resemble a dragon's sweeping form. The "Green Dragon" refers both to the azure-green color traditionally associated with the blade and to the dragon imagery that symbolizes power, wisdom, and divine favor in Chinese culture.

According to the Romance of the Three Kingdoms, the blade weighed 82 jin (approximately 41 kilograms or 90 pounds in modern measurements). This extraordinary weight meant that only someone of Guan Yu's legendary strength could effectively wield it in battle - the weapon itself became proof of his superhuman prowess.

Physical Characteristics:

  • Blade: Large crescent-shaped head with a pronounced forward curve, designed for powerful sweeping cuts and thrusts
  • Shaft: Long wooden pole, traditionally made from sturdy hardwood, allowing for both mounted and foot combat
  • Weight: 82 jin - so heavy that ordinary warriors could not even lift it, let alone wield it effectively
  • Dragon Motif: The blade itself was said to be decorated with or spiritually connected to a green dragon spirit
  • Total Length: Approximately 2.8 meters (over 9 feet)

Guan Yu and His Blade

The Historical and Legendary Figure

Guan Yu (died 220 CE) was a general serving under the warlord Liu Bei during the late Eastern Han dynasty and Three Kingdoms period. While historically documented, Guan Yu's legend has grown far beyond historical records through centuries of storytelling, culminating in the 14th-century novel Romance of the Three Kingdoms.

In both history and legend, Guan Yu is remembered as the paragon of loyalty and righteousness. His bond with Liu Bei and Zhang Fei - the famous "Oath of the Peach Garden" - became the model of sworn brotherhood. His refusal to betray Liu Bei even when offered wealth and position by Cao Cao exemplifies the Chinese ideal of yi (righteousness/loyalty).

Famous Deeds with the Blade

The Romance of the Three Kingdoms describes numerous legendary feats performed by Guan Yu with his Green Dragon Crescent Blade:

  • Slaying Hua Xiong: In his first major battle, Guan Yu killed the feared general Hua Xiong before the wine offered to him had cooled - a feat that established his reputation overnight
  • Passing Five Passes: When returning to Liu Bei's service, Guan Yu fought his way through five passes, killing six generals who blocked his path, all with his great blade
  • Single Combat Victories: Guan Yu defeated numerous champions in single combat, with his blade cleaving through opponents and their weapons alike
  • Night Operations: The blade was said to glow with ethereal light during night battles, visible across the battlefield
"Guan Yu raised his great blade and brought it down in a single sweeping arc. Before the warmth had left the wine cup, Hua Xiong's head lay at his feet."

- Romance of the Three Kingdoms

Symbolism and Cultural Impact

Deification of Guan Yu

Following his death, Guan Yu was gradually deified, eventually becoming known as Guan Di (Emperor Guan) or Guan Gong (Lord Guan). He is worshipped as the God of War, patron of soldiers and martial artists, but also paradoxically as a god of literature and wealth. Temples to Guan Di exist throughout China and wherever Chinese communities have settled.

The Green Dragon Crescent Blade appears in virtually every depiction of Guan Di, becoming inseparable from his image. The weapon represents not just martial power but the righteous application of force - violence in service of justice and loyalty.

Martial Arts Influence

The guandao (a generic term for this type of weapon, literally "Guan's blade") became a standard weapon in Chinese martial arts, inspired by Guan Yu's legendary weapon. Numerous martial arts schools teach guandao techniques, and the weapon appears in traditional forms and demonstrations.

In martial arts symbolism, the guandao represents mastery over great power. The difficulty of wielding such a heavy, long weapon makes it a test of strength, skill, and dedication - qualities associated with Guan Yu himself.

Symbol of Righteousness

The blade has come to symbolize righteous power throughout Chinese culture. Police stations, government buildings, and even businesses often display images of Guan Yu with his blade as protection against evil and corruption. The weapon represents justice that is strong enough to enforce itself.

Cultural Presence

Statues of Guan Yu with his blade stand throughout the Chinese-speaking world. The largest, in Jingzhou, China, stands 58 meters tall. The weapon continues to be a popular subject for traditional craftsmen, martial arts practitioners, and modern artists alike.
