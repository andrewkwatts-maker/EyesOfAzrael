üìú Historical Overview

Fatima is one of the most important Marian shrines in the world, where the Virgin Mary reportedly appeared to three shepherd children six times between May and October 1917. The apparitions concluded with the famous "Miracle of the Sun," witnessed by an estimated 70,000 people.

The apparitions occurred during World War I, and Mary's messages concerned prayer, penance, and the conversion of Russia. The three "secrets of Fatima" revealed to the children have been subjects of intense speculation, particularly the Third Secret, which was kept sealed until 2000.

"In the end, my Immaculate Heart will triumph. The Holy Father will consecrate Russia to me, and she will be converted, and a period of peace will be granted to the world."

‚Äî Our Lady of Fatima, July 13, 1917

üëß The Three Seers

The Virgin Mary appeared to three illiterate shepherd children tending their families' flocks in the Cova da Iria.

üëß Lucia dos Santos

Age 10 at apparitions. Became a Carmelite nun. Wrote memoirs detailing the secrets. Died in 2005 at age 97.

üë¶ Francisco Marto

Age 9 at apparitions. Could see but not hear the Lady. Died of Spanish flu in 1919. Canonized 2017.

üëß Jacinta Marto

Age 7 at apparitions. Youngest seer. Died of Spanish flu in 1920. Canonized 2017 as youngest non-martyr saint.

Before Mary appeared, an angel visited the children three times in 1916, teaching them prayers and preparing them for the apparitions. This angel, identifying himself as the "Angel of Portugal" or "Angel of Peace," instructed them in Eucharistic devotion.

‚ú® The Apparitions

Mary appeared on the 13th of each month from May to October 1917, except August when the children were imprisoned by local authorities.

May 13, 1917

First apparition. "A Lady more brilliant than the sun" asks the children to return on the 13th for six months

June 13, 1917

Mary reveals Jacinta and Francisco will die young; Lucia will live to spread devotion to the Immaculate Heart

July 13, 1917

The Three Secrets are revealed, including the vision of Hell and request for consecration of Russia

August 19, 1917

Apparition at Valinhos after children released from prison; promised miracle in October

September 13, 1917

30,000 witnesses see luminous globe carrying the Lady; she again promises October miracle

October 13, 1917

The Miracle of the Sun witnessed by 70,000 people; Mary identifies herself as Our Lady of the Rosary

üîê The Three Secrets

On July 13, 1917, Mary revealed three prophetic secrets to the children. The first two were disclosed by Lucia in 1941; the third remained sealed until 2000.

1Ô∏è‚É£ First Secret: Vision of Hell

The children were shown a terrifying vision of Hell with souls falling "like snowflakes." Mary told them: "You have seen Hell where the souls of poor sinners go. To save them, God wishes to establish in the world devotion to my Immaculate Heart."

2Ô∏è‚É£ Second Secret: World War & Russia

Prophecy of World War I ending, but warning of a worse war if people did not stop offending God. Request for consecration of Russia to prevent the spread of her errors (interpreted as Communism) and persecution of the Church.

3Ô∏è‚É£ Third Secret: The Bishop in White

Vision of a "Bishop dressed in white" being killed along with other clergy and faithful. The Vatican interpreted this as the 1981 assassination attempt on Pope John Paul II, who credited Mary with saving his life.

‚òÄÔ∏è The Miracle of the Sun

On October 13, 1917, approximately 70,000 people gathered at the Cova da Iria after the children announced the Lady would perform a miracle "so that all may believe." What occurred remains one of the most witnessed supernatural events in modern history.

üåßÔ∏è The Setting

Heavy rain had fallen all morning, leaving the crowd soaked and the ground muddy. At noon, Lucia called out to close umbrellas and the rain stopped.

üåÄ The Solar Display

The sun appeared to spin, throwing off colored lights in all directions. It "danced" in the sky, then seemed to plunge toward earth before returning to normal.

üëî Instantaneous Drying

When the phenomenon ended, the crowd's rain-soaked clothes and the muddy ground were completely dry - considered part of the miracle.

üì∞ Press Coverage

Even anticlerical newspapers reported the event. O Seculo, Portugal's largest paper, ran front-page coverage despite its anti-Church stance.

"The sun trembled, made sudden incredible movements outside all cosmic laws - the sun 'danced' according to the typical expression of the people."

‚Äî Dr. Almeida Garrett, Professor of Natural Sciences at Coimbra University, eyewitness

‚õ™ The Sanctuary

The Sanctuary of Fatima has grown from a simple chapel to a major pilgrimage complex visited by millions annually.

üèõÔ∏è Basilica of Our Lady of the Rosary

Neo-Baroque basilica completed in 1953, containing the tombs of Francisco, Jacinta, and Lucia. Its 65-meter tower is visible throughout the sanctuary.

üíí Chapel of Apparitions

Open-air chapel marking the exact spot of the apparitions. Contains the statue of Our Lady of Fatima and the holm oak tree where Mary appeared.

üèüÔ∏è Basilica of the Holy Trinity

Modern church completed in 2007, seating 8,633. One of the largest churches in the world, featuring art from around the globe.

üõê The Grand Esplanade

Vast plaza twice the size of St. Peter's Square, capable of holding 500,000 pilgrims. Site of major celebrations on May 13 and October 13.

‚ú® Religious Significance

üåç Cold War Prophecy

Fatima's warning about Russia spreading "her errors throughout the world" was interpreted as predicting Communist expansion, making it central to Catholic anti-Communist sentiment.

üáªüá¶ Papal Connection

Pope John Paul II credited Our Lady of Fatima with saving his life in the 1981 assassination attempt, which occurred on May 13 - Fatima's anniversary.

üîÑ Consecration of Russia

Multiple popes have performed consecrations of Russia/the world. Pope Francis renewed the consecration in 2022 during the Russia-Ukraine conflict.

‚òÆÔ∏è Peace Through Prayer

Fatima's message emphasizes that world peace depends on prayer, conversion, and devotion - a spiritual solution to geopolitical problems.

üåç Modern Significance

Fatima remains one of the world's most visited pilgrimage sites, with particular significance in Portuguese national identity and global Catholic devotion.

üáµüáπ National Shrine

Fatima is central to Portuguese Catholic identity. Major celebrations occur on May 13 and October 13, drawing hundreds of thousands of pilgrims.

üåê World Apostolate

The World Apostolate of Fatima (Blue Army) promotes the message globally with millions of members committed to the First Saturday devotion.

üö∂ Pilgrim Walks

Many pilgrims walk to Fatima from across Portugal, some on their knees across the sanctuary esplanade in acts of penance and devotion.

üéñÔ∏è Centenary Canonizations

Pope Francis canonized Francisco and Jacinta during the 2017 centenary visit, making them the youngest non-martyr saints in Church history.
