Key Observations

Striking Similarities

  • Divine decision to destroy via flood
  • One family chosen/warned
  • Vessel construction with specific details
  • Animals aboard for preservation
  • Waters cover mountains
  • Ark/boat lands on mountain
  • Birds sent to test habitability
  • Sacrifice offered after flood
  • Divine response to sacrifice ("sweet savor"/"flies")
  • Repopulation from survivors

Critical Differences

  • Theology: Monotheism (Genesis/Enoch) vs Polytheism (Mesopotamian/Greek)
  • Reason: Moral corruption (Genesis) vs Noise/annoyance (Mesopotamian) vs Cosmic rebellion (Enoch)
  • Divine Character: Sovereign/just (Genesis) vs Frightened/regretful (Mesopotamian)
  • Resolution: Covenant promise (Genesis) vs Immortality reward (Gilgamesh) vs Population control (Atrahasis)
  • Duration: 1 year (Genesis) vs 1-2 weeks (Mesopotamian)
  • Ark Shape: Ship (Genesis) vs Cube (Gilgamesh)
