The 22 Archetypal Trumps

The Major Arcana represents the soul's journey from ignorance to enlightenment, from the Fool's first step into the unknown to the World's cosmic completion. These 22 trumps are not merely cards but living archetypes, corresponding to the 22 paths on the Kabbalistic Tree of Life, the 22 letters of the Hebrew alphabet, and universal patterns of consciousness and transformation.

The Journey Begins - Cards 0-7

The first stage of the journey introduces the fundamental forces: the seeker (Fool), the tools (Magician), the mysteries (High Priestess), the creative and authoritative principles (Empress/Emperor), and the gatekeepers of tradition and choice (Hierophant/Lovers).

Understanding the Major & Minor Arcana

The Fool's Journey

The 22 trumps tell a story: the Fool (pure potential) encounters each archetype in sequence, learning lessons and gaining wisdom. By the World, the Fool has become the cosmic dancer, having integrated all experiences into enlightened wholeness. This journey mirrors every human life and every spiritual path.

Correspondences & Systems

Each trump corresponds to multiple systems simultaneously:

  • Hebrew Letters: The 22 letters of creation (Aleph through Tav)
  • Tree of Life Paths: The 22 paths connecting the 10 Sefirot
  • Astrology: Planets, zodiac signs, and elements
  • Elements: Fire, Water, Air, Earth distributed across the trumps
  • Alchemy: Stages of the Great Work from nigredo to rubedo
  • Psychology: Jungian archetypes of the collective unconscious

Three Major Decks

The Hermetic tradition recognizes three foundational tarot decks, each with unique symbolism:

  • Tarot de Marseille: The oldest traditional deck, emphasizing medieval Christian imagery
  • Rider-Waite-Smith: Created by A.E. Waite and Pamela Colman Smith (1909), integrating Golden Dawn teachings
  • Thoth Tarot: Designed by Aleister Crowley and Lady Frieda Harris (1943), emphasizing Thelemic philosophy
Primary Sources: Arthur Edward Waite's The Pictorial Key to the Tarot, Aleister Crowley's The Book of Thoth, Paul Foster Case's The Tarot, Robert Wang's The Qabalistic Tarot, Israel Regardie's The Golden Dawn
