Ezekiel 38-39 and Revelation 20:7-10

Ezekiel's Prophecy (Ezekiel 38-39)

  • Gog from land of Magog, chief prince of Meshek and Tubal
  • Comes from far north with vast army against Israel
  • "You will advance against my people Israel like a cloud that covers the land"
  • God intervenes: earthquake, sword against Gog, plague, torrential rain, hailstones, burning sulfur
  • "On the mountains of Israel you will fall, you and all your troops"
  • Birds and wild animals feast on corpses

Revelation's Fulfillment (Revelation 20:7-10)

  • After 1000 years, Satan released from prison
  • Deceives nations in four corners of earth‚ÄîGog and Magog
  • Gathers them for battle; number like sand on seashore
  • They march across earth, surround camp of God's people
  • Fire comes down from heaven and devours them
  • Devil thrown into lake of burning sulfur forever

Connection

Revelation uses "Gog and Magog" as symbolic names for the final rebellion of nations against God's people after the millennium. Just as Ezekiel's Gog was destroyed by divine intervention, so too the final rebellion is crushed by fire from heaven.

Key Difference: Ezekiel's Gog is defeated before restoration; Revelation's Gog and Magog come after the millennium, representing Satan's final attempt to overthrow God's kingdom before the eternal state.
