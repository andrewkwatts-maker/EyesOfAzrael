The Apocalypse of Jesus Christ

The final book of the Bible, revealing the ultimate victory of Christ, the judgment of evil, and the restoration of all creation. Written by John the Apostle on the island of Patmos, Revelation draws deeply from Old Testament prophecy to unveil heaven's final plan.

Overview

The Vision on Patmos

Around 95 AD, the Apostle John received a divine revelation while exiled on the Greek island of Patmos. This apocalyptic vision reveals the culmination of God's redemptive plan, showing:

  • Christ's Glory: The risen Lord appearing in majesty among seven golden lampstands
  • The Church's Mission: Letters to seven churches in Asia Minor addressing their strengths and failures
  • Heaven's Throne Room: The divine council and the Lamb who opens the sealed scroll
  • Judgment Cycles: Seven seals, seven trumpets, and seven bowls of divine wrath
  • Cosmic Conflict: The war between the Dragon (Satan) and God's people
  • Babylon's Fall: The destruction of the great harlot city representing worldly empire
  • Christ's Return: The King of Kings riding on a white horse to establish justice
  • New Creation: The restoration of Eden in the New Jerusalem, where God dwells with humanity forever

Revelation and the Old Testament

Revelation is the most Hebrew book in the New Testament, containing over 500 allusions to the Old Testament. John weaves together imagery from Daniel, Ezekiel, Isaiah, Zechariah, and the plagues of Exodus to show how Jesus fulfills all prophecy and brings creation to its destined end.
