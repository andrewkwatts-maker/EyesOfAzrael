Introduction: Four Empires Become One

Daniel received two visions of world empires: the metallic statue (Daniel 2) and four beasts (Daniel 7-8). Both outline the same succession of kingdoms from Babylon to the end times. Revelation 13 and 17 reveal that the final antichrist kingdom will be a composite of all previous empires‚Äîcombining the worst characteristics of Babylon, Medo-Persia, Greece, and Rome into one ultimate beast system.

Daniel 7: Four Beasts from the Sea

1st Beast: Lion with Eagle's Wings

Daniel 7:4

"The first was like a lion, and it had the wings of an eagle. I watched until its wings were torn off and it was lifted from the ground so that it stood on two feet like a human being, and the mind of a human was given to it."

Kingdom: Babylon

Symbolism: Lion = king of beasts (Babylonian symbol), eagle wings = swift conquest, humanized = Nebuchadnezzar's humbling (Dan 4)

2nd Beast: Bear Raised Up

Daniel 7:5

"And there before me was a second beast, which looked like a bear. It was raised up on one of its sides, and it had three ribs in its mouth between its teeth. It was told, 'Get up and eat your fill of flesh!'"

Kingdom: Medo-Persia

Symbolism: Bear = strength and ferocity, raised on one side = Persia dominant over Media, three ribs = Babylon, Lydia, Egypt conquered

Symbolism: Leopard = speed and agility, four wings = Alexander's unprecedented rapid conquest, four heads = empire divided among four generals (Ptolemy, Seleucus, Cassander, Lysimachus)

Revelation 13:1-2

"And I saw a beast coming out of the sea. It had ten horns and seven heads... The beast I saw resembled a LEOPARD, but had feet like those of a BEAR and a mouth like that of a LION. The dragon gave the beast his power and his throne and great authority."

Simultaneous: All empires combined in reverse order (leopard body, bear feet, lion mouth)

Theological Significance: The Ultimate Evil Empire

Why Reverse Order? Revelation lists the beasts backward (leopard-bear-lion instead of lion-bear-leopard) because John is looking back through history from the final empire. The composite beast embodies:

  • Leopard body (Greece): Speed, cunning, Hellenistic culture and philosophy
  • Bear feet (Persia): Crushing strength, overwhelming military might
  • Lion mouth (Babylon): Royal authority, idolatrous pride, claim to divine glory
  • Ten horns (Rome/Final): Political confederation, iron strength, global dominion

The final Antichrist kingdom is not just another empire‚Äîit is the summation of all previous empires, combining their worst elements into one satanically-empowered system. All the accumulated evil of human history converges in the beast.

Revelation 17: Seven Heads = Seven Kingdoms

The Vision The Interpretation

Revelation 17:3

"Then the angel carried me away in the Spirit into a wilderness. There I saw a woman sitting on a scarlet beast that was covered with blasphemous names and had seven heads and ten horns."

Revelation 17:9-11

"The seven heads are seven hills on which the woman sits. They are also seven kings. Five have fallen, one is, the other has not yet come; but when he does come, he must remain for only a little while. The beast who once was, and now is not, is an eighth king. He belongs to the seven and is going to his destruction."

Identification of the Seven Kingdoms

From John's perspective on Patmos (c. 95 AD):

  1. Egypt - First great oppressor of God's people (fallen)
  2. Assyria - Conquered Northern Kingdom (fallen)
  3. Babylon - Destroyed Jerusalem, exiled Judah (fallen)
  4. Medo-Persia - Ruled during return from exile (fallen)
  5. Greece - Hellenistic persecution (fallen)
  6. Rome - Currently ruling ("one is")
  7. Revived Rome / Final Confederation - Yet to come ("has not yet come")
  8. The Beast/Antichrist - "Eighth king... belongs to the seven" (final manifestation of all previous evil)

Alternative interpretation: Seven heads = seven specific Roman emperors. Either way, the pattern holds: sequential empires culminating in a final satanic ruler who embodies all previous kingdoms.
