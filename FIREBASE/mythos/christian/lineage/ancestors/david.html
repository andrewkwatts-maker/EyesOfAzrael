King David: Man After God's Heart

The shepherd boy who became Israel's greatest king. God made an eternal covenant with David, promising that his throne would be established forever - a promise fulfilled in Jesus Christ, the Son of David who reigns eternally.

When David desired to build a temple for God, the Lord responded by making an eternal covenant - David's house, kingdom, and throne would be established forever. This promise became the foundation of messianic expectation.

Position in Genealogy: Generation 14 in Matthew's first set - the pivotal ancestor (Matthew 1:6)

Significance: The genealogies are structured around David. Jesus is repeatedly called "Son of David" - the ultimate messianic title. Both Matthew and Luke trace Jesus's descent through David, though via different sons (Solomon vs Nathan).
