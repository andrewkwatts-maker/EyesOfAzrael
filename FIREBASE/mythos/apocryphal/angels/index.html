Angels & The Watchers

The celestial hierarchies, fallen angels, and divine messengers of Enochian tradition. From the highest archangels who stand before God's throne to the rebellious Watchers who descended to Earth, explore the complex angelology that bridges heaven and earth.

üî• The Watchers - Fallen Angels

The Great Transgression

In the days when humanity began to multiply upon the earth, 200 angels known as the Watchers descended from heaven to Mount Hermon. Led by Samyaza and Azazel, they swore a binding oath to take human wives and teach forbidden knowledge to mankind. This catastrophic rebellion against divine law would bring corruption, violence, and ultimately the Great Flood upon the world.

The Book of Enoch names the chief Watchers: Samyaza their leader, Azazel who taught warfare, Araqiel who taught earth's signs, Shamsiel who taught sun signs, Sariel who taught moon courses, Penemue who taught writing, Kasdeja who taught abortion and dark arts, and many more. Each revealed secrets that heaven had forbidden humans to know.

Heaven's Champions

In opposition to the fallen Watchers, the archangels remain faithful to God's will. These mighty beings serve as commanders of heavenly hosts, divine messengers, and agents of judgment. The four (or seven) archangels stand closest to the throne of God, each governing specific aspects of creation and divine intervention.

Commander of heaven's armies and protector of Israel. Michael bound the Watchers and cast them into darkness. He is set over the best part of humanity and over chaos, commanding the nations. Defeats Satan in the final battle.

üëÅÔ∏è Metatron - The Lesser YHVH

Enoch Transformed

In the third book of Enoch, the prophet who walked with God is transformed into the greatest of angels - Metatron, the "Lesser YHVH" or "Prince of the Divine Presence." Standing 13 feet tall with 72 wings and 365,000 eyes, Metatron sits on a throne at the door of the seventh heaven, second only to God himself.

Metatron serves as God's chancellor and celestial scribe, recording all deeds in the heavenly books. He commands 1,000 ministering angels and oversees the entire angelic hierarchy. His name may derive from "metator" (guide) or represent the divine throne itself. He is the Voice of God, speaking with divine authority to angels and humans alike.

Roles: Celestial Scribe, Chancellor of Heaven, Teacher of Prematurely Dead Children, Guide of Souls, Keeper of Divine Secrets, Voice of God

Attributes: 72 wings, 365,000 eyes, body of fire, throne next to God, 13 feet tall, wears divine crown, possesses 72 divine names

üèõÔ∏è The Angelic Hierarchies

Celestial Orders (Nine Choirs)

  • Seraphim - Six-winged beings of pure fire who surround God's throne, crying "Holy, Holy, Holy"
  • Cherubim - Guardians of sacred spaces, with four faces (human, lion, ox, eagle) and four wings
  • Thrones (Ophanim) - Wheel-beings covered with eyes, instruments of divine justice
  • Dominions - Regulate angelic duties and ensure cosmic order
  • Virtues - Grant blessings, miracles, and courage to the worthy
  • Powers - Warriors against evil, commanders of demonic forces
  • Principalities - Guardians of nations, cities, and sacred places
  • Archangels - Divine messengers and commanders of heavenly hosts
  • Angels - Messengers, guardians, and servants closest to humanity

Other Angelic Classes

  • Watchers (Grigori) - Originally set to watch over humanity, 200 fell and corrupted the world
  • Ishim - Angels of fire and song who surround the divine throne
  • Hashmallim - Beings of electrum who speak through storm and fire
  • Arelim - Mighty angels, valiant and strong, who control the elements
  • Malakim - Common angels, the vast majority of the heavenly host
  • Angels of the Presence - Elite angels who stand before God's face
  • Angels of Sanctification - Holy beings who maintain cosmic purity
  • Ministering Angels - Created daily from fire-stream, exist briefly to praise God

üìñ Angelic Lore & Wisdom

Characteristics of Angels

  • Appearance: Beings of fire and light, often appearing human but radiating glory. Some have wings (2, 4, or 6), multiple faces, or forms covered with eyes.
  • Language: Speak Celestial Hebrew, a pure form of the language used at creation. Their voices can shake mountains or whisper gently to souls.
  • Powers: Command elements, appear and disappear at will, read thoughts, see past and future, heal or destroy with a word.
  • Nature: Created beings, not eternal like God. Made of fire and spirit, they do not age, eat, or sleep. The Watchers proved angels can sin.
  • Numbers: Countless myriads - "ten thousand times ten thousand and thousands of thousands." Created before humanity, they watched the formation of the world.

The Divine Names

Each angel bears a name ending in -el ("of God") or -yah (shortened YHVH), signifying their divine origin and purpose. These names hold power, and knowing an angel's true name grants authority over them - as Solomon learned when he bound demons. The Books of Enoch preserve hundreds of angelic names, each encoding the angel's function and position in the cosmic hierarchy.

Notable Name Patterns: Michael ("Who is like God?"), Gabriel ("Strength of God"), Raphael ("God heals"), Uriel ("Light of God"), Metatron (possibly "One who guards"), Sandalphon ("Co-brother", paired with Metatron)
