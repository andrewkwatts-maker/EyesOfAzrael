## Methodology: What Makes a Correlation Strong vs. Weak?

### ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ TIER 1: Strong Correlations (70-95%)
**Criteria:**
- Appears in multiple independent domains (e.g., 64 in I Ching, DNA, spinors)
- Experimentally verified in physics (e.g., χ=72 confirmed by LEP 3-generation constraint)
- Mathematically necessary structure (e.g., 2⁶ binary encoding)

**Examples:**
- 64 Hexagrams = DNA Codons = Spinor (95%)
- 72 Transformations = χ = 72 Euler Characteristic (85%)
- 3 Realms = 3 Generations (95%)

**Caveats:** Even strong correlations likely reflect mathematical universality rather than encoded physics knowledge.

### ⭐⭐⭐ TIER 2: Medium Correlations (50-70%)
**Criteria:**
- Suggestive but not compelling
- Could be astronomical observation or cultural transmission
- Multiple alternative explanations exist

**Examples:**
- 7 Heavens = 7 Compactified Dimensions (60%)
- 72 Names of God = χ (70% - but likely astronomical origin)
- Yin-Yang = Matter/Antimatter (70%)

**Caveats:** Correlation ≠ causation. Selection bias is strong.

### ⭐⭐ TIER 3: Weak Correlations (40-50%)
**Criteria:**
- Highly flexible interpretations
- Common numbers with many possible mappings
- Base-10 or cognitive universal artifacts

**Examples:**
- 10 Sefirot = 10D M-Theory (50%)
- 10 Heavens = 10D (50%)
- 1+3 Brane Pattern (45%)
- Wu Xing 5 Elements = 5 States (40%)

**Caveats:** These are numerological coincidences, not physics.

### ⭐ TIER 4: Very Weak Correlations (30-40%)
**Criteria:**
- Forced retrofitting
- Universal myth archetypes
- No specificity

**Examples:**
- Watchers = Higher-Dimensional Beings (30%)
- Nephilim = Dimensional Hybrids (25%)

**Caveats:** These are creative interpretations with no evidential basis.
