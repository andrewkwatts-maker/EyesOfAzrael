Luxor Temple - Southern Sanctuary

Luxor (Ancient Thebes), Egypt

Ancient Egyptian New Kingdom Amun Worship

📜 Historical Overview

Luxor Temple (Egyptian: Ipet resyt, "Southern Sanctuary") is one of ancient Egypt's most magnificent temple complexes, dedicated primarily to the Theban Triad of Amun, Mut, and Khonsu. Unlike most Egyptian temples dedicated to a cult god or a pharaoh's funerary cult, Luxor Temple was dedicated to the rejuvenation of kingship - it was here that many pharaohs were crowned and where the annual Opet Festival celebrated the divine union that renewed the pharaoh's power. Located on the east bank of the Nile in the heart of modern Luxor, the temple was largely built by Amenhotep III (1390-1352 BCE) during the 18th Dynasty, with significant additions by Ramesses II.

"I built a monument for you at the southern sanctuary of Thebes, of excellent sandstone. Its columns reach up to heaven like the four pillars of heaven. It shall endure like heaven eternally." - Amenhotep III, temple dedication inscription

The temple remained in continuous use for over 3,000 years, evolving through Egyptian, Greek, Roman, and Christian eras. Alexander the Great rebuilt the sanctuary, and the Romans established a legion camp around it. A church was built within its walls, and the Abu Haggag Mosque, constructed atop ancient debris in the 13th century, remains an active place of worship within the temple precincts today. Connected to Karnak Temple by the Avenue of Sphinxes (recently restored), Luxor Temple served as the culmination point for the Opet Festival procession and stands as a living palimpsest of religious history.

🏛 Architectural Marvel

🛠 First Pylon (Ramesses II)

  • 65 meters (213 feet) wide
  • 24 meters (79 feet) high
  • Six colossal statues (originally)
  • Two seated Ramesses (23m tall)
  • Battle of Kadesh reliefs
  • Originally two obelisks (one now in Paris)
  • Grand ceremonial entrance

🔮 Colonnade of Amenhotep III

  • 14 massive papyrus columns
  • 16 meters (52 feet) tall each
  • Open papyrus capital design
  • Processional route for Opet Festival
  • Reliefs showing festival celebrations
  • One of finest in Egypt
  • Completed by Tutankhamun and Horemheb

🏙 Court of Ramesses II

  • Double row of columns
  • 74 papyrus columns total
  • Colossal Ramesses statues between columns
  • Abu Haggag Mosque above northeast corner
  • Triple-barque shrine for Theban triad
  • Usurped statues from Amenhotep III
  • Scene of self-glorification

🦁 Avenue of Sphinxes

  • 2.7 kilometers (1.7 miles) long
  • Connected Luxor to Karnak temples
  • Over 1,000 sphinxes originally
  • Human-headed (Nectanebo I section)
  • Ram-headed (Karnak section)
  • Processional route for Opet Festival
  • Recently restored and reopened

🛍 Sanctuary of Alexander

  • Inner sanctuary rebuilt by Alexander
  • Replaced Amenhotep III's original
  • Alexander depicted as pharaoh
  • Making offerings to Amun
  • Legitimized Greek rule
  • Preserved Egyptian religious tradition
  • Hellenistic-Egyptian fusion

🏠 Birth Room

  • Depicts divine birth of Amenhotep III
  • Amun visits Queen Mutemwia
  • Divine conception narrative
  • Khnum fashions baby on potter's wheel
  • Legitimizes pharaoh's divinity
  • Mother of ka concept
  • Template for later birth rooms

🌞 Religious & Mythological Significance

🎭 Opet Festival

  • Annual 11-27 day celebration
  • Amun, Mut, Khonsu statues processed
  • From Karnak to Luxor by river or sphinx road
  • Pharaoh renewed divine ka (spirit)
  • Sacred marriage ritual
  • Emerged rejuvenated as god
  • Public feasting and celebration

👑 Divine Kingship

  • Temple dedicated to royal ka
  • Pharaoh's divine aspect venerated
  • Coronation ceremonies performed
  • Divine birth proven here
  • Union with Amun renewed annually
  • Legitimacy through ritual
  • Political and religious intertwined

🌞 Amun-Ra Worship

  • King of the gods at Thebes
  • Hidden one merged with sun god
  • Supreme state deity
  • Invisible creative force
  • Entered statue during rituals
  • Pharaoh as son of Amun
  • Center of Theban theology

🌚 Theban Triad

  • Amun (father) - king of gods
  • Mut (mother) - divine consort
  • Khonsu (son) - moon god
  • Family mirrored royal household
  • Three barque shrines in temple
  • Procession of all three in Opet
  • Divine family model

👑 Associated Deities & Figures

🌞

Amun-Ra

King of the gods, hidden creative force merged with the sun. Primary deity of the temple.

🦅

Mut

Mother goddess and divine queen. Consort of Amun, depicted as vulture or lioness-headed.

🌚

Khonsu

Moon god, divine child of Amun and Mut. Traveler, healer, protector.

👑

Amenhotep III

18th Dynasty pharaoh who built the main temple. Depicted in divine birth reliefs.

⚔

Ramesses II

Added first pylon, courtyard, and colossal statues. Self-proclaimed greatest pharaoh.

🌐

Alexander the Great

Rebuilt the sanctuary, depicted as pharaoh worshipping Amun to legitimize rule.
