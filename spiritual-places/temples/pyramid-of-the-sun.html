Pyramid of the Sun - Teotihuacan

San Juan Teotihuacan, State of Mexico, Mexico

Mesoamerican Pre-Aztec Solar Worship

📜 Historical Overview

The Pyramid of the Sun (Spanish: Piramide del Sol) is the largest structure in the ancient city of Teotihuacan and one of the largest pyramids in Mesoamerica. Standing approximately 66 meters (216 feet) high with a base measuring 225 by 222 meters, it dominates the cityscape along the famous Avenue of the Dead. Built around 100 CE, the pyramid was constructed over a sacred cave that was likely a pilgrimage site for centuries before the city's founding. The Teotihuacanos considered this cave a place of origin - where humanity emerged from the underworld and where the sun and moon were born.

"It was here that the gods convened to create the Fifth Sun, sacrificing themselves so that a new age might begin for humanity. The pyramid marks the spot where divine fire first illuminated the world." - Aztec oral tradition

The original builders of Teotihuacan remain somewhat mysterious - by the time the Aztecs arrived in the 14th century, the city had been abandoned for over 700 years. The Aztecs gave the city and pyramid their current names, believing it was built by giants or gods. Modern archaeology has revealed that the pyramid was built in two phases, with the second stage roughly doubling its size. At its peak around 450 CE, Teotihuacan was one of the largest cities in the world with a population of 125,000-200,000, and the Pyramid of the Sun was its most sacred ceremonial center.

🏛 Architectural Marvel

The Pyramid of the Sun represents the pinnacle of Teotihuacan engineering and cosmological design. Its massive scale, precise orientation, and sacred cave foundation make it one of the most significant monuments of the ancient Americas.

📐 Dimensions & Scale

  • Height: 66 meters (216 feet) - originally taller
  • Base: 225 x 222 meters (738 x 728 feet)
  • Volume: Approximately 1 million cubic meters
  • Third largest pyramid in the world by volume
  • 248 steep steps to the summit
  • Five terraced levels
  • Originally covered in painted stucco

📍 Sacred Cave

  • Natural cave discovered beneath pyramid in 1971
  • Seven-chambered tunnel system
  • 103 meters long, ending in clover-leaf chamber
  • Believed to be original sacred site
  • Connected to origin mythology
  • Place of emergence (Chicomoztoc)
  • Artifacts suggest ritual use for millennia

🌞 Solar Alignment

  • Faces the sunset on specific dates
  • Aligned 15.5 degrees north of west
  • Sun sets directly in front on August 12
  • August 12: Beginning date of Mesoamerican calendar
  • Marks agricultural and ceremonial cycles
  • Zenith passage alignment
  • Stellar alignments to Pleiades suggested

🚧 Construction Method

  • Adobe brick core
  • Volcanic stone and rubble fill
  • Tezontle (volcanic gravel) outer layer
  • Originally lime-plastered and painted
  • Evidence of red paint decoration
  • No metal tools used
  • Built without the wheel

🏙 Urban Context

  • Located midway along Avenue of the Dead
  • Flanked by Plaza of the Sun
  • Near Pyramid of the Moon to the north
  • Temple of the Feathered Serpent to south
  • Central to city's sacred geography
  • Visible from entire city
  • Focal point of ceremonial activities

🔭 Archaeological Discoveries

  • Obsidian artifacts in foundations
  • Child burials at corners (consecration)
  • Greenstone offerings
  • Evidence of wooden temple on summit
  • Mica sheets in upper layers
  • Tunnels and chambers still being explored
  • Mercury pools found in related structures

🌞 Religious & Mythological Significance

The Pyramid of the Sun was the cosmic axis of Teotihuacan - the point where heaven, earth, and underworld connected. Its sacred cave foundation represented the womb of creation, while its towering height reached toward the celestial realm.

🌟 Creation of the Fifth Sun

According to Aztec legend, the gods gathered at Teotihuacan to create the current age:

  • Gods assembled after Fourth Sun's destruction
  • Nanahuatzin leaped into sacred fire
  • Became Tonatiuh, the Fifth Sun
  • Other gods sacrificed to start sun's motion
  • Established cosmic order of current age
  • Pyramid marks this sacred transformation
  • Human sacrifice continues this cosmic debt

🗺 Axis Mundi

  • World axis connecting cosmic levels
  • Cave below: Underworld (Mictlan)
  • Pyramid: Earthly realm
  • Summit temple: Celestial realm
  • Point of communication with gods
  • Where shamans entered spirit worlds

👑 Associated Deities & Concepts

🌞

Sun God

Likely Teotihuacan's solar deity, predecessor to Aztec Tonatiuh. Source of light, warmth, and cosmic order.

🌊

Tlaloc

Rain and storm god with ancient origins. Cave beneath pyramid connected to water and fertility.

🐍

Quetzalcoatl

Feathered Serpent deity prominent at Teotihuacan. God of wind, learning, and civilization.

🌠

Great Goddess

Mysterious spider-woman figure. Associated with cave, water, and underworld emergence.

🔥

Huehueteotl

Old Fire God, one of oldest Mesoamerican deities. Fire ceremonies on pyramid summit.

🌚

Moon Deity

Partner to Sun in creation myth. Pyramid of the Moon across plaza creates divine pair.
