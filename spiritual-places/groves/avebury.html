Avebury - The Greatest Stone Circle

Wiltshire, England

Neolithic British Prehistoric Megalithic

📜 Sacred Overview

Avebury is the largest prehistoric stone circle in Europe and one of the most remarkable monuments of Neolithic Britain. Built around 2850 BCE, the site encompasses a massive circular ditch and bank (henge) enclosing an outer ring of originally around 100 sarsen stones, with two smaller inner circles. The entire village of Avebury sits within the monument, making it one of the few places where you can walk among megaliths while having a pint at the pub. Unlike its more famous neighbor Stonehenge 25 miles to the south, Avebury's sheer scale - the outer circle is 427 meters (1,400 feet) in diameter - and accessibility allow visitors to experience the stones up close, touching stones placed by hands 4,800 years ago.

"Avebury doth as much exceed in greatness the so renowned Stonehenge as a cathedral doth a parish church." - John Aubrey, antiquarian (1663)

The monument is part of a vast ceremonial landscape including Silbury Hill (Europe's largest prehistoric mound), West Kennet Long Barrow (a Neolithic tomb), The Sanctuary (a wooden and stone structure), and the West Kennet and Beckhampton Avenues of standing stones. This complex suggests Avebury was the center of a sacred geography used for ceremonies, processions, and rituals we can only guess at. Many stones have distinctive shapes - tall narrow "male" stones and broad diamond-shaped "female" stones - placed in alternating patterns, hinting at beliefs about gender, fertility, and cosmic duality.

🟠 The Monument

📐 Dimensions

  • Outer circle: 427m (1,400 ft) diameter
  • Area: 28.5 acres (11.5 hectares)
  • Ditch: 21m wide, 11m deep originally
  • Bank: 6m high originally
  • Originally ~100 outer stones
  • Today: 27 original stones survive
  • Largest stone circle in world

🛠 Stone Types

  • Sarsen stones (silicified sandstone)
  • From Marlborough Downs nearby
  • Largest weighs ~65 tons
  • Unworked natural shapes
  • "Male" stones: tall, narrow
  • "Female" stones: broad, diamond
  • Alternating patterns observed

🏙 Inner Circles

  • Northern inner circle
  • Southern inner circle
  • Cove (three-stone setting) in north
  • Obelisk stood in southern circle
  • Ring Stone near center
  • Complex internal arrangements
  • Ritual focal points

🛣 Stone Avenues

  • West Kennet Avenue: 2.4km long
  • Connected to The Sanctuary
  • Paired stones lining route
  • Beckhampton Avenue (destroyed)
  • Processional routes suggested
  • Partially reconstructed
  • Dramatic approach to monument
