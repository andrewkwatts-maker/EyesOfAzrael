Mount Shasta - Mystic Mountain of the West

Siskiyou County, California, USA

Native American New Age Esoteric

📜 Sacred Overview

Mount Shasta is a towering stratovolcano rising 4,322 meters (14,179 feet) in northern California, dominating the landscape as a solitary, glacier-clad peak visible for over 100 miles. For the indigenous peoples of the region - including the Shasta, Wintu, Achumawi, Atsugewi, and Modoc - the mountain has been sacred since time immemorial, considered the home of the Great Spirit and the place where the Creator made the first people. In modern times, Mount Shasta has become a global center for New Age spirituality, attracting seekers who believe it is a powerful energy vortex, a UFO landing site, and the home of Lemurian survivors living in an underground city called Telos. Few mountains in the world attract such diverse and intense spiritual attention.

"When I first caught sight of Mount Shasta over the braided folds of the Sacramento Valley, I was fifty miles away and afoot, alone and weary. Yet all my blood turned to wine, and I have not been weary since." - John Muir, naturalist (1874)

The mountain's spiritual reputation has grown over more than a century. In the 1880s, occultist Frederick Spencer Oliver wrote of an advanced civilization living inside the mountain. By the 1930s, the "I AM" movement centered their teachings on Mount Shasta as a place of spiritual masters. Since then, countless spiritual teachers, channelers, and seekers have made pilgrimage to Shasta, drawn by legends of the Lemurians, reported UFO sightings, lenticular clouds interpreted as spacecraft, and a palpable sense of energy that many feel on and around the mountain.
