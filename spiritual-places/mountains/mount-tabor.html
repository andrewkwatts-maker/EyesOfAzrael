Mount Tabor - Mountain of Transfiguration

Lower Galilee, Israel

Christian Jewish Biblical

📜 Sacred Overview

Mount Tabor (Hebrew: Har Tavor) is a distinctive, dome-shaped mountain rising 575 meters (1,886 feet) above the Jezreel Valley in Lower Galilee. Revered as the traditional site of Jesus Christ's Transfiguration - where he appeared in divine glory before Peter, James, and John, conversing with Moses and Elijah - Mount Tabor has been a place of Christian pilgrimage since at least the 4th century CE. Its isolated position, rising dramatically from the surrounding plain, has made it a natural sacred site since ancient times, mentioned in the Hebrew Bible in connection with the prophetess Deborah and the boundary of tribal territories.

"And he was transfigured before them, and his face shone like the sun, and his clothes became white as light. And behold, there appeared to them Moses and Elijah, talking with him." - Matthew 17:2-3

The Transfiguration is one of the key events in Christ's ministry, revealing his divine nature to his closest disciples and confirming his identity as the fulfillment of the Law (Moses) and the Prophets (Elijah). The voice from the cloud declaring "This is my beloved Son, with whom I am well pleased; listen to him" echoes the baptism account and establishes Christ's authority. Today, the mountain is crowned by the magnificent Church of the Transfiguration, built in 1924, along with Greek Orthodox chapels and Franciscan monastery ruins, making it a major destination for Christian pilgrims visiting the Holy Land.

  • Romanesque-Syrian style
  • Two towers flanking facade
  • Golden mosaic of Transfiguration
  • Three chapels (Jesus, Moses, Elijah)
  • Byzantine church ruins beneath
  • Franciscan custody
