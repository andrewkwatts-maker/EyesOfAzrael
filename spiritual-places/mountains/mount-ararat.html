Mount Ararat - Where the Ark Rested

Eastern Turkey (visible from Armenia)

Christian Armenian Jewish Islamic

📜 Sacred Overview

Mount Ararat (Turkish: Agri Dagi, Armenian: Masis) is a snow-capped dormant volcano and the highest peak in Turkey, rising 5,137 meters (16,854 feet) near the border with Armenia and Iran. For thousands of years, this majestic twin-peaked mountain has been identified as the resting place of Noah's Ark after the Great Flood - the place where humanity began anew. While the Bible speaks only of the "mountains of Ararat" (a region, not a specific peak), tradition has fixed this particular mountain as the site where Noah, his family, and the animals disembarked to repopulate the earth. This identification has made Ararat one of the most symbolically powerful mountains in the Abrahamic traditions.

"And the ark rested in the seventh month, on the seventeenth day of the month, upon the mountains of Ararat." - Genesis 8:4 (King James Version)

For Armenians, Ararat holds even deeper significance as a national and spiritual symbol, representing their ancestral homeland and identity. Armenian legend holds that their people descended from Hayk, a great-grandson of Noah who settled in the shadow of the mountain. Though Ararat is now in Turkey, it dominates the skyline of the Armenian capital Yerevan and appears on the national coat of arms. The mountain has inspired countless expeditions searching for remains of Noah's Ark, none definitively successful, yet the search continues, driven by faith and the mountain's powerful mystique.

🏔 The Mountain

📐 Physical Dimensions

  • Greater Ararat: 5,137 m (16,854 ft)
  • Lesser Ararat: 3,896 m (12,782 ft)
  • Highest peak in Turkey
  • Dormant stratovolcano
  • Permanent ice cap above 4,200m
  • Last eruption: 1840
  • Volcanic origin (Quaternary)

🌨 Climate & Glaciers

  • Permanent snow above 4,200m
  • Ice cap 10 sq km (shrinking)
  • Extreme cold at summit
  • Violent storms common
  • Best climbing: July-September
  • Technical climb required
  • Altitude sickness risk

🌐 Geographic Setting

  • Eastern Turkey near Armenian border
  • Visible from Yerevan (40km away)
  • Near Iranian border
  • Dominates Anatolian plateau
  • Isolated volcanic massif
  • Ahora Gorge (dramatic canyon)
  • Surrounded by Kurdish villages

🗻 Names

  • Agri Dagi (Turkish: "Mountain of Pain")
  • Masis (Armenian)
  • Koh-i-Nuh (Persian: "Noah's Mountain")
  • Ararat (Hebrew/Biblical)
  • Urartu (ancient kingdom)
  • Multiple traditions claim it
  • Names reflect diverse reverence
