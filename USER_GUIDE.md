## Getting Started

### What is Eyes of Azrael?

Eyes of Azrael is a comprehensive encyclopedia of world mythology featuring:
- **500+ deities** from Greek, Norse, Egyptian, Hindu, Buddhist, Celtic, and more
- **Cross-cultural connections** linking equivalent deities across pantheons
- **User contribution system** for sharing theories and insights
- **Advanced search** with multiple modes and filters
- **Immersive WebGL backgrounds** that change based on mythology

### First Visit

When you first visit [https://eyesofazrael.web.app](https://eyesofazrael.web.app):

1. **Browse the home page** to see featured mythologies
2. **Click on a mythology** (e.g., Greek, Norse, Egyptian) to explore
3. **Use the search bar** to find specific deities or concepts
4. **Sign in with Google** to contribute theories (optional)

## Using Search

### Search Modes

Eyes of Azrael offers multiple search modes:

#### 1. Generic Search (Default)
- Search across all entity types
- Type any keyword (e.g., "thunder", "wisdom", "underworld")
- Results include deities, items, places, and concepts

#### 2. Language Search
- Search by deity name in original language
- Useful for finding entities by their native names
- Example: Search "ŒòŒµœåœÇ" for Greek deities

#### 3. Source Search
- Search by ancient text or scripture
- Find entities mentioned in specific sources
- Example: Search "Bhagavad Gita" or "Book of Revelation"

#### 4. Term Search
- Search by specific terminology or concepts
- Find entities related to theological terms
- Example: Search "reincarnation" or "salvation"

#### 5. Advanced Search
- Combine multiple criteria
- Filter by mythology, entity type, domains
- Set date ranges and source types

### Search Tips

- **Use wildcards**: Search "thun*" to find "thunder", "thunderbolt", etc.
- **Cross-mythology**: Results show equivalents across pantheons
- **Autocomplete**: Press Enter or click suggestions as you type
- **Keyboard navigation**: Use arrow keys to navigate suggestions
- **Clear results**: Click the X button to clear your search

### Display Modes

Choose how search results are displayed:
- **Grid View**: Visual cards with images
- **Table View**: Detailed information in rows
- **List View**: Compact text list
- **Panel View**: Expandable panels with details
- **Inline View**: Summary with quick links

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General

**Q: Is Eyes of Azrael free to use?**
A: Yes! All content is free to browse. Theories require a free Google account.

**Q: Can I use this content for research?**
A: Yes, with proper attribution. Cite as: "Eyes of Azrael. [Entity Name]. Retrieved [Date] from https://eyesofazrael.web.app"

**Q: Is this site affiliated with any religion?**
A: No. Eyes of Azrael is an educational resource studying mythology from a comparative perspective.

**Q: How accurate is the content?**
A: We compile from scholarly sources and ancient texts. User theories reflect individual perspectives.

### Contributions

**Q: Do I need an account to browse?**
A: No, browsing is completely open. Accounts are only needed to contribute theories.

**Q: Why Google sign-in only?**
A: Google OAuth provides secure, easy authentication without storing passwords.

**Q: Can I edit my theory after submitting?**
A: Yes, you can edit or delete your own theories anytime.

**Q: Can I add new deities to the database?**
A: Entity additions require contributor access. Email AndrewKWatts@Gmail.com to request access.

### Technical

**Q: Does the site work on mobile?**
A: Yes, Eyes of Azrael is fully responsive and optimized for mobile devices.

**Q: Why do you use cookies?**
A: Cookies store your authentication token and cache preferences. No tracking cookies.

**Q: Is my data secure?**
A: Yes. We use Firebase security rules and industry-standard encryption.

**Q: Can I use this site offline?**
A: Partially. Previously viewed pages work offline via browser cache.

### Content

**Q: Which mythology has the most content?**
A: Currently Greek (113 entities), followed by Christian (68) and Egyptian (60).

**Q: Will you add more mythologies?**
A: Yes! We're actively expanding to include more traditions.

**Q: How do I suggest a mythology to add?**
A: Email AndrewKWatts@Gmail.com with your suggestion.

**Q: Are user theories fact-checked?**
A: No. User theories represent individual research and perspectives. Read critically.
