## Analytics and Tracking

We use comprehensive analytics to improve our services. Here's exactly what we track and why:

### Google Analytics 4

We use Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to understand website usage:

**What We Track**:
- Page views and navigation paths
- Time spent on pages
- Bounce rate and exit pages
- User demographics (age, gender, interests - provided by Google)
- Geographic location (country, region, city)
- Technology (browser, OS, screen resolution, device type)
- Acquisition source (how you found our site)
- Custom events (see below)

**Why We Track It**:
- Understand which content is most valuable
- Identify usability issues
- Optimize for different devices and browsers
- Measure effectiveness of outreach efforts

**Privacy Protections**:
- IP addresses are anonymized
- User IDs are pseudonymous (not linked to real identity)
- Data is aggregated in reports
- Complies with GDPR and CCPA

**Opt-Out**: Use [Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on](https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout)

### Firebase Analytics

We use Firebase Analytics for real-time event tracking:

**What We Track**:
- Custom events (mythology viewed, search performed, theory submitted)
- User engagement metrics
- App performance (load times, errors)
- Conversion funnels

**Why We Track It**:
- Real-time monitoring of site health
- Track feature usage
- Identify and fix errors quickly
- Measure user engagement

**Privacy Protections**:
- No personally identifiable information (PII) in events
- Event parameters are minimized
- Data retention limited to 14 months

### Custom Events

We track the following custom events:

#### Content Viewing
- **mythology_viewed**: Which mythology you view (e.g., Greek, Norse)
- **deity_viewed**: Which deity details you open
- **entity_detail_opened**: When you open any entity modal

**Why**: Understand content popularity, prioritize expansions

#### Search Behavior
- **search**: Your search query
- **search_result_clicked**: Which result you clicked and its position

**Why**: Improve search relevance, identify content gaps

#### User Contributions
- **theory_submitted**: When you submit a theory (not the content)
- **entity_submitted**: When you submit an entity (not the content)
- **user_contribution**: Generic contribution event

**Why**: Optimize submission process, measure community engagement

#### Interactions
- **button_click**: Which buttons you click
- **form_submit**: Which forms you complete
- **scroll_depth**: How far you scroll (25%, 50%, 75%, 100%)
- **time_on_page**: Active time spent on page

**Why**: Identify UX friction points, measure content quality

#### Performance
- **page_load**: Page load time, render time
- **firestore_read**: Database query performance
- **performance_metric**: Various performance measurements

**Why**: Ensure fast, reliable experience

#### Errors
- **error**: JavaScript errors, API failures, broken features

**Why**: Proactively fix bugs, improve stability

### What We DON'T Track

We explicitly do NOT track:

- **Personal Identifiable Information (PII)**: No names, addresses, phone numbers
- **Sensitive Data**: No health info, religious views, political opinions
- **Content of Contributions**: We track that you submitted a theory, not what it says
- **Private Messages**: No tracking of communications
- **Precise Location**: Only country/region, not GPS coordinates
- **Individual Browsing History**: Data is anonymized and aggregated
- **Cross-Site Tracking**: We don't track you on other websites

## Cookies and Similar Technologies

### What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device that help websites remember your preferences and track usage.

### Types of Cookies We Use

#### Essential Cookies
Required for the site to function:
- **Session cookie**: Keeps you logged in
- **CSRF token**: Security protection
- **Authentication state**: Remembers you're signed in

**Cannot be disabled**: These are necessary for core functionality

#### Functional Cookies
Enhance your experience:
- **Theme preference**: Remember dark/light mode choice
- **Language**: Remember language selection
- **Display options**: Remember grid/list view preference

**Can be disabled**: Site will work but preferences won't be saved

#### Analytics Cookies
Help us improve the site:
- **_ga**: Google Analytics user identifier (2 years)
- **_gid**: Google Analytics session identifier (24 hours)
- **analytics_consent**: Your consent preference (permanent)

**Can be disabled**: See "Your Privacy Rights" below

#### No Advertising Cookies
We do NOT use:
- Third-party advertising cookies
- Tracking cookies for ad networks
- Social media tracking pixels (except if you share using social buttons)

### Local Storage

We use browser local storage for:
- **User preferences**: Theme, language, display options
- **Analytics consent**: Your opt-in/opt-out choice
- **Cache**: Temporarily store data for faster loading

### Managing Cookies

**Browser Settings**: All browsers allow you to:
- View cookies
- Delete cookies
- Block cookies
- Get notified when cookies are set

**Our Cookie Controls**: (Footer → Privacy Settings)
- Disable analytics cookies
- Clear all stored data
- Export your preferences

## Data Security

### How We Protect Your Data

#### Encryption
- **In Transit**: HTTPS/TLS encryption for all connections
- **At Rest**: Database encryption for stored data
- **Backups**: Encrypted backup storage

#### Access Controls
- **Authentication**: Secure Firebase Authentication
- **Authorization**: Role-based access control
- **Admin Access**: Limited to authorized personnel only
- **Audit Logs**: Track access to sensitive data

#### Security Practices
- **Regular updates**: Keep software and dependencies up to date
- **Security scans**: Automated vulnerability scanning
- **Penetration testing**: Periodic security audits
- **Incident response**: Plan for handling security breaches

#### Firebase Security Rules
Strict database rules prevent unauthorized access:
```javascript
// Users can only read/write their own data
match /users/{userId} {
  allow read, write: if request.auth.uid == userId;
}

// Anyone can read published content
match /deities/{deityId} {
  allow read: if true;
  allow write: if request.auth != null;  // Must be signed in
}
```

### Limitations

**No system is 100% secure**. While we use industry-standard protections:
- Internet transmission can be intercepted
- Unauthorized access can occur despite safeguards
- You are responsible for keeping your password secure

**Your responsibilities**:
- Use a strong, unique password
- Don't share your account credentials
- Sign out on shared devices
- Report suspicious activity

### Data Breach Notification

In the unlikely event of a data breach:
1. We will investigate immediately
2. Notify affected users within 72 hours (GDPR requirement)
3. Provide details of what data was affected
4. Explain steps we're taking to prevent recurrence
5. Offer resources to protect yourself (e.g., password reset)

## Your Privacy Rights

### Rights Under GDPR (European Users)

If you're in the EU/EEA, you have these rights:

#### Right to Access
- Request a copy of all data we have about you
- Receive data in machine-readable format (JSON/CSV)
- **How**: Email privacy@eyesofazrael.com
- **Response time**: Within 30 days

#### Right to Rectification
- Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
- Update your profile information
- **How**: Edit your profile or contact us

#### Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten")
- Request deletion of your data
- We will delete unless legally required to retain
- **How**: Account settings → Delete Account, or email us
- **Response time**: Completed within 30 days

#### Right to Restrict Processing
- Limit how we use your data
- **Example**: Stop analytics tracking while keeping account
- **How**: Contact privacy@eyesofazrael.com

#### Right to Data Portability
- Export your data to another service
- **Format**: JSON or CSV
- **Includes**: Profile, contributions, preferences
- **How**: Account settings → Export Data

#### Right to Object
- Object to specific data processing
- **Example**: Object to analytics tracking
- **How**: Privacy settings or contact us

#### Right to Withdraw Consent
- Revoke consent for data processing
- **Effect**: We will stop that processing going forward
- **How**: Privacy settings or contact us

### Rights Under CCPA (California Users)

If you're a California resident:

#### Right to Know
- What personal information we collect
- Categories of sources
- Business purposes for collection
- Categories of third parties we share with
- **How**: Review this Privacy Policy or contact us

#### Right to Delete
- Request deletion of your personal information
- **Exceptions**: Legal obligations, security, fraud prevention
- **How**: Same as GDPR erasure right above

#### Right to Opt-Out of Sale
- **Good news**: We don't sell your data!
- No action needed

#### Right to Non-Discrimination
- We will not discriminate for exercising your rights
- Same service quality regardless of privacy choices

### Exercising Your Rights

**To exercise any privacy right**:

1. **Email**: privacy@eyesofazrael.com
2. **Subject**: "Privacy Right Request - [Type]"
3. **Include**:
   - Your email address associated with your account
   - Specific request (access, deletion, etc.)
   - Verification information (to confirm your identity)

**We will**:
- Respond within 30 days (GDPR) or 45 days (CCPA)
- Verify your identity before processing
- Provide requested information or complete action
- Explain if we cannot fulfill request (with legal basis)

**No fees**: Exercising your rights is free

### Analytics Opt-Out

**Multiple ways to opt out of analytics**:

#### Option 1: Site Settings (Easiest)
1. Footer → Privacy Settings
2. Toggle "Analytics Tracking" to OFF
3. Preference saved in browser

#### Option 2: Browser Extension
- Install [Google Analytics Opt-out Add-on](https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout)
- Blocks GA across all sites

#### Option 3: Do Not Track
- Enable "Do Not Track" in browser settings
- We honor this preference

#### Option 4: Browser Settings
- Block third-party cookies in browser settings
- Disable JavaScript (will break site functionality)

#### Option 5: Ad Blocker
- Most ad blockers prevent analytics scripts
- uBlock Origin, Privacy Badger, etc.

## Definitions

**Personal Information**: Information that can identify you (email, name, etc.)

**Anonymized Data**: Data that cannot be linked back to an individual

**Aggregated Data**: Combined data from multiple users (e.g., "1000 users viewed X")

**Cookies**: Small text files stored on your device

**Processing**: Any operation on data (collection, storage, use, disclosure, deletion)

**Data Controller**: Entity that determines purposes of data processing (Eyes of Azrael)

**Data Processor**: Entity that processes data on behalf of controller (Google, Firebase)
